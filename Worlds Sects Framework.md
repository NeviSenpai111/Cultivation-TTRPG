## 1. The Scale Ladder

Cultivation worlds nest. Every named structure in this document sits somewhere on this ladder, and every campaign operates within a defined band of it.

| Scale | Typical Unit | PC Realm That "Owns" This Scale | Example |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Village** | A few hundred mortals | Qi Condensation Early | Wang Lin's Heng Yue Village |
| **Town / Region** | 10K–1M mortals, one minor sect | Qi Condensation Peak → FE | A Rank 2–3 sect's territory |
| **Province** | Several regions, a Rank 3–4 sect | Foundation Establishment → CF | A typical country sub-unit |
| **Country** | A political structure (Rank 1–5) | Core Formation → Nascent Soul | See §2 |
| **Planet** | A world (Rank 1–9) | Nascent Soul → Ascendant | See §3 |
| **Star System** | Many planets under one governance | Ascendant → Nirvana | See §4 |
| **Continent / Realm** | Cosmological horizon | Nirvana Shatterer+ | Immortal Astral Continent, §8 |

**Design principle:** a party "graduates" to a new scale roughly each time they advance a full realm. The village drama that was terrifying at QC is a backwater footnote by CF; the sect politics that dominated CF play are minor when the party reaches Nascent Soul and begins dealing with cultivation-country affairs. **The GM scales the world as the PCs scale.**

## 2. Countries (Rank 1–5)

Countries are *mortal political structures* with a cultivator as the ultimate protector/ruler. They exist where sects don't dominate — more common in Alliance-governed systems than Cloud Sea-style ones.

### 2.1 Country Rank Table

| Rank | Peak Cultivator Realm | Mortal Population | Scope |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Mortal** | None | Up to a few million | Cultivators pass through, but the state is politically mortal |
| **1** | Foundation Establishment | ~1–10 million | Cultivator-ruled duchy or minor kingdom |
| **2** | Core Formation | 10–100 million | Substantial kingdom |
| **3** | Nascent Soul | 100M–1B | Continental empire |
| **4** | Soul Formation | Billions | Planetary power |
| **5+** | Soul Transformation or above | Planetary / multi-planetary | Cultivation country; often overlaps with Planet ranking |

### 2.2 Country vs. Sect

Countries and sects **are not the same thing and often share territory.** A sect may operate within, across, or above a country. Countries provide tribute (mortal resources, taxes, spirit-stone extraction, conscripted disciples); sects provide protection and occasional intervention.

- **Sect-dominant setting (Cloud Sea):** countries are an afterthought. Most cultivators don't care what mortal kingdom they're in. The sect's word is the law that matters.
- **Alliance-dominant setting (Alliance Star System):** countries are first-class political actors. Rank 4+ countries have Alliance seats, tribute obligations, and sanctioned legal combat.
- **Clan-dominant setting (Allheaven):** countries are family holdings. "Country" and "Great Clan" blur together.

### 2.3 Tribute Relationships

High-rank countries typically owe tribute *upward* — to sects, to star-system alliances, or to higher-rank countries. This is the canonical Xian Ni plot engine for inter-country conflict.

Tribute forms:

- **Spirit stones** — the standard unit, demanded in fixed annual quantities
- **Pills** — an Alchemy-Hall-heavy country may tribute rare pills instead of stones
- **Talented disciples** — the sect harvests the country's best cultivation prospects
- **Mortal resources** — rare materials, beast hides, herbs that grow only locally
- **Military service** — the country's cultivators fight in the superior power's wars
- **Access** — the country grants the sect exclusive passage through its territory

**Failure to tribute** triggers escalating responses: warning, public disciple killed, protector cultivator challenged, country annexed.

### 2.4 Country Feel Notes

- A Rank 1 country is small enough that **one PC at Core Formation can disrupt it by themselves.**
- A Rank 2–3 country is the typical "home kingdom" for an early-campaign party; big enough for political arcs, small enough that the party can be known across it.
- Rank 4+ countries start to feel like *sects in disguise* — huge, layered, with internal politics that mirror sect hierarchy.

## 3. Cultivation Planets (Rank 1–9)

The most important spatial unit in the framework. A cultivation planet is a whole world with ranked cultivation infrastructure — sects, spirit veins, countries, Celestial Doors.

### 3.1 Planet Rank Table

| Rank | Description | Typical Peak Sect | Typical Spirit Energy | Population Feel |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **1** | Backwater. No serious sect. | Rank 1 family-group | Thin | Scattered cultivators, mostly mortal planet |
| **2** | Frontier. One or two sects scraping by. | Rank 2 | Thin | Cultivation is regional, mostly unknown to mortals |
| **3** | Minor cultivation world. | Rank 3 | Standard (low) | Cultivators are rare but known — city-level rumors |
| **4** | Standard cultivation planet. | Rank 4–5 | Standard | Cultivators are a known class in major cities |
| **5** | Mid-tier planet. | Rank 5–6 | Standard (rich pockets) | Sects + cultivation countries coexist |
| **6** | Major planet. Hosts a Rank 7 sect. | Rank 7 | Rich | Cultivation world proper — sect politics dominate |
| **7** | **Peak surface-dwelling world.** | Rank 7–8 (dominant) | Very rich | The common peak for mortal-inhabited planets; has Ascendants |
| **8** | **Rare. Sect-owned home planet.** | Rank 8 (single) | Dense | A handful per star system; Nirvana cultivators present |
| **9** | **Unique. One per star system.** | Rank 9 (single) | Saturated / origin-class | Hidden, guarded, mysterious; "the" planet of the star system |

### 3.2 What Planet Rank Means Mechanically

- **Spirit Energy density** — affects cultivation speed. A PC on a Rank 3 planet cultivates at 1×; the same PC on a Rank 7 planet cultivates at roughly 1.5×; on a Rank 9 planet, 2×+. (GM adjudicates per scene — this becomes meaningful during seclusion between sessions.)
- **Threshold of conflict** — a Foundation Establishment PC on Rank 3 is a significant figure. The same PC on Rank 7 is a street-level nobody. **Scale your scenes to the planet, not the PC.**
- **Treasure availability** — Spirit-tier treasures are market-standard on Rank 4+ planets. Celestial-tier treasures are traded openly only on Rank 6+. Void-tier treasures are near-unfindable outside Rank 7+.
- **Resources** — spirit veins, rare herbs, origin-energy pockets, and tribulation weather all correlate with planet rank.
- **Tribulation geometry** — at Rank 7+ planets, sect-owned tribulation-diversion formations exist; lower-rank planets have none and a tribulation can cascade into a city-scale disaster.

### 3.3 Sect-Planet Ownership

At high enough ranks, **sect and planet fuse.** This is pure Xian Ni.

| Sect Rank | Relationship to Cultivation Planets |
|---|---|
| **Rank 1–6** | Operate within territory granted or tolerated by greater powers. Own grounds, not planets. |
| **Rank 7** | Dominant power *on* a planet (typically Rank 5–6) but does not formally *own* it — shares the surface with countries and lesser sects. |
| **Rank 8** | **Claims a cultivation planet as home territory** (typically Rank 6–7). Sect name and planet name blur — e.g. "Evil Earth Sect" rules the planet "Evil Earth." Has branches on other planets. |
| **Rank 9** | **Resides on its own Rank 8+ cultivation planet as sovereign territory.** Canonically rare — all four Rank 9 sects of Cloud Sea each reside on their own planet. |

**What planet-ownership means:**

- **Absolute authority** within the planet's atmosphere — sect law *is* the law
- **Control of spirit veins** — cultivation resources flow to the sect's disciples first
- **Formation sovereignty** — Void+ planetary defensive formations powered by ancestral Elders
- **Immigration control** — outsiders may be barred, taxed, monitored, or disciple-tested at the atmosphere
- **Tribute from lesser worlds** — nearby lower-rank planets owe tribute to the sect's planet

**Campaign implication:** *"Our sect is Rank 6. One day, we claim a planet of our own."* — a 50+ session long-arc that naturally maps the party's progression from CF/NS realms to Ascendant/Nirvana realms. Claiming a planet requires:

1. Reach Rank 8 as a sect (peak cultivator must be Nirvana Scryer+)
2. Either **find an unclaimed planet** (expedition arc) or **seize one** (war arc, usually against an existing Rank 7–8 sect)
3. **Establish planetary defensive formations** (crafting arc, months–years of Seclusion Slots)
4. **Survive the inevitable challenges** from other Rank 8+ sects testing the new neighbor

**Canonical examples:** God Sect's Rank 9 home planet (Cloud Sea); the Alliance Star System's four Rank 8 cultivation planets (Country of Rising Cloud, Country of Reed Flute, Temple of Heaven, Evil Earth Sect); the single hidden Rank 9 planet per star system.

## 4. Star Systems (Scope & Governance)

A star system is the **cosmologically bounded region** containing many planets under a shared governance model. For most campaigns, this is the maximum scope of play.

### 4.1 What a Star System Contains

A typical star system has:

- **3–5 Rank 9 sects** (or equivalent great powers) — setting-defining
- **6–15 Rank 8 cultivation planets** — including those owned by Rank 9 sects
- **Dozens of Rank 6–7 cultivation planets**
- **Hundreds of Rank 3–5 planets** with mixed infrastructure
- **Mortal planets too many to count**
- **Several Celestial Doors** — portals to the setting's Celestial Realms
- **One or more space-fracture zones, battlegrounds, or hunting grounds** — lawless or dangerous regions with unique loot

### 4.2 Canonical Xian Ni Star Systems

Useful as flavor or direct borrow.

| Star System | Governance | Defining Feature | Notable Powers |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Cloud Sea** | Sect-dominant | Origin-crystal resources; largest system | Four Rank 9 sects — God / Ghost / Demon / Heaven-Breaking |
| **Alliance** (formerly Brilliant Void) | Alliance-governed | Sanctioned slaughter, strict rules | Two sects, four temples, eight realms; one Rank 9 country |
| **Allheaven** | Clan-dominant | Bloodline cultivation, marriage politics | Several great families with sealed ancestors |
| **(Fourth, less documented)** | Thunder-adjacent | Chronic celestial-realm contact | Often invaded / invading across the Thunder Celestial Realm |

### 4.3 Travel Between Star Systems

Interstellar travel is **reserved for cultivators at Soul Formation and above.** Between continents of a star system: Ascendant and above. Below those thresholds, cultivators are effectively bound to their planet or region — space itself is the cage.

- **Void-tier treasure transport** — a Void-tier flying sword or vessel can carry one Nascent Soul across a star system; a Nirvana-tier vessel can carry a Nascent Soul party.
- **Spatial tunneling formations** — built by Nirvana cultivators; permanent but fuel-hungry
- **Celestial Doors** — see §6; one-way or two-way depending on construction

Governance can restrict travel. An Alliance-governed star system may bar outsiders from Rank 7+ planets without Alliance papers.

## 5. Spirit Veins & Geomancy

Spirit veins (地脉 / dìmài) are **subsurface channels of concentrated spiritual energy** — the geological circulatory system of cultivation worlds. They are the single most contested resource in any setting.

### 5.1 Spirit Vein Tiers

| Tier | Scale | Effect on Cultivators in Proximity |
|---|---|---|
| **Faint** | Village / courtyard | +10% cultivation speed |
| **Moderate** | Town / estate | +25% cultivation speed |
| **Strong** | City / mountain | +50% cultivation speed; supports QC → FE breakthroughs |
| **Grand** | Region | +100% cultivation speed; supports CF → NS breakthroughs |
| **Primal** | Continent / planet | +200%+ speed; supports NS → SoF+ breakthroughs; worth a war |
| **Origin** | Planet-defining | Effectively infinite; supports any breakthrough; only on Rank 8+ planets |

### 5.2 Strategic Importance

- **Sects are placed on spirit veins.** A sect's founding site is almost always a Strong+ vein. Move the sect and you kill it.
- **Formations consume veins.** Large-scale formations (protective arrays, cultivation caves, tribulation diverters) are powered by the vein beneath them. A sect's Void-tier protective formation requires a Grand+ vein.
- **Veins can be severed.** A killing-blow strike to a vein's heart (usually inside a mountain or underground chamber) permanently destroys it. Rare — only Ascendant+ cultivators can do it, and only a Nirvana Shatterer can sever a Primal+ vein.
- **Veins can be grown.** Pouring spirit stones, dragon-blood, or refined origin-energy into a vein over centuries can elevate it one tier. This is the peaceful alternative to conquest.

### 5.3 Geomantic Features to Seed

When designing a location, pick 1–2:

- **Spirit spring** — a natural wellspring of pure spirit qi; bathing or drinking accelerates cultivation for a session
- **Origin stone** — a crystal node; can be mined for spirit stones until exhausted
- **Dao-concept hotspot** — a place where a specific Dao concept manifests naturally (a volcano for Fire Dao, a mausoleum for Death Dao); cultivation here earns +1 MP for that Dao per session
- **Sealed entity** — an ancient demon, cursed soul, or slumbering beast bound by the vein; breaking the seal releases it
- **Tribulation basin** — a region where heavenly lightning falls more often; dangerous to inhabit but rich in tribulation-refined materials
- **Beast herd grounds** — spirit beasts congregate here for the qi; hunting grounds that also double as danger zones

## 6. Celestial Realms & Celestial Doors

Above the mortal-accessible worlds lie **Celestial Realms** — fragments of the ancient Celestial Domain, linked to mortal planets through **Celestial Doors** (major permanent portals).

### 6.1 Canonical Celestial Realms

- **Thunder Celestial Realm** — lightning-saturated; home of the Thunder Celestial Temple (which operates Celestial-Title tournaments)
- **Rain Celestial Realm** — water/storm flavor
- **Wind Celestial Realm** — air/freedom flavor
- **Lightning Celestial Realm** — related to but distinct from Thunder; separate canonical appearance

### 6.2 Celestial Spiritual Energy

The spiritual energy of Celestial Realms is **denser, purer, and hostile to weak cultivators.**

| Visitor's Realm | Effect |
|---|---|
| **Below Soul Formation** | Burns them. Hours to days of exposure causes death. Protective treasures can mitigate but not fully prevent. |
| **Soul Formation** | Tolerable; cultivation accelerated 3×–5×. |
| **Soul Transformation+** | Fully thrives. Cultivation accelerated further. Nirvana tribulations are easier to survive here. |

This is a **hard gate** on who can meaningfully campaign in a Celestial Realm. A Soul Formation party can manage; a Nascent Soul party will spend the whole expedition hiding in sealed caves.

### 6.3 Celestial Doors

Permanent portals from mortal planets to Celestial Realms.

| Door Feature | Notes |
|---|---|
| **Fixed location** | Typically at a Rank 7+ planet's major peak, sacred mountain, or ancient battlefield |
| **Access control** | Almost always sect- or alliance-controlled; passage is rationed, taxed, or competed for |
| **Transit fee** | Spirit stones, pills, or rare materials; may require sponsorship |
| **One-way doors** | Some doors open inward only — once you go, the Realm chooses when to spit you back out |
| **Time dilation** | A month in the Celestial Realm may be a year in the mortal world, or vice versa; GM's call per door |

### 6.4 What's In A Celestial Realm

- **Celestial corpses** — dead gods/beasts whose bodies still radiate Nirvana-tier material
- **Origin-energy hotspots** — places where the Dao is legible in the very air
- **Ancient techniques** — inheritances left by dead Celestials, often sealed in impossible trials
- **Rare Void/Nirvana materials** — the primary reason anyone goes
- **Celestial Title competitions** — Thunder Celestial Temple's canonical 1,000-year tournament
- **Native powers** — Celestial Realms have their own factions, some of which predate the mortal star system

### 6.5 Celestial Titles

Canonical: earning a Celestial Title is a massive Face event.

- **Tournament scope** — all comers of qualifying realm across the connected mortal worlds
- **Title ranks** — typically bronze / silver / gold, with exponentially rarer slots
- **Face reward** — Title of Cold-Cloud Sword Sovereign type earns **+6 to +10 Face**, permanent, with the Title becoming a formal address
- **Duration** — some Titles are 1,000-year (must defend); some are permanent once earned
- **Mechanical perks** — Title holders often receive Void-tier material grants from their sponsoring Celestial Temple

## 7. Cave Worlds

A **Cave World** (洞天 / dòngtiān) is a **cultivator-created pocket dimension** — a world-within-a-world, shaped by its creator's will. Canonically, the Seven-Colored Celestial Sovereign's Cave World contained entire civilizations who did not know they lived inside a pocket dimension.

### 7.1 Scale Ranges

| Scale | Creator Realm | Contents |
|---|---|---|
| **Training space** | Soul Formation | Cave-sized; a few rooms; cultivation-only |
| **Estate** | Soul Transformation | Manor-sized; a courtyard, fields, a sealed library |
| **Small world** | Ascendant | Hundreds of square kilometers; a full landscape, rivers, spirit beasts |
| **Inhabited kingdom** | Nirvana Scryer | Millions of inhabitants; canonical but rare |
| **Complete cosmos** | Nirvana Shatterer+ | A functioning sub-universe; the Immortal Astral Continent contains several |

### 7.2 Cave World Mechanics

- **Creation:** requires Soul Formation+ and months to years of seclusion. The creator shapes the dimension from refined spatial essence — a massive one-time investment.
- **Access:** the creator holds a **Spatial Key**. Keys can be inherited, stolen (the creator's death unbinds them), or given.
- **Inhabitants:** if populated, inhabitants are typically **sealed in** — they cannot leave without the creator's permission. They may rebel, kill the master, but canonically cannot escape.
- **Time dilation:** Cave Worlds often run faster than the "real world." A month inside can be a day outside. This makes them **perfect extended-seclusion spaces** for high-realm cultivators.
- **Spiritual energy:** usually matches the creator's peak realm's density, making them superior cultivation environments for anyone below that threshold.
- **Vulnerability:** the Cave World collapses if its creator dies and the key is not inherited quickly. Inhabitants perish with it.

### 7.3 Campaign Use

Cave Worlds are fantastic dungeon and arc locations. A whole mid-campaign arc can take place inside one, with the "real world" frozen in time outside. Classic uses:

- **The inheritance dungeon** — the party finds a Spatial Key; the Cave World within contains the last legacy of an extinct sect
- **The prison** — an antagonist is sealed in their own Cave World; the party must enter to finish them
- **The refuge** — the party's patron shelters them in their Cave World during a sect-war arc; inside, they train and grow out of proportion to apparent time
- **The hostile habitat** — the creator is dead but the Cave World persists; its inhabitants are ancient, mad, or both

## 8. The Setting Horizon — Immortal Astral Continent

The **Immortal Astral Continent** is the ultimate canonical horizon — the cosmic "continent" beyond any star system.

- **Home of the Ancient Clan** (rigid monarchy) and **Celestial Clan** (loosely federated) — entire civilizations at Nirvana+ realms
- **At its center**: a vast sea that periodically opens to the **Immemorial God Realm**, a cosmic event usually happening every few centuries
- **Realm requirement**: Nirvana Shatterer minimum to travel there meaningfully
- **Feel**: setting-ending arc territory. A campaign that reaches here is approaching its climax.

**GM note:** you probably won't use this as an active play area. Its value is in giving the world a **horizon** — characters *know* it exists, hear legends from it, and may receive messengers bearing edicts no mortal-realm cultivator can refuse. The Immortal Astral Continent is the answer to "what's bigger than our Nirvana Shatterer ancestors?"

**Above even this** lies the **Immemorial God Realm** — truly beyond this framework's scope. For a campaign capped at Nirvana Shatterer, this is setting myth, not setting fact.

## 9. Inter-World Travel Thresholds

Summary of the hard gates on travel, which the GM should respect when placing plot elements.

| Travel Scope | Minimum Realm | Typical Means |
|---|---|---|
| **Within a country** | Qi Condensation | Walking, flying swords (QC Peak+) |
| **Between countries on same planet** | Foundation Establishment | Flying sword (Mysterious+ for long haul) |
| **Between continents of a planet** | Nascent Soul | Celestial-tier flying sword or ship |
| **To a neighboring planet (same star system)** | Soul Formation | Void-tier flying vessel, or sect-owned spatial tunnel |
| **Across a star system** | Soul Transformation | Void-tier flagship, rare stellar storms, Celestial Doors |
| **Between star systems** | Ascendant | Nirvana-tier vessel, permanent spatial tunnels, specific Celestial Realm passages |
| **To Immortal Astral Continent** | Nirvana Shatterer | One-way invitation events, Immemorial God Realm opening |

**These aren't rules — they're guardrails.** A plot device can always move a PC up a tier. But the GM should be honest with the table about when this is happening ("your master opens a one-way spatial tunnel for you; you can get *to* the Thunder Celestial Realm but not back on your own").

# PART B — SECT SYSTEM REFERENCE

## 10. What Is a Sect

A **sect** (宗门 / zōngmén) is an **organized body of cultivators, ranked 1–9, that transmits techniques, manages cultivation resources, and defends territory.** Sects are the primary social structure of most cultivation worlds.

A sect is distinguished from:

- **A country** (§2) — countries govern mortals, sects govern cultivators
- **A clan** (§13) — clans are bloodline-based; sects admit by talent
- **An alliance** (§13) — alliances federate multiple sects, countries, and temples
- **A guild / loose grouping** — lacking formal rank, transmission, or territory

A sect has, at minimum:

1. **A rank** (1–9) — the power signal to the outside world
2. **A doctrine** — Orthodox / Demonic / Neutral / Heretical (§15)
3. **A specialization** — what the sect is *known for* (§16)
4. **A hierarchy** — Servants → Ancestors (§14)
5. **A territory** — grounds, a mountain, a city, a planet
6. **A transmission** — the signature techniques, artifacts, and manuals it passes down
7. **A spirit vein** — the geomantic foundation (§5)

---

## 11. Anatomy of a Sect

Every sect has these fields. Fill them all when writing one up; missing fields create ambiguity at the table.

| Field | Purpose |
|---|---|
| **Name** | Evocative; 2–4 words. ("Heng Yue Sect", "Blood River Cult", "Starseal Temple") |
| **Rank** | 1–9 (§12). Sets membership, resources, and scope. |
| **Doctrine** | Orthodox / Demonic / Neutral / Heretical (§15). |
| **Specialization** | What the sect is known for — swordsmanship, alchemy, formations, soul arts, bloodline, etc. (§16) |
| **Governance Model** | Sect / Alliance member / Clan (§13). |
| **Territory** | Grounds scale (§1): mountain, city, region, planet. |
| **Peak Cultivator** | Name + realm. This single number determines rank. |
| **Elder Roster** | 3–10 named figures, roughly mapped to the internal hierarchy (§14). |
| **Signature Techniques** | 2–5 named techniques the sect teaches and is identified with. |
| **Signature Treasures** | 1–3 named artifacts (usually Inherited, §Treasure Framework). |
| **Spirit Vein** | Tier of the vein the sect sits on (§5). |
| **Doctrinal Position** | Orthodox, demonic, neutral, or heretical — and *why*. |
| **Faction Seeds** | 2–4 internal factions with their tensions (§18). |
| **External Relationships** | Sworn enemies, tributaries, rivals, allies, debts. |
| **Founding Event** | How the sect came to be (1–2 sentences). |
| **The Secret** | One thing the sect hides — from itself, its rivals, or the world. (§18, §28) |

## 12. Sect Rank System (1–9)

Sects are formally ranked 1–9 by **the realm of their peak cultivator, modified by membership and territory.** This is canonical Xian Ni (Cloud Sea Star System).

| Rank | Description | Peak Realm | Disciple Count | Scope |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **1** | Family cultivation group — barely a sect | Qi Condensation Peak | 5–20 | Single village |
| **2** | Small cultivation school | Foundation Establishment | 20–80 | Town + surrounding |
| **3** | Town-level sect | Core Formation | 80–300 | A city and hinterland |
| **4** | Minor sect | Nascent Soul | 300–800 | A province |
| **5** | Mid-tier sect (often a Rank 8 branch) | Soul Formation | 800–2,000 | A region |
| **6** | Major regional sect | Soul Transformation | 2,000–5,000 | Large region / small planet |
| **7** | Greater sect | Ascendant | 5,000–15,000 | Planet or minor star-cluster |
| **8** | Elite sect — claims a cultivation planet | Nirvana Scryer / Cleanser | 15,000+ | Star-system-level; has branches |
| **9** | Setting-defining sect — owns its own Rank 8+ planet | Nirvana Shatterer | 15,000+ (plus branches) | Star-system-dominant; 3–5 exist per setting |

### 12.1 Rank 9: The Old Monsters

Rank 9 sects are **rare** — canonically, Cloud Sea has only four (God Sect, Ghost Sect, Demon Sect, Heaven Breaking Sect). A campaign setting typically has **3–5 Rank 9 sects total**, and their leaders are the "old monsters" shaping the world.

- **Ancestral seclusion** — a Rank 9 sect's most powerful figures have been in seclusion for centuries and are invoked only in existential crises
- **Cross-system influence** — Rank 9 sects have branches and debts across the star system
- **Setting authority** — a Rank 9 sect's declaration on a matter is effectively law; even other Rank 9 sects weigh whether to challenge it

### 12.2 Rank Advancement

A sect advances rank when:

1. Its **peak cultivator** breaks through to the realm required for the next tier, AND
2. Its **membership and territory** can support the higher rank

A Rank 6 sect whose single Soul Formation elder dies drops to Rank 5 automatically. Conversely, a Rank 6 sect whose founder ascends to Soul Transformation and who has the disciple base to match becomes Rank 7 *upon sect-world recognition*, which can take years or be contested.

**Contested rank** is a recurring plot engine: a rising Rank 7 sect that other Rank 7 sects refuse to recognize, a declining Rank 8 sect that ambitious neighbors smell blood on.

## 13. Governance Models

Every region of the world organizes cultivation power differently. Pick one (or mix them) per region of the setting.

### 13.1 Sect-Dominant (Cloud Sea Model)

Sects are the primary power. Mortal countries exist but are secondary — they pay tribute to the sects whose territory they fall within. Sect politics dominate the cultivation world.

- **Pros:** simple, iconic, well-supported by the Xian Ni framework
- **Setting feel:** Sect A vs. Sect B, young-master rivalries, disciple competitions
- **Campaign fit:** Sect-disciple campaigns, sect-war campaigns
- **Canonical example:** Cloud Sea Star System

### 13.2 Alliance-Governed (Alliance Star System Model)

A central **Cultivation Alliance** rules. Alliance is divided into constituent bodies — typically **two sects, four temples, and eight realms** (or similar). Rules are strict. Rank 4+ countries are Alliance members with tribute obligations. **Slaughter is canonically part of daily life** — sanctioned combat, Death-Duel permissions, and Kill Orders are routine Alliance administration.

- **Pros:** enables political intrigue, rules-lawyering, internal factions
- **Setting feel:** strict hierarchy, competitions, sanctioned combat, daily lethality
- **Campaign fit:** rising-through-ranks campaigns, political cultivators, Alliance-service arcs
- **Canonical example:** Alliance Star System

Structural notes:

| Alliance Body | Function | Typical Count |
|---|---|---|
| **Sect** | Core cultivation arts; front-line power | 2 |
| **Temple** | Specialist institution (alchemy, formation, divination, inheritance) | 4 |
| **Realm** | Regional administrative zone; governs member countries | 8 |
| **Country** | Member tribute-paying polity | Rank 4+ countries, dozens |

### 13.3 Clan-Dominant (Allheaven Model)

Power lives in **bloodline cultivation families.** Clans pass techniques, treasures, and even cultivation methods through heredity. Individual talent matters less than lineage. Politics is family rivalry; alliances are marriages; betrayals are branch schisms.

- **Pros:** rich for inheritance plots, family drama, bloodline mechanics (ties into Heavenly Bodies)
- **Setting feel:** *Dune*-like great houses, marriage politics, bloodline awakenings
- **Campaign fit:** heir-apparent campaigns, family-betrayal arcs, bloodline-awakening stories
- **Canonical example:** Allheaven Star System, Immortal Astral Continent's Ancient Clan

Clan-specific mechanics:

- **Bloodline purity ranks** affect inheritance access
- **Marriage as alliance** is the primary diplomatic tool
- **Branch schisms** — a clan branch breaking away is a war event
- **Sealed ancestors** — cryo-slumbering progenitors who wake only in crisis

### 13.4 Mixing Models

A setting can blend models by region. The eastern continent might be sect-dominant while the western is clan-dominant. A city-state on the Alliance's frontier might officially operate under Alliance law but be secretly run by a sect. **Conflicts between models are a rich source of campaign plot.**

Common blend examples:

- **Sect within Alliance** — the sect formally answers to the Alliance but plays internal politics
- **Clan-run sect** — a "sect" that admits only bloodline members; hybrid of Sect and Clan governance
- **Alliance on top of clan-dominant region** — outer Alliance authority over inner clan politics; constant friction

## 14. Internal Hierarchy

Within any sect (regardless of rank), the internal structure follows a common pattern. **The names vary** — Xian Ni's Heng Yue Sect uses colored robes to mark Inner Disciple rank, for example — but the functional tiers are consistent.

### 14.1 Tier Table

| Tier | Who They Are | Typical Realm | Count in a Typical Rank 6 Sect |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Servants / Staff** | Mortals or failed cultivators who keep the sect running | Mortal / QC | Hundreds to thousands |
| **Honorary Disciples** | Entry-level — may have been bribed/connected in, not full members | QC Early–Mid | Hundreds |
| **Outer Disciples** | Proper disciples, low tier — do chores, receive basic training and pills | QC Late–Peak to FE | ~500 |
| **Inner Disciples** | Ranked by strength; receive significant resources; often sub-ranked by robe color (red → white → black → purple) | FE Late to CF | ~100 |
| **Core Disciples / True Disciples** | The sect's hopes; personally tutored by Elders or Ancestors | CF to Nascent Soul | 10–30 |
| **Elders** | Run the sect; teach, lead expeditions, handle external affairs | Nascent Soul to Soul Formation | 10–30 |
| **Grand Elders / Peak Elders** | Strongest active elders, running specific divisions (Alchemy Hall, Outer Affairs, etc.) | Soul Formation to Soul Transformation | 3–10 |
| **Sect Master** | Public leader; typically Soul Transformation+ | Soul Transformation / Ascendant | 1 |
| **Ancestor(s)** | Reclusive high-realm figures, rarely active, invoked only in crises | Ascendant to Nirvana Shatterer | 0–3 |

### 14.2 Note on "Elder"

In smaller sects (Rank 3–4), cultivators at surprisingly low realms (Peak Qi Condensation, Early Foundation Establishment) may hold the title "Elder" simply because they're the oldest active cultivators. Wang Lin's first master, Sun Dazhu, is an Elder at Qi Condensation 12. In a Rank 7 sect, a Nascent Soul cultivator might be a middling Elder. **Title ≠ realm.** Always list both.

### 14.3 Resources Granted Per Tier

| Tier | Typical Resources |
|---|---|
| **Honorary** | Basic cultivation method, mortal-grade lodging, minor pill allowance |
| **Outer** | Sect-standard techniques, common spirit stones stipend, access to outer library |
| **Inner** | Mysterious-grade techniques, monthly pill supply, access to training formations |
| **Core / True** | Celestial-grade techniques, direct Elder tutelage, private cultivation cave |
| **Elder** | Access to sect's Void-grade treasures; personal disciples; seat on Council |
| **Grand Elder / Sect Master / Ancestor** | Sect-defining resources; commission Nirvana-grade crafting; access to forbidden archives |

### 14.4 Face Modifier

Sect tier grants Face within the sect: Outer = 1, Inner = 2, Core = 4, Elder = 6, Sect Master = 8. This is the sect-Face value referenced in the base framework §9.

**Across sects**, Face transfers partially: a Rank 6 sect's Inner Disciple (Face 2) carries roughly the same weight as a Rank 4 sect's Core Disciple (Face 4) when they meet in a neutral setting. **Rank multiplies Face.** Rough rule: **effective external Face = internal Face × (sect rank / 5)**.

### 14.5 Progression Through the Hierarchy

A typical disciple's career, from entry:

1. **Apply / be inducted** — admission test, usually a bloodline or root test + a combat/cultivation test
2. **Honorary → Outer** — 1–3 years; contingent on a breakthrough to FE or on patron sponsorship
3. **Outer → Inner** — 3–10 years; breakthrough to CF typical; often involves an internal competition
4. **Inner → Core** — requires being noticed by an Elder or winning a sect tournament
5. **Core → Elder** — requires Nascent Soul breakthrough *and* political positioning; the sect must have a seat open
6. **Elder → Grand Elder** — rare, typically requires Soul Formation + decades of service
7. **Grand Elder → Sect Master** — dynastic or election; frequently violent

Most disciples never make it past Inner. Core is already a rare tier; Elder is rarer still. **The sect is a pyramid, and the pyramid is meant to narrow.**

## 15. Sect Doctrines

Every sect has a doctrinal alignment that shapes its methods, its reputation, and its compatibility with techniques and treasures.

### 15.1 The Four Doctrines

| Doctrine | Core Ethos | Methods | Reputation | Tribulation Flavor |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Orthodox** | Cultivate in harmony with heaven; respect life and karma | Slow, safe, with heavy Face norms | Pillars of the cultivation world | Clean; standard tribulation |
| **Demonic** | Cultivate through slaughter, suffering, or consumption; defy karma | Fast, lethal, exploitative | Feared, hunted by Orthodox | Heavier; Black Karma accumulates |
| **Neutral** | Pragmatic; accepts both paths; emphasizes power over alignment | Mixed | Distrusted by both extremes | Standard |
| **Heretical** | Rejects the canonical realm system; pursues forbidden paths | Extreme, often lethal to practitioner | Persecuted by everyone; rarer than Demonic | Unpredictable; often catastrophic |

### 15.2 Orthodox Sects

- **Cultivation speed:** baseline
- **Karma hygiene:** must avoid Black Karma; Blood Debts are tracked, judged, and repaid
- **Face economy:** elevated; breaking oaths or killing in cold blood costs Face
- **Typical specializations:** sword arts, formations, alchemy, soul arts, righteous body refinement
- **Canonical examples:** Heng Yue Sect (early Xian Ni), most Cloud Sea Rank 9 sects *except* the demonic ones
- **Alliance relations:** dominant; Orthodox sects usually form the Alliance's core

### 15.3 Demonic Sects

- **Cultivation speed:** +25%–+50% faster than Orthodox (demonic techniques are efficient)
- **Karma hygiene:** deliberately wicked; accumulate Black Karma, face harsher tribulations
- **Face economy:** measured differently — Demonic Face is earned by lethality and ruthlessness, not diplomacy
- **Typical specializations:** blood cultivation, soul-eating, corpse refinement, poison, puppet sacrifice
- **Canonical examples:** Demon Sect (Cloud Sea), Blood River Cult, Ghost Sect
- **Alliance relations:** frequently at war with Orthodox; some Alliance systems formally recognize certain Demonic sects as "Rank 9 adversaries" rather than outlaws

### 15.4 Neutral Sects

- **Cultivation speed:** baseline +0 to +15%
- **Karma hygiene:** no strict rules; the sect cares about results
- **Typical specializations:** treasure hoarding, mercenary specialists, secret arts
- **Canonical examples:** most mid-tier independent sects; many Clan-governance sects lean Neutral by default
- **Alliance relations:** shifts with political winds

### 15.5 Heretical Sects

- **Cultivation speed:** irregular — some techniques leap realms, others stall
- **Karma hygiene:** deliberately breaks the system; tribulations may be mild or apocalyptic
- **Typical specializations:** forbidden techniques, self-modification, Dao-hijacking, reversal arts (Wang Lin's "reversal of the heavens" instinct fits here)
- **Canonical examples:** the forbidden paths pursued by characters like Wang Lin in late Xian Ni; the Heaven Breaking Sect qualifies partially
- **Alliance relations:** outlaw status, persecuted by Orthodox and often by Demonic too — everyone fears a heretic

### 15.6 Doctrine Compatibility

A cultivator's Method (the canonical cultivation method they run) has a doctrinal tag. Techniques, treasures, and pills of opposing doctrine may be **incompatible, causing Qi Deviation risk** when attempted. See the Techniques Framework §7.3 for the mechanics.

**Rough compatibility matrix:**

| Method \ Technique | Orthodox | Demonic | Neutral | Heretical |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Orthodox** | Full | Severe penalty | Full | Deviation risk |
| **Demonic** | Severe penalty | Full | Full | Full |
| **Neutral** | Full | Full | Full | Penalty |
| **Heretical** | Penalty | Full | Full | Full |

## 16. Sect Specializations

Every sect is known for something. This is the **one-line identity** that fellow cultivators will associate with the sect's name.

### 16.1 The Specialization Menu

Pick 1 primary and optionally 1 secondary. A Rank 8+ sect may have 2 primary.

| Specialization | Identity Hook | Signature Outputs |
|---|---|---|
| **Sword Arts** | "The ten-thousand-sword mountain" | Sword techniques, Sword-Spirit flying swords |
| **Alchemy** | "Every pill in the region came from their furnace" | Rare pills, pill-market dominance, Alchemy Hall |
| **Formations** | "Their grounds are uncrackable" | Defensive and offensive formations, cartographers, Void+ arrays |
| **Body Cultivation** | "Their disciples punch through steel at Foundation Establishment" | Iron-body techniques, Heavenly-Body awakenings |
| **Soul Arts** | "They read your Karma at a glance" | Soul techniques, Divine Sense mastery, divination |
| **Talismans** | "Their paper is Void-grade" | Talisman production, siege-grade talismans |
| **Puppet Craft** | "Their Celestial Guards walk among us" | Puppets, refined cultivator-constructs |
| **Poison / Gu Arts** | "Their venom has no antidote" | Poison pills, Gu-insects, alchemical weapons |
| **Bloodline / Heavenly Body** | "Their heir is born with eyes of fire" | Bloodline techniques, inherited treasures, sealed awakenings |
| **Artifacts / Forging** | "The best spears in the star system" | Treasures, rare-material markets, Forging Hall |
| **Demonic Blood Arts** | "Enter their grounds, leave without blood" | Blood-cultivation, sacrifice rituals, Nirvana-lethal ancestors |
| **Corpse / Ghost Arts** | "The dead serve them" | Ghost-cultivators, sealed corpse armies, Ghost Sect ties |
| **Divination / Karma** | "Their Ancestor named the star system's future" | Prophecy pills, Karma-severance techniques, fated-event knowledge |
| **Elemental Orthodoxy** | "The Frozen-Flame Temple" | Element-pure techniques, environment-shaping arts, one Dao focused |

### 16.2 Specialization Consequences

Specialization shapes:

- **The sect's technique library** — 80% of techniques taught fit the specialization
- **The sect's treasures** — the signature pieces are specialization-themed
- **The sect's Elders** — Elders of the specialization are first-class; specialists from other disciplines are second-class
- **The sect's external trade** — the sect exports its specialization and imports everything else
- **The sect's vulnerabilities** — a sect specialized in Sword Arts may have weak formations and be vulnerable to a formation-based assault

**Design tip:** a sect's specialization is its **identity *and* its weakness.** Make both visible.

## 17. Sect Resources & Economy

How a sect sustains itself.

### 17.1 Revenue Streams

| Source | Who | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Tribute from subject countries** | Sect-dominant | §2.3 |
| **Tribute from branch sects** | Rank 8+ sect masters | §19 |
| **Pill / talisman / treasure sales** | Alchemy/Forging/Talisman sects | Pricier in higher-rank markets |
| **Disciple fees** | All sects | Entry fees, annual dues |
| **Expedition loot** | All sects | Sect sponsors expeditions, takes 30–70% |
| **Mercenary contracts** | Neutral + Demonic | Sect sells elder protection, assassination, siege support |
| **Celestial Door taxes** | Sects controlling a Door (§6) | Major income for Rank 7+ sects |
| **Spirit vein leasing** | Sects with surplus vein capacity | Cultivate-here fees for outsiders |

### 17.2 Core Expenditures

| Expense | Who |
|---|---|
| **Elder stipends** | All Elders receive pill/spirit-stone allowances |
| **Formation fuel** | Planetary defensive formations consume spirit stones by the ton |
| **Disciple training** | Pills, technique scrolls, treasure loans |
| **Bribes / diplomacy** | Gifts to Alliance, neighboring sects, Celestial Temples |
| **Branch subsidies** | Rank 8 sects fund their Rank 5 branches |
| **Tribulation insurance** | Reserves for Ancestor breakthrough events |

### 17.3 The Spirit Stone Standard

| Grade | Relative Value | Use |
|---|---|---|
| **Low-grade spirit stone** | 1 | Base unit; used for formations, QC cultivation |
| **Mid-grade** | 100 | FE–CF cultivation; rare-pill purchase |
| **High-grade** | 10,000 | NS+ cultivation; Void-tier material purchase |
| **Top-grade** | 1,000,000 | Nirvana cultivation; planet-scale formation fuel |

(Full treasure economy in the Treasures Framework §14.)

### 17.4 Sect-to-Sect Economic Relationships

- **Tribute relationship** — one sect formally acknowledges another's superiority; pays annual tribute
- **Trade relationship** — mutual exchange of specialization outputs (alchemy sect ⇄ sword sect)
- **Debt relationship** — one sect owes the other (a loan of treasures, a favor called in); the ledger shapes politics
- **Marriage relationship** — Clan-governance variant; inheritance transfers entangle the sects
- **War relationship** — hostilities ranging from skirmishes to sect-extinction campaigns

## 18. Sect Politics

A sect that's not politically complicated is dead drama. Even a Rank 3 sect should have at least **two factions in tension.**

### 18.1 Internal Faction Types

| Faction | Typical Agenda | Conflict Generator |
|---|---|---|
| **The Orthodox Hardliners** | Strict doctrine, formal Face norms | Clash with Pragmatists |
| **The Pragmatists** | Results over rules; willing to bend doctrine | Clash with Hardliners |
| **The Rising Young Masters** | New Core Disciples pushing for recognition | Clash with Conservative Elders |
| **The Conservative Elders** | Defend the old order, their own seats | Clash with Young Masters |
| **The Ancestor's Circle** | Disciples of the sect's reclusive Ancestor(s) | Above-politics; enter when invoked |
| **The Branch Rivalry** | Another branch of this Rank 8 sect (§19) | External-internal conflict |
| **The Exile Faction** | Disciples who were almost expelled; politically fragile, dangerous | Either reform or rebel |
| **The Doctrine Splitters** | Demonic cell inside an Orthodox sect (or vice versa) | Secret, potentially apocalyptic |

**Design rule:** pick **2–4 factions** when designing a sect. Give each a goal and a grievance. When the PCs interact with the sect, at least one faction should be aligned with them and one opposed.

### 18.2 Heart Oaths & Karma

Sects are **Heart-Oath machines.** Upon induction, disciples typically swear at least:

- **A non-betrayal oath** — not to reveal sect secrets
- **A non-harm oath** — not to kill sect members without sanction
- **A tribute / service oath** — to serve the sect for X years after training

These are real mechanical Oaths (see base framework §9). Breaking them generates Karma and often mechanical backlash.

**Sect Master Oaths** bind the sect itself:

- Oaths to allied sects (non-aggression, mutual defense)
- Oaths to the Alliance or governing body
- Oaths to Ancestors ("I will not let the sect fall below Rank X in my reign")

### 18.3 External Relationships

Every sect should have defined:

- **1–2 sworn enemies** — sects that wronged it in the past, with an ongoing Blood Debt clock
- **1 rival** — a same-rank peer competing for resources/territory/Face
- **1 patron or ally** — often a Rank 8 sect for a Rank 5 branch, or a neighboring sect of similar rank
- **1 debt** — a favor owed to another sect, callable at dramatic moments
- **1 tributary** — a weaker sect that owes it

This gives the GM **five threads** to pull at any time.

### 18.4 Signature Rivalry Plots

- **The Tournament Rivalry** — scheduled competitive event every N years (Branch Competition, Celestial Title tournament)
- **The Treasure Rivalry** — two sects claim the same inherited artifact, alternately stealing it
- **The Vein Rivalry** — spirit vein lies between two sect territories; who drains it is a 100-year cold war
- **The Heir Rivalry** — sects exchanged disciples for training, each was secretly poached; now their "sons" are enemies
- **The Doctrinal Rivalry** — Orthodox vs. Demonic, genocide-long
- **The Old Grudge** — an Ancestor wronged another Ancestor 3,000 years ago; neither has forgotten

## 19. Branch Sects & the Branch Competition

Canonical Xian Ni mechanic, worth preserving in any Sect-dominant setting.

### 19.1 Branch Structure

- **A Rank 8 sect sponsors Rank 5 branch sects** — typically 6–12 of them across its sphere of influence
- **Each branch has its own territory, Sect Master, and internal hierarchy** but owes tribute, Face, and loyalty to the parent Rank 8 sect
- **Branch disciples who excel may be promoted to the main sect** — canonically, this is the primary way a local cultivator rises to star-system-level relevance

### 19.2 The Branch Competition

- **Held every 1,000 years** (per Cloud Sea canon; GM may adjust cycle length for campaign pacing — every 100 years works for tighter settings)
- **All sponsored branches send their top disciples** (usually 10 per branch) to compete
- **Last-place-two-cycles-in-a-row branches are disbanded** and their survivors absorbed into other branches
- **First-place branch receives massive rewards** — Void-tier treasures, Rank elevation, a seat at the main sect, and their Sect Master often graduates to an Elder role at the parent sect

### 19.3 Campaign Use

A generational arc can climax with the Branch Competition. Typical shape:

1. **Arc 1 (Outer Disciple era, QC–FE):** the PCs learn of the Branch Competition in the distance
2. **Arc 2 (Inner Disciple era, FE–CF):** the PCs are selected as participants; the sect prepares them
3. **Arc 3 (Core Disciple era, CF–NS):** the Competition itself — a multi-session tournament arc
4. **Arc 4 (post-Competition):** aftermath — the winning branch elevates, losing branches bleed, PCs are rewarded or exiled

**The Branch Competition is also a great recurring tournament structure** for a sect-disciple campaign frame.

## 20. Sect Lifecycle

Every sect has a life arc. Knowing where a sect is in that arc shapes how the GM plays it.

### 20.1 The Five Stages

| Stage | Description | Typical Age | Faction Dynamics |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Founding** | A single founder gathers disciples; informal hierarchy | 0–50 years | Founder is everyone's patron; few factions |
| **Rising** | Formal hierarchy solidifies; rank climbs; external recognition | 50–500 years | Young-master vs. conservative-elder tension forms |
| **Peak** | Rank stable, often for centuries; maximum Face and resources | 500–2000 years | Multi-faction rivalry; sclerosis begins |
| **Declining** | Ancestor dies or secludes without return; rank stalls or drops | 2000+ years | Factions fight for scraps; branches secede |
| **Dissolution** | Disciples disperse; territory lost; name survives as a legacy | varies | No factions — the sect is a memory |

### 20.2 Stage-Based Plot Hooks

- **Founding sects** — PCs are original disciples; everything is possible, nothing is established
- **Rising sects** — PCs ride the wave; rank-up arcs; competitive recognition
- **Peak sects** — PCs are in the classic sect-politics mode; factions abound
- **Declining sects** — PCs are holding the line; treasures being lost; Ancestor-awakening arcs
- **Dissolving sects** — PCs are the last disciples; carry-the-flame arc; refound-the-sect arc at Nascent Soul+

### 20.3 The Sect Death Events

A sect dies when one of these happens:

- **The Peak Cultivator dies without successor** — if the last Nascent-Soul+ Elder falls and no disciple reaches that realm, the sect plummets in rank and is preyed upon
- **Territory is lost** — the spirit vein is severed or captured; the sect is rootless
- **Branch secession** — a major branch declares independence and takes resources
- **Tribulation reprisal** — the sect, demonic or heretical, draws down a collective tribulation that kills the leadership
- **Sworn-enemy final war** — a lingering rivalry culminates in genocidal war; one sect wins and ends the other

**Dying sects are plot gold.** The PCs can be the sect's last hope, or they can be the scavengers picking the corpse.

# PART C — DESIGN FRAMEWORK

## 21. Design Principles

These hold for both world-building and sect-building.

1. **Rank signals scale, not quality.** A Rank 3 sect is not a "bad" sect — it's a sect that fits a particular scale of play. A well-designed Rank 3 sect is as playable as a Rank 9 one.
2. **Canon first, generalize when needed.** If Xian Ni has a named structure (Star System, Celestial Realm, Branch Competition), preserve it.
3. **Every sect has two factions minimum.** Monolithic sects are dead drama.
4. **Every planet has one resource worth fighting over.** Otherwise it's wallpaper.
5. **Every star system has a horizon.** The Celestial Realms, the Immortal Astral Continent, or an equivalent — something the PCs can never quite reach, but always sense.
6. **A secret per sect.** Every sect hides one thing: a forbidden archive, a dead Ancestor, a demonic cell, a bloodline shame. The secret is the GM's scalpel.
7. **Relationships over lists.** Five well-defined inter-sect relationships beat fifty undefined sects.
8. **Budget where it matters.** For sects — the SP budget calibrates internal resources. For worlds — the feature menu defines what makes this planet *this* planet.

## 22. Building a Star System — 7-Step Process

**Use for:** the top-level setting. Do this once at campaign start.

### Step 1 — Pick a Governance Model

Sect / Alliance / Clan / Mixed. See §13. This is the single biggest aesthetic decision.

### Step 2 — Name the 3–5 Great Powers

For a Sect-dominant system, these are 3–5 Rank 9 sects. For Alliance, 2 sects + 4 temples + 8 realms (or similar). For Clan, 3–5 Great Clans.

For each: give **name, doctrine, specialization, one-line hook.**

**Example (sect-dominant):**
- *Azure Vault Sect* — Orthodox, sword arts, "the swords hum when an enemy enters the system"
- *Blood Wheel Cult* — Demonic, blood cultivation, "every disciple has killed a named rival"
- *Nine Lanterns Temple* — Orthodox, soul arts, "they divine what will happen, and make it happen"
- *Crimson Tide Clan* — Neutral, poison / beast-taming, "they breed the star system's worst things"

### Step 3 — Place the Rank 8 Planets

Typically 6–15 per star system. Each hosts one Rank 8 sect or is owned by one of the Rank 9 sects as a branch planet. Name 4–6 you care about; the rest are background.

### Step 4 — Seed the Rank 7 Planets

These are the **play-zone planets** — where most of the campaign will happen. Pick 3–5 to name, each with:

- Its Rank 7 dominant sect
- Its defining resource
- Its political quirk (Alliance member, Clan stronghold, open-market, etc.)

### Step 5 — Place the Celestial Doors

1–3 Celestial Doors, each leading to a named Celestial Realm. Decide who controls each Door — usually a Rank 9 sect or a dedicated Celestial Temple. (See §6.)

### Step 6 — Name the Hidden Rank 9 Planet

Every star system canonically has **one hidden Rank 9 planet.** Name it. Give it one defining trait. Nobody goes there — or if they do, they don't come back. This is a late-campaign destination or mystery.

### Step 7 — Seed 3 Setting-Wide Tensions

Even if the PCs never engage them, these shape the background:

- A war about to break (two Rank 9 sects sharpening knives)
- An old Ancestor about to emerge from seclusion (and nobody knows what they'll do)
- A Celestial Realm event approaching (the next 1,000-year Thunder Title Tournament, the Immemorial God Realm opening)

**Output:** one page per star system. This is the setting bible.

---

## 23. Building a Cultivation Planet — 5-Step Process

**Use for:** each planet the PCs will actually play on. Do this when the PCs are about to encounter the planet.

### Step 1 — Pick the Rank (1–9)

Match the PC realms. PCs at Foundation Establishment → Rank 3–4 planet. PCs at Nascent Soul → Rank 5–6. PCs at Soul Formation+ → Rank 7–8.

### Step 2 — Pick the Dominant Sect

The Rank 7 or Rank 8 sect (or equivalent) that owns the planet, if any. Name it, set its doctrine and specialization. For lower-rank planets, instead name the 2–3 Rank 5–6 sects sharing the planet.

### Step 3 — Identify the Spirit Vein

What tier? What geography? Who controls it? Is it contested? This is the planet's **heart** — if the vein is severed, the planet dies as a cultivation world.

### Step 4 — Place 2–4 Key Features

Pick from the Feature Menu (§27). A typical planet has:

- 1 major resource (origin stone deposit, celestial corpse, rare herb field, etc.)
- 1 political quirk (independent sect, heretical cult, Alliance holdout, Clan rivalry)
- 1 danger zone (sealed-entity site, tribulation basin, space-fracture zone)
- 1 mystery (a forbidden archive, a Celestial Door rumor, an ancient tomb)

### Step 5 — Write Three Sentences

One sentence each:

1. **What this planet is known for** (resource, sect, culture)
2. **What the PCs will encounter first** (the local sect, city, or figure they meet)
3. **What will happen if they stay** (the planet's current political drift; the tension that pulls them in)

**Output:** one paragraph per planet. This is enough to play.

## 24. The Sect Power (SP) Budget

Sects are **calibrated by rank.** Each rank gives a pool of Sect Power (SP) to spend on what makes the sect *this* sect — techniques, treasures, elders, formations, signature resources, external relationships.

### 24.1 The SP Table

| Sect Rank | SP Budget | Key Signature Slots |
|---|---|---|
| **Rank 1** | 3 SP | 1 |
| **Rank 2** | 6 SP | 1 |
| **Rank 3** | 10 SP | 2 |
| **Rank 4** | 15 SP | 2 |
| **Rank 5** | 22 SP | 3 |
| **Rank 6** | 32 SP | 3 |
| **Rank 7** | 45 SP | 4 |
| **Rank 8** | 64 SP | 5 |
| **Rank 9** | 90 SP | 6+ |

**Key Signature Slots** are separate from the SP budget. They allow expensive "keyword" features that aren't quantifiable in SP — a named Ancestor, a signature inherited treasure, a famous Heart Oath, a planetary formation. Each slot holds one of these.

**Unspent SP** becomes reserve — interpret as hidden reserves, war-chest spirit stones, or unsubscribed Elder seats. Can be spent in-session to represent the sect deploying reserves.

### 24.2 Balance Sanity Check

When done, the sect should feel:

- **Rank-appropriate in at least 2 of:** peak realm, territory, signature output, Face projection
- **Specialized in 1 primary + 1 optional secondary**
- **Politically complicated** (2+ factions, 3+ external relationships)
- **Secretive about 1 thing**

If it doesn't feel that way, revise.

## 25. Building a Sect — 9-Step Process

**Use for:** any named sect the PCs will engage with more than passingly.

### Step 1 — Pick the Rank (§12)

Sets the SP budget, the peak realm, the disciple count.

### Step 2 — Pick the Doctrine (§15)

Orthodox, Demonic, Neutral, Heretical. Shapes everything downstream.

### Step 3 — Pick the Specialization (§16)

1 primary; optionally 1 secondary at Rank 5+.

### Step 4 — Name + Peak Cultivator

- Name the sect (2–4 words, evocative)
- Name the peak cultivator with realm and one signature trait

### Step 5 — Spend SP on Resources (§26)

Distribute your SP budget across the Resource Menu. Note what you buy.

### Step 6 — Fill Signature Slots

Use your Signature Slots (§24) for the sect's named, iconic features: Ancestors, inherited treasures, planetary formations, unique Oaths.

### Step 7 — Seed 2–4 Internal Factions (§18)

Pick from §18.1 or invent. Give each a goal and a grievance. At least one faction should be sympathetic to the likely PCs.

### Step 8 — Write the External Web

- 1–2 sworn enemies
- 1 rival
- 1 patron/ally
- 1 debt (owed or owing)
- 1 tributary (if any)

### Step 9 — Write The Secret

One sentence: what this sect hides from the world (or from itself). This is the GM's scalpel.

**Output:** one page per sect. Reusable as NPC reference.

## 28. Restrictions & Secrets

As with the treasures and techniques frameworks, **restrictions are narrative weights that earn design refunds or dramatic hooks.**

### 28.1 Sect Restrictions (SP Refunds)

A sect can take Restrictions to get SP back and gain flavor. Each restriction below refunds SP but creates campaign friction.

| Restriction | SP Refund | Flavor |
|---|---|---|
| **Ancestor in seclusion for N centuries** | 2 SP | Their Signature Slot is "asleep" — powerful but can't be invoked |
| **Dying spirit vein** | 3 SP | Vein drops one tier in 10 years unless rejuvenated |
| **Unpaid Blood Debt** | 3 SP | A sworn enemy has an active Blood Debt pursuer clock |
| **Brittle succession** | 2 SP | Sect Master is aging; no clear heir |
| **Doctrinal schism** | 4 SP | Internal demonic cell within an Orthodox sect (or vice versa) |
| **Severed branch** | 3 SP | A former branch sect is now a rival |
| **Sealed-but-resurging enemy** | 4 SP | The sect sealed a Nirvana entity long ago; the seal is weakening |
| **Karmic curse** | 3 SP | Inherited curse affects every Ancestor's tribulation |
| **Loss of signature treasure** | 2 SP | One of the sect's inherited treasures is currently missing |

### 28.2 The Secret (Required, Every Sect)

Every sect has one secret. This doesn't cost SP — it's a design requirement. Pick from below or invent.

- **The Buried Founder** — the sect's founder didn't die, they were sealed by the current Ancestors
- **The Demonic Root** — this Orthodox sect's cultivation method is secretly demonic-derived
- **The Extinct Bloodline** — this Clan-sect's ruling bloodline died out centuries ago; the current "heirs" are impostors
- **The Stolen Inheritance** — the sect's signature treasure was stolen from another sect, who still hunts for it
- **The Traitor-Ancestor** — one of the reclusive Ancestors has already betrayed the sect to a rival; they're just waiting for the moment
- **The Puppet** — the Sect Master is a Celestial Guard puppet; the real Sect Master died decades ago
- **The Cave World Exile** — the sect's true headquarters is a hidden Cave World; the visible sect is a decoy
- **The Ghost Disciple** — a founding Elder died but their ghost still participates in Council meetings; only the Sect Master knows

**The Secret is the GM's scalpel.** Don't reveal it unless the plot demands. When it does, it changes everything.

## 29. Worked Examples

### 29.1 Example Sect — Heng Yue Sect (Rank 3, Orthodox, Sword Arts)

A classic early-Xian Ni feel — the PCs' starter sect.

- **Rank:** 3 (town-level) · 10 SP · 2 Signature Slots
- **Doctrine:** Orthodox · **Specialization:** Sword Arts (primary)
- **Peak Cultivator:** Elder Sun Dazhu, Qi Condensation 12 (late-QC; unusually weak for a CF-peak sect — see Restriction)
- **Governance:** Independent
- **Territory:** Heng Yue Mountain + a few surrounding villages

**SP spend (10 SP):**
- 1 named Elder above rank-typical (2) — *Elder Yuan, Foundation Establishment Late (unusually strong)*
- 2 Mysterious-tier sword techniques (1 + 1 = 2) — *Heng Yue Sword Form, Rising Crane Slash*
- 1 Mysterious-tier signature sword (2) — *Scarlet Tongue, a Refined-quality flying sword (Signature Slot 1)*
- Faint spirit vein (0; default) + rank-typical defensive formation (3)
- Tributary sect (2) — a tiny Rank 1 village sect owes Heng Yue annual tribute

**Restrictions:** Peak Cultivator is one realm below rank-typical (+2 SP) — instead of CF, they're Peak QC. Brittle succession (+2 SP) — Elder Sun Dazhu is aging. **Total refund: +4 SP**, spent on extra signature technique + extended territory.

**Signature Slot 1:** Scarlet Tongue (signature sword, inherited)
**Signature Slot 2:** *(empty — reserved for future inheritance)*

**Factions:**
- *Elder Sun's Loyalists* — traditional, respect the rank ladder
- *Elder Yuan's Circle* — stronger, younger, want Yuan as the next Sect Master
- *The Outer Disciple Collective* — resentful of Inner Disciple privileges

**External:**
- **Sworn enemy:** The Four Factions (mountain bandits who killed the previous Sect Master)
- **Rival:** The nearby Green Bamboo Sect (same rank, competes for Outer Disciple recruits)
- **Ally:** Gossamer Temple (Rank 3 Alchemy sect; trade swords for pills)
- **Debt:** owed to the Rank 5 Flamebird Sect, who aided in a past conflict

**The Secret:** Elder Sun Dazhu knows the sect's true founder was a Demonic-Path cultivator who converted to Orthodoxy three generations ago. The original sect manual is hidden in a sealed library only the Sect Master can access.

### 29.2 Example Sect — Ghost Sect (Rank 9, Demonic, Corpse/Ghost Arts)

A Rank 9 setting-defining sect; the shadow over the star system.

- **Rank:** 9 (setting-defining) · 90 SP · 6 Signature Slots
- **Doctrine:** Demonic · **Specialization:** Corpse/Ghost Arts (primary), Soul Arts (secondary)
- **Peak Cultivator:** Ghost Ancestor "The Withered Crown," Nirvana Shatterer
- **Governance:** Sect-sovereign; owns its own Rank 8 planet, "Deadveil"
- **Territory:** Planet Deadveil + 5 branch planets across Cloud Sea

**Signature Slots (6 filled):**
1. **Ghost Ancestor The Withered Crown** — Nirvana Shatterer; in seclusion for 2,000 years
2. **Second Ancestor Lady Gall** — Nirvana Cleanser; active, serves as de facto Sect Master
3. **The Ten-Thousand Soul Cauldron** — Nirvana-tier inherited treasure; weapon of mass soul-refinement
4. **Deadveil Planetary Formation** — Nirvana-grade defensive formation (even Soul Transformation cultivators can't enter without a Soul-Token)
5. **The Bone Registry** — sect's Soul-Arts archive; contains Heart Oaths of every soul owed to Ghost Sect across a thousand years
6. **The Crown Edict** — sect-wide standing Heart Oath: all disciples owe The Withered Crown unquestioning obedience upon awakening

**SP spend (88 SP of 90):**
- 4 additional Elders (8 SP) — ST to Ascendant
- 2 Grand Elders (8 SP) — Ascendant and Nirvana Scryer
- 3 Nirvana-tier signature techniques (9) — *Crown-Ash Seal, Ten-Thousand Ghost Tide, Withered-Face Curse*
- 2 Void-tier signature techniques (2) — *Bone Net, Soul Shackle*
- 2 Void-tier signature treasures (4) — *Gall's Staff, The Grave Bell*
- Expanded disciple count (3) — 30,000+ disciples across all planets
- Control of Celestial Door (8) — the Deadveil Door leads to the Wind Celestial Realm; Ghost Sect taxes passage
- Grand spirit vein (8) — a Primal-grade vein on Deadveil
- 5 tributary sects (10) — smaller Demonic sects across Cloud Sea
- Alliance seat / Cloud Sea Rank-9 Council position (3)
- Setting-wide infamy (2) — mention of Ghost Sect makes mortals sign themselves
- Rare resource control (4) — cornered the star system's soul-crystal market
- Reverse-Karma tradition (4) — Ghost Sect disciples can sever their own Black Karma via ritual soul-refinement (expensive)
- Infamous Heart Oath (2) — *The Crown Edict*; already placed as Signature Slot 6
- Mercenary reputation (3) — Ghost Sect disciples hired for assassination across the star system (brand: "If you need it quiet")
- Expanded territory — already covered by 5 branch planets (8)

**Restrictions (+2 SP refunded, spent above):**
- **Ancestor in seclusion** (The Withered Crown, 2,000 years) — a sleeping demigod who *might* wake
- **Karmic curse** (inherited) — every Ghost Ancestor's tribulation is 2 tiers harsher

**Factions:**
- *The Withered Crown's Circle* — the sleeping Ancestor's disciples; conservative, traditional; wait for the Awakening
- *Lady Gall's Active Faction* — runs the sect day-to-day; pragmatic, outward-facing
- *The Bone Physicians* — the sect's Alchemy-Soul specialist wing; quiet, dangerous
- *The Branch Rivalry* — the Blackmaw branch on a neighboring planet is openly contesting Lady Gall's authority

**External:**
- **Sworn enemies:** God Sect (the Rank 9 Orthodox antithesis)
- **Rivals:** Demon Sect (same doctrine, different specialization; uneasy peers)
- **Tributaries:** 5 named lesser Demonic sects across Cloud Sea
- **Debt:** owed a favor to Heaven Breaking Sect from a 3,000-year-old war

**The Secret:** The Withered Crown didn't voluntarily enter seclusion — they were sealed by Lady Gall after the previous Nirvana tribulation went wrong. If The Withered Crown awakens, they will kill Lady Gall and most of the current inner circle before anyone can react.

### 30.4 The Sanity-Check Questions

Before finalizing any sect or world, ask:

**For a sect:**
- Is the rank consistent with peak cultivator, disciple count, and territory?
- Does the Specialization show up in at least 3 mechanical places (techniques, treasures, Elders)?
- Are there 2+ internal factions with real tensions?
- Are there 5 external relationships defined?
- Is there one Secret?

**For a planet:**
- Is there 1 resource worth fighting over?
- Is there 1 political feature that generates plot?
- Is there 1 danger or mystery?
- Do the three sentences tell me how the PCs will encounter this planet?

**For a star system:**
- Are the 3–5 Great Powers distinguishable?
- Do their rivalries generate plot?
- Is there a horizon (Celestial Realms, Immortal Astral Continent)?
- Is there a hidden Rank 9 planet for late-campaign?
- Are there 3 tensions the GM can pull on at any time?

If any answer is "no," revise.

## Closing Note

A world is not a list of places, it's a **web of tensions.** A sect is not a list of features, it's a **small society with two factions and one secret.** The budgets and menus in this framework exist to make sure the tension and the secret are paid attention to — not to replace them.

Build the big frame first (star system, in one evening), then planets as you need them (one paragraph each), then sects as the PCs meet them (one page each). The world grows at the pace of play.

The Heavens are hostile. The sects are political. The planets are finite. Every treasure has a prior owner. Every Elder has a grudge. Every Ancestor has a secret.

*And every secret, eventually, gets out.*

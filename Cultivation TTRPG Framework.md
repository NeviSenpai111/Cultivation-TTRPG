# Cultivation TTRPG Framework
### A system inspired by *Renegade Immortal* (仙逆)

**Design ethos:** Ruthless pragmatism. Talent is a cruel lottery. Preparation beats power. The Heavens are hostile, and defiance is earned through suffering — not granted. No plot armor, no chosen-one safety net. If you die unprepared, you die.

## 1. The Five Design Problems

Cultivation worlds break most TTRPG assumptions. The framework must solve:

1. **The realm gap** — higher-realm cultivators don't just out-level, they *crush*.
2. **Long timelines** — years of seclusion, decade-long breakthroughs.
3. **Techniques vs. realm** — weaker cultivators should sometimes punch up via superior technique, treasure, or Dao.
4. **Karma, face, and sect politics** — mechanical, not just flavor.
5. **Dao comprehension** — not the same as realm; two same-realm cultivators can be functionally different.

---

## 2. The Six Pillars

### Pillar 1: Realm as the Primary Power Axis (d6 Dice Pool)
- Roll a pool of d6s equal to **Realm Tier + relevant Attribute**.
- Count 4+ as successes.
- Realm tiers scale: Mortal (0), Qi Condensation (1–4), Foundation Establishment (5–8), Core Formation (9–12), Nascent Soul (13–16), and upward through the Soul stages, Ascendant, and Nirvana stages (capping at Nirvana Shatterer, Tier 40).
- **Realm Barrier rule:** when attacking someone a full realm above you, they cancel your successes 1-for-1 before damage. Bypassing this requires techniques, treasures, or Dao insight.

### Pillar 2: One Realm, Three Attribute Scores
- A cultivator has **one realm** at any given time (e.g., "Qi Condensation Mid" or "Foundation Establishment Peak"). This is what other cultivators see, what sects use to rank you, what tribulations respond to.
- Within that realm, the character has **three attribute scores** that define how they've cultivated: **Body** (physique, strikes, durability), **Qi** (spiritual energy capacity, technique power, flight), **Soul** (sense, craft, formation, soul defense).
- All three attributes start at 1–3 and grow to 5 through play. A body cultivator has high Body and lower Qi; a classic cultivator inverts that.
- **Dice pools use the shared Realm Tier plus the relevant attribute**, so a Foundation Establishment cultivator with Body 4 / Qi 2 hits hard physically but has a weaker qi reserve.
- **Parallel cultivation paths** (Ancient God body cultivation, Ji Realm, etc.) are separate tracks that can run alongside the main realm — see the Optional Rules layer.

### Pillar 3: Techniques as Graded Modules
- Tiers: **Spirit → Mysterious → Celestial → Void → Nirvana.**
- Each requires a minimum cultivation base, costs qi to activate, has an effect.
- High-tier techniques can bypass or reduce Realm Barriers — this is the underdog engine.
- Techniques are collectible: tradeable, stealable, inheritable.

### Pillar 4: Dao Comprehension (Parallel Track)
- Independent of realm.
- Players pick a Dao Seed; accumulate Insight through meaningful experience (not just combat).
- Duels, loss, witnessing, meditation — all can grant Insight.
- Insight unlocks perks that pierce Realm Barriers. This is where "great Dao beats higher realm" lives.

### Pillar 5: Breakthroughs as Structured Events
- To advance: **Resources + Insight + Breakthrough Scene.**
- Possible outcomes: Success / Stalled / Qi Deviation / Catastrophic Deviation.
- Higher realms trigger **Heavenly Tribulation** — escalating lightning/karmic trials as a full set-piece scene.

### Pillar 6: Seclusion, Karma, and Face (Downtime Systems)
- Seclusion actions: cultivate, refine pill, study technique, forge artifact.
- Time passes in months or years.
- **Karma** — saved lives, broken oaths, blood debts (resurfaces mechanically).
- **Face** — social currency with sects, clans, rivals. Opens/closes doors.

## 3. Character Creation

### Step 0: Session Zero — Frame & Group Concept

Before any dice roll, the table agrees on a few things. This prevents mismatched expectations and ugly session-one surprises.

**Discuss and agree:**
- **Campaign Frame** (see section 12.9) — Sect Disciple, Rogue Cultivator, Clan Heir, Mortal World Survivors, Sect War, or Exploration. This affects Step 8 (Affiliation) significantly.
- **Starting Realm** — usually Mortal crossing into Qi Condensation during session one, but higher starts are possible (Foundation Establishment for experienced-character campaigns).
- **Random Roots confirmed** — confirm the table agrees with the fully-random spiritual root rule (Section 4). Any table that wants to opt out should have that conversation *now*, not mid-campaign.
- **Lethality expectations** — the system is deliberately brutal. Confirm everyone's on board. Dead is dead, unless prepared.
- **Group concept** — same sect? rival sects? sworn siblings? rogue band? family? The creation process differs based on whether PCs start bonded or independent.
- **Content comfort** — the setting canonically includes the Demonic path, ritual sacrifice, slavery-adjacent binding, and cruelty. What's in and out for your table?

The GM also decides the **starting setting scale** at this point (see section 12.10). A region, a planet, or a star-system campaign all start differently.

### Step 1: Origin & Background

Every cultivator was someone before they were a cultivator. Pick an **Origin** — this grants a small mechanical package and seeds your character's social fabric.

| Origin | Attribute | Lores (knowledge) | Practices (skills) | Starting Wealth | Starting Face | Family Ties | Hook |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Mortal Village Youth** *(Wang Lin's)* | +1 Body | Mortal Lore, Herb Lore | Survival, Athletics | Impoverished | Face 1 in home village | Living parents (Life Debt) | Filial obligation |
| **Fallen Noble** | +1 Soul | Sect Politics, Mortal Lore | Etiquette, Insight | Modest (debts) | Face 2 noble / 0 elsewhere | Scattered kin, ruined name | House to restore or family that sold you out |
| **Merchant's Child** | +1 to any | Mortal Lore, Artifact Appraisal | Negotiation, Deception | Modest | Face 1 across 2–3 trade cities | Living trading family | A specific debt |
| **Mountain Hunter / Herbalist** | +1 Body | Herb Lore, Beast Lore | Tracking, Survival | Modest (self-reliant) | Face 0 (rural) | Isolated family or sole survivor | A wilderness only you know |
| **Servant of a Sect** | +1 Soul | Sect Politics, Cultivation Lore | Stealth, Etiquette | Impoverished | Face 1 in home sect | A former master | Unfinished sect business |
| **Orphan / Wanderer** | +1 to any | Mortal Lore | Stealth, Survival | Destitute | Face 0 | **No family, no safety net** | Your life starts now |
| **Battlefield Survivor** | +1 Body | Cultivation Lore | Intimidation, Tracking | Modest (loot) | Face 1 among veterans | Scattered comrades | **Bloodstained karma** — a past kill haunts you |
| **Young Master / Clan Heir** | +1 Soul | Sect Politics, Cultivation Lore | Etiquette, Insight | Wealthy | Face 3 clan / 1 allied | Extensive clan; rivals | Inherited duties, inherited enemies |

**GM's discretion on variants:** Temple Initiate (+1 Soul, religious context, spiritual calling), Criminal/Outlaw (+1 any, Face **−1** as infamous, active Blood Debt), and others work the same way — take a base origin and reflavor.

**Starting Karma from Origin:**
- **Mortal Village Youth** — Life Debt owed to parents/family
- **Fallen Noble** — Ruined Name accumulates as a soft Black Karma (−1)
- **Battlefield Survivor** — one named Blood Debt from a past kill (pursuer clock inactive but real)
- **Sect Servant** — dependent relationship with former master (unresolved)
- **Young Master** — one inherited family enemy (Blood Debt from ancestor's action)

Origin also shapes **literacy, dialect, and cultural knowledge**, which matters more than you'd think — the merchant's child can read auction ledgers; the mountain hunter cannot. The young master knows sect protocol instinctively; the orphan has to fake it.

### Step 2: The Three Attribute Scores

Distribute **5 points** across **Body**, **Qi**, and **Soul**. Minimum 1, maximum 3 at creation. Origin's attribute bonus adds on top (so a Body-focused Village Youth could end up with Body 4 at character creation).

These are **attribute scores** — numerical proficiencies that grow over play, separate from your cultivation realm. You have **one realm** at any given time (Qi Condensation, Foundation Establishment, etc.); your attribute scores describe how you've cultivated within that realm. Dice pools combine both: **Attribute Score + Realm Tier + modifiers**.

#### What Each Attribute Does

**Body** — physical conditioning, pain tolerance, unarmed and weapon strikes, poison resistance, wound capacity (section 8.7). A Body focus means you hit hard, soak damage, and recover fast. Body cultivators scale unusually well — canonically, a Body Refinement path at Ascendant can match a Qi cultivator a full realm higher.

**Qi** — spiritual energy capacity (section 8.8 qi pool), technique power, elemental manipulation, later flight. Qi is the classic cultivator stat. Most combat techniques scale off it, and its role rises at higher realms.

**Soul** — perception, spiritual sense, soul attacks, formation work, **all crafting** (section 10.2.1), artifact refining, soul defense, and resistance to Presence. A Soul focus means you're a crafter, a scholar, or a subtle combatant. Also drives countermeasure awareness — a high-Soul character *senses* threats others miss.

#### Attribute Score Scale

| Score | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **1** | Weak / neglected — a liability |
| **2** | Typical disciple level |
| **3** | Strong focus (starting maximum) |
| **4** | Exceptional — reached only through advancement in play |
| **5** | Peak mortal / near-superhuman for your realm — hard cap |

Attribute scores grow through play (see section 7.10, *Attribute Advancement*). They do **not** automatically rise when you breakthrough a realm — they must be deliberately cultivated.

#### Archetype Suggestions (5 points, before Origin bonus)

| Archetype | Body | Qi | Soul | Feels Like |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Body Cultivator** | 3 | 1 | 1 | A walking fortress; devastating physical strikes, hard to kill |
| **Classic Cultivator** | 1 | 3 | 1 | The novels' default — techniques, flight, elemental attacks |
| **Scholar / Crafter** | 1 | 1 | 3 | The party alchemist/formation master; weak in a pinch, invaluable in seclusion |
| **Sword Cultivator** | 2 | 2 | 1 | Blade and qi; the duelist archetype |
| **Balanced Disciple** | 2 | 2 | 1 | Well-rounded; no weakness, no standout |
| **Soul Cultivator** | 1 | 2 | 2 | Subtle combatant; Soul attacks, formations, perception-based play |

There is no "wrong" distribution, but starting with a 1 in one attribute is a real liability — your weakest stat gates entire subsystems. A Body 1 character will struggle in physical crises; a Soul 1 character will never be a crafter. Choose deliberately.

You begin at **Mortal (Tier 0)**. You are not a cultivator *yet* — you become one by learning your first Cultivation Method (Step 5) and proceeding through session-one play, where you typically reach **Qi Condensation Early (Tier 1)**.

### Step 3: Spiritual Roots — The Talent Roll

**Roll 2d10. Publicly, at the table. No rerolls. The dice decide.** Spiritual root tests are public ceremonies in-fiction; the roll is witnessed.

| Roll | Root Grade | Cultivation Speed | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| 2 | **Heavenly Spiritual Root** | ×3 | **Single pure element** (player-chosen from Five Elements). One in a generation; sect-level recruitment offers. |
| 3–4 | **Heavenly Dao Root** | ×2.5 | **Dual harmonious elements** (player-chosen pair — must be harmonious, see Five Elements below). Sect-prized. |
| 5–7 | **Superior Root** | ×2 | **Triple-element** (player-chosen combination). Any Rank 5+ sect wants you. |
| 8–12 | **Common Root** | ×1 | **Four or five elements mixed** (player picks which four or five). *Wang Lin starts here.* |
| 13–15 | **Mortal Root (Waste Root)** | ×0.5 | All five elements, impure. Cannot normally pass Foundation Establishment. |
| 16–17 | **Broken Root** | — | Damaged. Cannot cultivate through normal means. |
| 18–19 | **Mutant Root** | Variable | Unusual element — **roll on Mutant Root table below**. Non-chosen; the mutation picked *you*. |
| 20 | **Heavenly Body** | N/A | Roll on Heavenly Body table (section 5) instead. |

#### The Five Elements & Their Relationships

The canonical five elements of cultivation are:

| Element | 五行 | Associations |
|---|---|---|
| **Metal** (金) | Gold / blade / refinement | Sharp cuts, weapons, armor, purification |
| **Wood** (木) | Growth / life | Healing, poison, vitality, plant-shaping |
| **Water** (水) | Flow / ice-adjacent | Adaptation, flowing techniques, ice (secondary), soul-calming |
| **Fire** (火) | Flame / destruction | Direct damage, forging, explosions |
| **Earth** (土) | Stone / stability | Defense, formation-adjacent, endurance, terrain shaping |

**Generative Cycle (相生 — harmonious pairs):**
- Wood feeds Fire
- Fire produces Earth (ash)
- Earth bears Metal
- Metal carries Water (condensation)
- Water nourishes Wood

**Destructive Cycle (相克 — opposing pairs):**
- Wood pierces Earth
- Earth absorbs Water
- Water quenches Fire
- Fire melts Metal
- Metal cuts Wood

**Why this matters:**

- **Heavenly Dao Root** (dual element): the two elements must be **harmonious** (adjacent on the generative cycle). You can pick Wood+Fire, Fire+Earth, Earth+Metal, Metal+Water, or Water+Wood. You *cannot* pick opposing pairs — a Wood+Metal dual root would be clashing, not harmonious.
- **Superior Root** (triple element): pick any three elements. Three harmonious elements in sequence are strongest (Wood+Fire+Earth, Fire+Earth+Metal, etc.). Three with a destructive pair included work but cost more Insight to master advanced techniques.
- **Common Root** (four or five): with four or five elements, you always have some internal conflict. This is why Common Root cultivators advance at ×1 speed — the elements pull against each other.
- **Technique compatibility:** a technique keyed to a specific element (e.g., Crimson Pulse Arrow is Fire) gets **+1 die** when cast by a cultivator whose roots include that element, and **−1 die** if it opposes one of your elements (a Water-rooted cultivator casting a Fire technique faces internal resistance).

**For Mortal Root (13–15):** you have *all five* elements but none of them are pure. Every element roll modifier cancels out. You get no element bonus but also no element penalty — you're the blank slate the Heavens rejected.

#### The Mutant Root Table (rolled on 18–19)

When you roll Mutant Root, the element is not your choice — something rarer and stranger has awakened in you. Roll 1d6:

| d6 | Mutant Root | Element Behavior |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Ice Root** | Water variant; ×1 cultivation speed, but ice techniques gain +2 dice and fire techniques lose −2 dice. Excels in cold/polar regions. |
| 2 | **Thunder Root** | Lightning variant of Metal+Fire fusion; ×1.25 speed; breakthrough tribulations are harsher (+1 wave). Favored by cultivators on the edge of enlightenment. |
| 3 | **Wood-Metal Hybrid** | Conflicting elements held in paradox; ×0.75 speed, but any technique you produce is unique — produces "Blood Silver" qi that pierces armor. Sect alchemists will study you. |
| 4 | **Yin Spirit Root** | Darkness/ghost-adjacent; ×1 speed in Soul-path cultivation, ×0.5 in Body-path. Excels in Ghost and Corpse Daos. Sects of orthodox alignment may distrust you. |
| 5 | **Yang Spirit Root** | Light/life-adjacent; ×1 speed in Life and Medicine Daos; weak in concealment (aura shines naturally). Can resist demonic corruption easily. |
| 6 | **Unnamed / Unique** | GM invents something specific to the campaign — a root tied to a cosmic phenomenon, a bloodline echo, an ancient pact. One-of-a-kind in the setting. |

Mutant roots cultivate at **×1 in their affinity** and **×0.5 in opposing elements**. They qualify as "exotic" in the cultivation world — sects and clans may hunt down specific mutant types for their research or bloodline collections.

#### Non-Standard Elements Beyond the Five

Some settings include elements outside the canonical five — Lightning, Wind, Light, Dark, Space, Time, etc. These are **not** available to Heavenly / Heavenly Dao / Superior / Common roots through normal means. They show up only through:

- **Mutant Root** (rolled, not chosen)
- **Heavenly Body** (specific bodies like Nine-Yin, Thunder Body, Void Spirit Body)
- **Bloodline awakenings** (plot events during play)
- **Cultivation path specialization** (a cultivator who has reached Soul Formation and comprehends the relevant Dao can *refine* a non-standard element into their cultivation)

This means at character creation, the Five Elements are your palette unless the dice hand you something weirder. This is deliberately constraining — it keeps starting characters grounded while leaving room for the exotic to arrive through play.

#### What "Cultivation Speed" Modifies

Cultivation speed modifies how quickly **Insight**, **milestones**, and **sub-stage advancement** proceed during Seclusion. At ×0.5 (Mortal Root), a seclusion slot grants roughly half the normal Insight; milestones take twice as long.

Root also affects the **Breakthrough Roll** (section 7.4): Heavenly Root adds +3 dice, Heavenly Dao +2, Superior +1, Common +0, Mortal −2, Broken (cannot roll through normal means).

#### Heavenly Resentment Pool (the Wang Lin Mechanic)

Characters with **Mortal Root or Broken Root** gain a starting **Heavenly Resentment pool of 3**. Mutant Root characters start with 1.

Players may **voluntarily accept a worse root** for extra Resentment: downgrade one grade for +2 Resentment (so a player rolling Common Root could declare they actually have a Mortal Root for a total pool of 5). This is permanent and confirms the Wang Lin archetype.

**Spending Heavenly Resentment:**

| Cost | Effect |
|---|---|
| 1 | Reroll a die in any roll after seeing the result |
| 1 | Cancel 1 success of a Realm Barrier (once per scene) |
| 2 | Add +2 dice to a breakthrough roll |
| 2 | Survive a killing blow *once* — equivalent to a prepared countermeasure, but at the cost of losing all remaining Resentment and any chance of refilling it for the next 3 sessions |
| 3 | Unlock a hidden inheritance, dormant bloodline, or plot-level discovery (GM-arbitrated; story-significant) |

**Refilling Resentment:**
- +1 when defeated / humiliated by a higher-realm cultivator and you vow to surpass them (Heart Oath-adjacent)
- +1 when losing a major Tie (death of a loved one, betrayal by a sworn ally)
- +1 at each session where you suffer for your weakness and refuse to quit

Resentment caps at (starting pool + 3). It cannot be grinded — the Heavens see through performance.

**Design intent:** Resentment mechanizes *defiance*. A Wang Lin character isn't just weak — they're *angry*, and that anger is a resource. It keeps low-root characters competitive without handing them power they didn't earn.

### Step 4: Dao Seed

You don't *comprehend* a Dao at creation. You have a **predisposition** — a Dao Seed. Insight into this Dao accumulates at **2× the normal rate**. Other Daos remain accessible, just slower.

**Starting Dao Insight: 0** in your Seed Dao. You'll earn the first points through play (combat Moments of Truth, seclusion meditation, key experiences).

#### Dao Categories

**Elemental** — common, combat-versatile, well-documented
- **Sword** — blade-work incarnate; bonus to sword techniques; sentient swords favor you
- **Fire** — destructive, volatile, common among young masters
- **Water** — flowing, adaptive, healing-adjacent
- **Earth** — defensive, formation-adjacent, slow but enduring
- **Metal** — sharp, piercing, armor and weapons
- **Wood** — life, growth, healing; harmonizes with Medicine Dao
- **Wind** — speed, evasion, reconnaissance
- **Lightning** — fast breakthroughs, harsh tribulations, high-risk-high-reward

**Conceptual** — rare, difficult, incredibly powerful at Adept+
- **Space** — teleportation, spatial storage, distance manipulation (Wang Lin's Core-Treasure Sword had this)
- **Time** — slow/speed/loop; almost always Void+ tier; campaign-defining if reached
- **Karma** — see karmic threads; manipulate debts; canonical peak of Wang Lin's Daos
- **Cause & Effect** — subtle influence over outcomes; detective/political power

**Dark** — fast progression, karmic cost
- **Slaughter** — gains from kills; demands them
- **Demonic** — brute power with corruption track
- **Ghost** — soul-adjacent, spectral
- **Corpse** — undeath, reanimation, reach into death

**Life-Death**
- **Life** — healing, resurrection, bloom
- **Death** — decay, entropy, endings
- **Medicine** — heals; pairs with Alchemy craft; orthodox healer path
- **Poison** — debilitates; pairs with Alchemy craft; demonic/assassin path

**Craft Daos** — slow in combat, devastating in downtime
- **Formation** — inscribed arrays; tactical master-planning
- **Artifact** — forge treasures; the path of refiner-masters
- **Alchemy** — can overlap Medicine/Poison as primary craft Dao

**Personal / Unconventional** — Wang Lin-style
- **Solitude** — the strength of being alone against the world
- **Endurance** — outlast everything
- **Resentment** — the will to defy Heaven; pairs naturally with Heavenly Resentment pool
- **Perseverance** — refusing to stop

#### Dao Compatibility

You can pursue multiple Daos, but not all pair well:
- **Natural pairings:** Sword + Wind, Fire + Lightning, Metal + Earth, Medicine + Life, Slaughter + Demonic
- **Difficult but powerful:** Space + Time (Void+ tier; ludicrous power ceiling), Karma + Cause & Effect
- **Opposing pairs** (one cancels the other): Life vs. Death, Medicine vs. Poison, Slaughter vs. Life, Order (Cause & Effect) vs. Demonic

Pursuing opposing Daos simultaneously is canonically possible but brutally hard — the tension can grant unique insights but often causes qi deviation at breakthroughs.

### Step 5: Starting Cultivation Method

Your **Cultivation Method** is the core manual that governs how you refine qi. It's distinct from individual combat techniques — it's the *framework* within which all your cultivation happens. Every method has:

- **Type** (Orthodox / Demonic / Body / Incomplete / Self-Taught)
- **Peak Realm** — the highest realm you can reach before needing to replace or refine the method
- **Method-Root Compatibility** — some methods demand specific roots
- **Breakthrough aids or costs** — bonuses or penalties at milestone/breakthrough rolls

| Method Type | Peak Realm Cap | Bonus | Cost |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Orthodox Sect Method** | Typically Soul Formation; sect-specific | Safe, standard; +1 die to Spirit-Gathering formations; compatible with most pills and resources | Sect-branded — loyalty expectations; methods not portable between sects |
| **Demonic Method** | Variable (some reach Nirvana); no canonical cap | +1 cultivation speed tier; faster Insight | Accumulates **Demonic Heart** corruption (1 point per seclusion using the method); +1 Black Karma per major use; Qi Deviation when Demonic Heart ≥ 10 |
| **Body Refinement Method** | Typically Ascendant | +1 Body tier advancement; Body wounds halved | Qi growth cut in half — Qi realm lags permanently; qi-based techniques less powerful |
| **Family Heirloom / Incomplete** | Partial — missing upper realms | Unique techniques; often a secret | Must find the rest of the method by Core Formation or stall; fragmentary methods canonical of Wang Lin's early path |
| **Self-Taught / Improvised** | Limited; depends on talent | Resistance to doctrinal mind attacks; creative improvisation (+1 die on novel technique use) | Slow progression (−1 Insight per seclusion at breakthroughs); no support network |

#### Example Named Methods

- **Heng Yue Pure Heart Method** (Orthodox; Foundation Establishment peak) — Wang Lin's first sect method; solid, unremarkable, broadly compatible
- **Underworld Ascension Method** (Demonic; Ascendant peak) — Situ Nan's gift to Wang Lin canonically; fast but corrupting
- **Iron Flesh Scripture** (Body Refinement; Soul Formation peak) — brutal physical grinding
- **Nine Yang Golden Body** (Body Refinement, Yang variant; Ascendant peak) — better quality, requires fire-affinity root
- **Three-Slash Sword Method** (Orthodox sword specialization; Soul Formation peak) — incomplete method with three named slashes
- **Wandering Cloud Scripture** (Self-Taught; variable) — a patchwork grimoire from disparate sources

#### Upgrading Methods

You can *replace* your method (not simultaneously run two) via:
- **Sect advancement** — promotion from Outer to Inner grants a better method in many sects
- **Inheritance** — finding a superior method in a tomb, inheritance, or master's gift
- **Writing your own** — at Soul Formation+, cultivators can combine insights to write improved methods; at Nirvana, they can create methods that transcend their original peak

Replacing a method is disruptive — it typically requires a seclusion dedicated to reconciling the new with the old. Mismatch causes temporary qi deviation.

### Step 6: Starting Techniques

You get **2 Spirit-tier techniques** — the seeds of your combat repertoire. They come from your Cultivation Method, your Origin, or your starting sect. Pick with your archetype in mind.

#### Sample Spirit-Tier Techniques to Choose From

**Attack**
- **Cloud Piercing Finger** (1 qi) — ranged Qi attack, +2 dice
- **Azure Wind Slash** (1 qi) — sword/blade attack, +2 dice
- **Rising Sun Palm** (1 qi) — close-range fire-tinged strike, +2 dice
- **Crimson Pulse Arrow** (1 qi) — bow/projectile, +2 dice
- **Bone-Shaking Shout** (1 qi) — soul-adjacent, intimidate/stun, +2 dice (Soul-based)

**Defense**
- **Iron Skin (Lesser)** (1 qi per activation) — +1 die to physical defense for a scene
- **Cloud Barrier** (1 qi) — temporary qi shield, absorbs 1 success
- **Sliding Step** (1 qi) — reactive dodge, +2 dice to one defense roll

**Movement**
- **Concealment Step** (1 qi) — stealth/evasion, +2 dice to evasion or stealth
- **Wind Walk** (1 qi) — short burst speed, double movement for one action
- **Shadow Trace** (1 qi) — silent, leaves no tracks for a scene

**Utility**
- **Spirit Sight** (1 qi) — reveals spiritual presences / auras nearby
- **Muffle Sound** (1 qi) — silence around you for a scene
- **Minor Flame** (free) — basic fire ignition, illumination

Players can also propose original Spirit-tier techniques with GM approval. The examples above are a menu, not a limit.

#### Technique Compatibility

Techniques with your **Dao Seed** resonate — they're easier to master (Mastery advances with ×1.5 Insight) and may gain flavor bonuses. A Sword Dao cultivator picking "Azure Wind Slash" is a stronger choice than picking "Cloud Piercing Finger."

### Step 7: Starting Kit, Wealth, and Countermeasures

Your material starting point depends on origin and affiliation.

#### Default Starting Kit (all cultivators)

- **1 mortal weapon or tool** — sword, staff, dagger, herb-gathering knife, scholar's brush — whatever fits origin
- **Travel supplies** — 3 months of rations (longer for hunter origins)
- **Basic clothing** — sect robes if affiliated; plain travel garb if not

#### Spirit Stones (Starting Wealth)

Roll 1d6 low-grade spirit stones. Modify by Origin:

| Origin | Starting Spirit Stones |
|---|---|
| Destitute (Orphan, Wanderer) | 1d3 low-grade |
| Impoverished (Village Youth, Sect Servant) | 1d6 low-grade |
| Modest (most origins) | 2d6 low-grade |
| Wealthy (Young Master, prosperous Merchant) | 1d6 mid-grade (= ~10 low-grade each) |

Spirit stones are the universal cultivation currency — fuel for techniques (occasionally), materials for formations, consumed at breakthroughs, used to pay tuition, purchase pills, and bribe elders.

**Rough equivalences:**
- 1 **low-grade** spirit stone ≈ one month's mortal-world living expenses
- 1 **mid-grade** ≈ 10 low-grade (canonically exchanged in cultivator markets)
- 1 **high-grade** ≈ 10 mid-grade
- 1 **origin-grade** (appears at Void+ treasures) ≈ 10 high-grade

#### Personal Item (Narrative Weight)

Every character starts with one **personal item** — not mechanically powerful, but narratively significant. A mother's ring. A dead brother's sword. A sect master's calligraphy gift. The letter you never delivered.

The GM takes note. This item **will come up in play**. It's an anchor for Karma, a key to hidden plots, or the thing you lose when you're at your lowest.

#### Starting Countermeasures

- **Sect-affiliated** characters: **1 Spirit-tier life-saving talisman** (sect-issued). Single-use. Something like a minor teleportation or a soul-delay effect.
- **Clan Heir** characters: **1 Mysterious-tier talisman** (family heirloom). More powerful; may have one-time activation phrase known to the character.
- **Rogue / Orphan** characters: **no countermeasure talismans** unless they buy them. *This is a meaningful disadvantage.*

#### Spatial Ring

- **Sect-affiliated (Inner Disciple or higher)**: Spirit-grade Spatial Ring (small room volume)
- **Clan Heir**: Mysterious-grade Spatial Ring (large room volume)
- **Outer Disciple / Sect Servant / Merchant's Child**: may or may not have one (GM call; typically no)
- **Other origins**: none — pockets, pouches, and saddlebags

This matters because without a Spatial Ring, you're limited to what you can carry. Pills in your sleeve. A talisman in your belt. When you find treasure, you need to transport it physically.

### Step 8: Affiliation

New step. Your character's **affiliation** is their organizational context — the social structure that defines their starting Face, obligations, and day-to-day stakes. This is where character creation meets the campaign frame (Step 0).

#### Sect Disciple (most common)

- **Sect Name:** agreed with GM or assigned by the campaign
- **Sect Rank:** usually 3–6 at campaign start
- **Tier within Sect:** typically **Honorary Disciple** (entry-level) or **Outer Disciple**. Starting PCs are rarely Inner Disciples unless the campaign is starting higher.
- **Face:** 1 (Honorary) or 2 (Outer) in home sect; 0 elsewhere; Face Type *Righteous* for orthodox sects, *Demonic* for demonic sects
- **Obligations:** monthly sect tasks, loyalty to superiors, attendance at competitions
- **Resources:** basic cultivation method, Spirit-tier techniques, modest pill allowance, starter spatial ring (Inner+)

#### Clan Heir (Allheaven-style campaigns)

- **Clan Name:** agreed with GM
- **Clan Rank:** 3–5 in regional standing
- **Position:** direct-line heir, branch family, or marriage-in
- **Face:** 3 in clan, 1 in allied clans
- **Obligations:** family duties, marriage considerations, honor obligations
- **Resources:** family cultivation method, one family-heirloom technique (Mysterious-tier), access to clan treasury, family spatial ring

#### Rogue Cultivator

- **No formal affiliation.** May have informal ties (mentor, friend, old sect you left).
- **Face:** 0 everywhere by default
- **Obligations:** none — total freedom
- **Resources:** whatever you scrounged; no safety net, no regular pill supply, no one coming to save you

Rogue is the **hardest** starting position mechanically, but it offers the most narrative freedom. Canonically, Wang Lin becomes a rogue multiple times — and he survives by being exceptionally prepared.

#### Mortal World Starter

- **You are not yet a cultivator.** This is the lowest-power frame.
- **No sect, no method yet.** Session one typically sees you encountering the cultivation world for the first time.
- **Face:** may have mortal-world Face (village hero, merchant reputation), but no cultivator Face
- **Obligations:** mortal life — family, village, town duties
- **Resources:** mortal-world equivalent only; no spirit stones or techniques

### Step 9: Lores & Practices

Two kinds of specialties beyond your cultivation bases: **Lores** (what you *know*) and **Practices** (what you *do*). These handle the non-combat specialization space — the skills and knowledge that distinguish a Mountain Hunter from a Fallen Noble even if their attributes match.

#### How They Work

**Lores (binary knowledge domains).** You either have a Lore or you don't. If you have it:
- You automatically know basic facts in that domain (no roll)
- You can attempt advanced or obscure questions with a **Soul + Realm Tier + 2** roll
- Without the Lore, advanced inquiry requires a teacher, a book, or extensive research

**Practices (active skills, +1 die when relevant).** When you roll an action that falls within one of your Practices, add **+1 die** to your pool. You can invest further:
- **Trained (1 rank)** — +1 die
- **Well-Practiced (2 ranks)** — +2 dice
- **Masterful (3 ranks)** — +3 dice (caps here)

Trained vs. Well-Practiced vs. Masterful advances via Seclusion Study (see below) or narrative training.

#### Starting Lores & Practices

You gain:
- **2 Lores + 2 Practices** from your Origin (see Step 1 table)
- **+1 Lore and +1 Practice** from your Affiliation (see below)
- All starting Practices begin at **Trained (+1 die)**

**Affiliation grants:**
- **Sect Disciple** — +1 Cultivation Lore (if not already known; else take another), +1 Spiritual Sense Practice
- **Clan Heir** — +1 Karmic Lore *or* +1 Artifact Appraisal (choice), +1 Negotiation Practice
- **Rogue Cultivator** — +1 free choice Lore (whatever you learned by surviving), +1 free choice Practice
- **Mortal World Starter** — no additional (the cultivation world is new to you; you'll learn in play)

#### The Lore List

| Lore | What It Covers |
|---|---|
| **Mortal Lore** | Mortal politics, trade, languages, dialects, passing as mortal |
| **Sect Politics** | Sect hierarchies, recent events, rivalries, who's who |
| **Cultivation Lore** | Realms, famous techniques, legendary cultivators, cultivation history |
| **Herb & Pill Lore** | Identification of spirit herbs, pill recipes, medicinal effects |
| **Artifact Appraisal** | Identifying treasure grades, binding states, Dao resonance |
| **Beast Lore** | Spirit beasts — realms, habits, weak points, habitats |
| **Ruin & Tomb Lore** | Ancient sites, common restrictions, trap patterns, treasure placement |
| **Karmic Lore** | Reading karmic threads, blood debts, prophetic signs (rare; usually learned only by Karma-path cultivators) |

#### The Practice List

| Practice | What It Covers | Typical Base |
|---|---|---|
| **Spiritual Sense** | Detecting auras, scanning an area for presences, reading someone's realm | Soul |
| **Stealth** | Hiding, silent movement, masking one's own aura | Body or Soul |
| **Deception** | Lies, disguise, forgery, misdirection | Soul |
| **Insight** | Reading intentions, detecting lies, judging character | Soul |
| **Intimidation** | Frightening mortals or weaker cultivators (distinct from Presence) | Body or Face |
| **Negotiation** | Persuasion, deals, alliance-building | Soul / Face |
| **Tracking** | Following trails, wilderness navigation, pursuing by residue | Body |
| **Survival** | Wilderness life, foraging, weathering environments, making shelter | Body |
| **Etiquette** | Formal protocols, sect/clan decorum, diplomatic ceremony | Soul |
| **Athletics** | Climbing, leaping, running, feats of physicality beyond combat | Body |

#### Advancing Lores & Practices in Play

- **Studying in Seclusion**: 1 Seclusion Slot + appropriate teacher, text, or environment → acquire 1 new Lore *or* advance 1 Practice by 1 rank
- **Narrative gain**: the GM may grant Lores from deep experiences (explore an ancient tomb for a full session → gain Ruin & Tomb Lore) and advance Practices from prolonged use (a whole arc of stealth missions → advance Stealth by 1 rank)
- **Master's instruction**: learning from an expert NPC can accelerate gains; a Grandmaster Formation teacher can teach Formation-adjacent Lore in 1 slot where normal study would take 3

#### Design Note

This system deliberately stays lightweight. There are no dozens of skills with proficiency bonuses — just 8 Lores and 10 Practices, most characters know maybe 5 of each by mid-campaign, and their effect is simple (+1 die or +2 dice when relevant). The point is to give mechanical weight to origin and training without bloating character sheets.

**What Lores & Practices deliberately *don't* cover:** pure combat (handled by Body/Qi attributes + techniques + realm), pure cultivation (handled by Insight + Daos), crafting (handled by Craft Ranks + Craft Dao). If an action is better resolved by an existing subsystem, use that.

### Step 10: Karma, Ties, and Heart Oaths

Your starting **Karma Ledger** establishes the web of obligations, grudges, and cosmic debts your character carries into play. See section 9 for the full Karma system.

#### The Three Ties

Write down three named individuals:

1. **One person you love** — parent, sibling, lover, master, sworn friend. Their safety moves you.
2. **One person you owe** — a debt of kindness, a life saved, a promise made. The Life Debt tied here is mechanical.
3. **One person you hate** — specific, not abstract. A rival, a betrayer, a killer of someone you loved. This is the seed of a future Blood Debt.

These three feed the Karma system and give the GM concrete hooks. Your tie-people can appear, be threatened, die, return changed — whatever the plot needs.

#### Optional: Starting Heart Oath

You may choose to begin play with **one Heart Oath** (see section 9.3.4) — a cultivation-binding promise sworn on your origin soul. Examples:
- "I swear on my cultivation to avenge my mother's death before I reach Core Formation."
- "I swear on my Dao to protect my younger sister's child until she is grown."
- "I swear never to take a human life unnecessarily."

A Starting Heart Oath grants **+2 Insight toward your Dao Seed** at character creation — the oath crystallizes your conviction. Breaking it triggers full Heart Oath consequences (Qi Deviation, Black Karma, Face loss).

#### Starting Karma Balance

Usually 0 White / 0 Black unless your Origin specifies otherwise:

| Origin | Starting Karma Modification |
|---|---|
| **Mortal Village Youth** | Life Debt to parents (named) |
| **Fallen Noble** | −1 Black (ruined name soft-karma) |
| **Battlefield Survivor** | 1 named Blood Debt (pursuer clock inactive but can activate) |
| **Young Master / Clan Heir** | 1 inherited family enemy (Blood Debt from ancestor's action) |
| **Criminal / Outlaw** (variant) | 1 active Blood Debt + −2 Black |
| *All others* | 0 / 0 |

### Step 11: Name, Age, Appearance

#### Names

Cultivation naming follows layered conventions:

- **Birth name** — family name + personal name (e.g. Wang Lin: Wang family, Lin personal)
- **Style name / courtesy name** — taken at adulthood or on entering a sect. Often references aspirations or personal character. Elders and peers use this; juniors use "Senior Brother [Courtesy Name]."
- **Cultivation name / title** — earned later through deeds. "Master Demon" (Wang Lin's moniker), "Sword Sovereign," "Flame Empress." Titles unlock as you gain Face and reputation.

For play, pick a birth name now. Add a courtesy name if your origin grants it (Young Master typically has one; Wanderer typically does not).

#### Age

| Starting Realm | Typical Age |
|---|---|
| Mortal | 14–20 |
| Qi Condensation (recently broken through) | 16–25 |
| Foundation Establishment (experienced start) | 30–60 (cultivators age slowly) |
| Core Formation (rare PC start) | 80–200 |

Cultivators age dramatically slower after Foundation Establishment. A Core Formation cultivator in their hundreds appears to be in their thirties. At Nascent Soul, appearance becomes semi-chosen — older cultivators often deliberately look ancient to establish authority.

#### Appearance

Cultivators and mortals look the same at Qi Condensation. Foundation Establishment brings subtle signs — clearer eyes, smoother skin, a faint presence. Core Formation has unmistakable aura (controllable but noticeable when active). Nascent Soul and above radiate Presence that mortals find instinctively unsettling.

Beyond base appearance, note:
- **Distinguishing marks** — scars, heterochromia, notable hair color (white hair is a common cultivation-world trope for tragic backstories or certain bloodlines)
- **Robes / garb** — sect colors if affiliated; style signals rank; inner disciples of Heng Yue Sect wear red/white/black/purple by rank
- **Carried objects** — what's visible? A sword? A brush? A pouch of beads?

At the end of creation, your sheet should contain:

- **Name** (birth, courtesy, future title)

  - Wound Capacity
  - Qi Pool
  - Spiritual Sense Range
  - Soul Defense
  - Presence Tier
  - Lifespan

Session one typically opens with your character *becoming* a cultivator — the Qi Condensation breakthrough, a spiritual root test at a sect, or a first lesson from a wandering master. Starting stats at Mortal (Tier 0) update to Qi Condensation Early (Tier 1) in-session.

### Derived Stats Reference

Several stats derive automatically from your attributes and realms. Keep these on your sheet for quick reference.

| Derived Stat | Formula | Use |
|---|---|---|
| **Wound Capacity** | Realm Tier + Body Attribute | Wound boxes before penalties begin at 50%; fully filled = Dying |
| **Qi Pool** | Realm Tier × 10 + Qi Attribute × 5 | Fuel for techniques; full recovery at long rest (8 hours) |
| **Spiritual Sense Range** | (Realm Tier + Soul Attribute) × 10 meters | Distance at which you can detect auras/presences of realm ≤ yours |
| **Soul Defense** | Realm Tier + Soul Attribute | Target successes to resist soul attacks, mental intrusion, heart demons |
| **Presence Tier** | Realm Tier | Used when deliberately releasing aura against lower-realm targets (section 9.2.8) |
| **Initiative Base** | Realm Tier + 1d6 (rolled each combat) | See section 8.4 |
| **Max Active Heart Oaths** | (Realm Tier ÷ 2) + 1 | Section 9.3.4 |
| **Seclusion Slots per Session Gap** | Scales with realm (section 7.1 table) | How much cultivation work you can do between sessions |

**Lifespan by Realm** (when you will die of old age if you don't break through):

| Realm | Lifespan |
|---|---|
| Mortal | ~80 years |
| Qi Condensation | ~120 years |
| Foundation Establishment | ~200 years |
| Core Formation | ~500 years |
| Nascent Soul | ~1,000 years |
| Soul Formation | ~3,000 years |
| Soul Transformation | ~10,000 years |
| Ascendant | ~30,000 years |
| Nirvana Scryer+ | Effectively immortal (barring violence or tribulation) |

**Spiritual Sense nuances:**
- Detecting a cultivator *at your realm or below* is automatic within range (no roll)
- Detecting *above your realm* requires a Spiritual Sense roll; they can usually hide from you easily
- Cultivators can deliberately **mask their aura** (Stealth Practice roll) to appear lower-realm or mortal
- **Concealment formations** and some pills fully suppress aura
- Your Spiritual Sense Range counts as the radius of your "awareness bubble" — within it, you auto-notice combat actions unless deliberately hidden

## 4. Rule Lock-Ins

### Spiritual Roots: Fully Random, Rolled Publicly
The roll is witnessed by the table — spiritual root tests are public ceremonies in-world, and the shame or pride of the roll is part of the drama. No rerolls. The dice decide.

### Lethality: Dead Is Dead (Unless You Prepared)
**Core principle: HP is not the buffer — preparation is.** Taking a killing blow with no countermeasure = you die. Roll a new character.

#### Canonical Countermeasures (Must Be Prepared in Advance)

| Countermeasure | How It Works | Cost |
|---|---|---|
| **Life-Saving Talismans** | Single-use, declared when you'd die. Tiers: short-range teleport → wooden-doll substitution. | Must be crafted/purchased, must be on your person. |
| **Secret Techniques (Forbidden Arts)** | Burn cultivation base, lifespan, or blood essence to survive. | Ruinous aftermath — lifespan is *gone*. |
| **Soul Escape / Nascent Soul Flight** | Body dies, soul escapes. Find a new body (possession, spare body, etc.). | Nascent Soul realm+. Months-to-years of in-world time to recover. |
| **Blood Contracts / Sect Measures** | Sect master's protective mark. | Single-use. You now owe them enormously. |
| **Pre-placed Escape Formations** | Triggered when you return to a specific location. | Doesn't help if you die in the field. |
| **Corpse Puppets / Doppelgangers** | "You weren't really there." | Advanced, expensive, late-game. |

**Design rule:** every countermeasure must be declared and paid for *in advance*. No retroactive reveals. This is the core of the system — the game rewards paranoid preparation.

#### Qi Deviation (Separate from Death)
Non-fatal but devastating. Failed breakthroughs, pushed techniques, demonic cultivation can:
- Drop you a realm
- Lock a meridian
- Corrupt your Dao
- Permanently cripple cultivation base

Often worse than death — you keep playing the ruined character until you find a cure.

### Heavenly Bodies: Two Paths
**Rare Gift (the 20 roll):** ~1% chance at creation. Roll on Heavenly Body table instead of getting normal roots.

**Alternate Path (via play):** Heavenly Bodies can be *unlocked*, *cultivated into*, or *stolen* mid-campaign. Rare fruits, specific tribulations, awakened bloodlines. This is explicitly a plot reward. Wang Lin-style characters have a path to transcend their terrible roots.

Roll d12.

| Roll | Heavenly Body | Description |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Demonic Body** | Feeds on qi of others. Grows stronger by devouring cultivators. Hunted. |

| 7 | **Nine-Yin / Nine-Yang Body** | Extreme yin or yang. Massive cultivation speed in one element. Poisonous to yourself without a counterweight (partner or pill regimen). |

## 7. Progression & Cultivation Mechanics

### 7.1 The Session/Seclusion Rhythm

The core structural principle: **time between sessions = cultivation time.**

- At the **end of every session**, the GM declares how much in-world time will pass before the next session.
- Players **declare their seclusion action(s)** for that period — either at session end or via a between-sessions channel (Discord, chat, etc.).
- The next session opens with the results of those seclusion actions.

Time passed scales with realm and narrative needs:

| Character's Current Realm | Typical Time Between Sessions | Seclusion Slots |
|---|---|---|
| Mortal / Qi Condensation | Days to weeks | 1–2 |
| Foundation Establishment | Weeks to months | 2–3 |
| Core Formation | Months to a year | 3–4 |
| Nascent Soul | Years | 4–6 |
| Soul Formation and above | Decades | 6+ |

**Seclusion Slots** are the currency — you spend them on declared activities. Higher-realm cultivators get more slots per session gap but also need more slots per milestone, so the grind keeps pace with their power.

The GM is the sole arbiter of time passed. A session might end with "three months pass" or "seven years pass" depending on narrative needs. Party members at very different realms may get different slot counts for the same time gap (a mortal gets little from seven years of training; a Core Formation cultivator gets a lot).

### 7.2 Seclusion Actions

Each slot can be spent on one of:

| Action | Effect |
|---|---|
| **Cultivate** | Progress toward next sub-stage milestone (see 7.3). Primary slot use. |
| **Study Technique** | Master a known technique (raise its Mastery tier) or begin learning a new one. |
| **Refine Pill / Artifact** | Craft — requires the relevant Dao/skill. Produces consumables or items. |
| **Comprehend Dao** | Meditate on a Dao track. Gain 1 Insight toward a chosen Dao (2 if it's your Dao Seed). |
| **Scout / Travel / Prepare** | Narrative prep — establish caches, set formations, gather rumors, scout locations. |
| **Recover** | Heal from Qi Deviation, injuries, or tribulation backlash. Some wounds require multiple slots. |
| **Seek a Master / Tutor** | Narrative pursuit of instruction — may require multiple slots and GM pacing. |

Slots can be combined: spending 2 slots on Cultivate doubles the progress toward a milestone; spending 3 slots on Pill Refining produces a rarer pill.

### 7.3 Sub-Stage Progression (Milestone System)

Within a realm, advancement from **Early → Mid → Late → Peak** is **milestone-based**. No rolls. You meet the condition, you advance. Each sub-stage grants **+1 to your realm tier dice pool** for that cultivation base (Body/Qi/Soul).

Typical milestones (GM tailors to character):

| Transition | Example Milestones |
|---|---|
| **Early → Mid** | Spend 1–2 Cultivate slots with adequate resources; complete first full qi circulation. |
| **Mid → Late** | Defeat an opponent of equal realm; OR master a technique; OR gain meaningful Insight in Dao. |
| **Late → Peak** | Gather and refine a significant resource cache (pills, spirit stones, a spirit vein); OR survive a qi deviation; OR defeat an opponent above your realm. |
| **Peak → Next Realm** | **MAJOR BREAKTHROUGH** — triggers full Breakthrough Scene (see 7.4). |

Milestones should be **narrative, not checklist**. The GM and player collaborate: "You've been grinding through bandit cultivators for months, you've refined enough spirit stones — you hit Mid Qi Condensation on your trip back to the city." That's how it should feel.

### 7.4 Major Breakthroughs (Between Realms)

At Peak of any realm, the next step is a full **Breakthrough Scene**. This is always a significant moment — often a whole session's climax.

**Requirements to attempt:**
1. **Resources** — realm-appropriate pills, spirit stones, and ideally a suitable cultivation location (cave, spiritual vein, formation).
2. **Insight** — minimum Insight threshold in any Dao relevant to your path (scales with target realm).
3. **A breakthrough scene** — declared in-fiction, typically requiring 1+ Seclusion Slots and uninterrupted time.

**The Breakthrough Roll:**
- Dice pool = Relevant Attribute + Insight invested (burn Insight from your pool; it's spent)
- Optional: add dice for rare resources (a Foundation Pill adds 2 dice, a superior Foundation Pill adds 4)
- Target successes set by GM based on realm difficulty

**Outcomes:**

| Result | Effect |
|---|---|
| **Success (meets target)** | Advance to Early sub-stage of next realm. |
| **Great Success (doubles target)** | Advance *and* gain a permanent bonus: +1 die permanently, or a Dao Insight perk, or extended lifespan. |
| **Stalled (just misses)** | No advance, no penalty. Try again next seclusion. Resources spent anyway. |
| **Qi Deviation (significant miss)** | Drop one sub-stage, sustain injury (requires multiple Recover slots to heal). |
| **Catastrophic Deviation (botch)** | Drop a full realm, permanent damage to cultivation base unless cured by rare resource or grandmaster. Possible death. |

**At Core Formation and above: Heavenly Tribulation triggers alongside the breakthrough.**

### 7.5 Heavenly Tribulation

The Heavens test every cultivator who reaches for higher realms. Triggered at breakthrough to **Core Formation**, **Nascent Soul**, and every realm beyond.

Tribulation is a **scene, not a roll** — a set-piece encounter structured in waves.

**Standard Structure:**
- **3–9 waves** depending on realm (3 for Core Formation, scaling up)
- Each wave is a distinct threat: **lightning** (physical), **karmic fire** (burns sins), **heart demons** (mental attack drawing on your regrets and Ties), or **Dao trials** (tests specific to your comprehension)
- Each wave requires a response: withstand with Body, channel with Qi, resist with Soul, or use a prepared defense (talisman, formation, ally intervention)
- **Surviving each wave** accumulates "tribulation residue" — the power of the lightning purifies your cultivation base, granting bonuses on success
- **Failing a wave** inflicts damage proportional to the wave's tier; failing the final wave is usually fatal

**Preparation matters enormously:**
- Tribulation Formations can divert or weaken lightning
- Tribulation Pills can increase survival odds
- An ally one realm above you can intervene (but doing so draws the Heavens' attention to *them* too)
- Hiding from tribulation is nearly impossible — once you begin the breakthrough, the Heavens find you

**Success bonuses** (tribulation residue):
- Core Formation: body purification, +1 permanent die to one base
- Nascent Soul: your Nascent Soul is born — a spiritual duplicate that can survive your body's death
- Higher: setting-specific, including Dao Fruit, longevity, or physical transformation

Tribulation is **the big lethality moment** of the campaign. Every major breakthrough is a potential character death, and players who prepared will survive; players who didn't, won't. This is *Renegade Immortal* canonical — Wang Lin's tribulations nearly kill him every time.

### 7.6 Dao Comprehension (Insight Track)

Dao Insight is earned, not bought. It accumulates in specific Daos through play.

**Comprehension Levels:**

| Level | Insight Required | Effect |
|---|---|---|
| **Novice** | 5 | Minor perk tied to Dao; bypass 1 success of Realm Barrier in Dao-relevant situations |
| **Initiate** | 15 | Passive effect manifests; bypass 2 successes |
| **Adept** | 40 | Major perk; bypass 3 successes; can improvise minor Dao-effects narratively |
| **Master** | 100 | Can teach this Dao; bypass 4 successes; create personal techniques |
| **Grandmaster** | 300 | Full Realm Barrier bypass in Dao-relevant situations; your techniques in this Dao gain a tier |
| **Dao Lord** | ??? | Nirvana-tier comprehension — you have *walked* the Dao. Setting-defining. |

**Your Dao Seed gains Insight at 2× rate.** Other Daos can still be pursued but slower.

**Insight sources:**
- Seclusion: Comprehend Dao slot (1 Insight, or 2 for Seed)
- In-combat: Moments of Truth and the Insight Gambit (see 7.8)
- Narrative experiences: loss, witnessing, duels, specific events the GM calls out
- Special locations and inheritances: ancient Dao stones, enlightenment under a Dao tree, etc.

### 7.7 Technique Learning & Mastery

Every technique in your arsenal exists at one of six states. The first state (Unknown) means you have no access at all. Once you gain access, you must *learn* it (Unknown → Rough), and then *master* it (Rough → Perfected) over the course of play.

| State | Description |
|---|---|
| **Unknown** | You have no access. Cannot attempt to use. |
| **Rough** | Just learned; usable but clumsily |
| **Familiar** | Comfortable; no penalty to standard use |
| **Proficient** | Trained; −1 qi cost *or* +1 die on use |
| **Mastered** | Deep understanding; −2 qi cost *and* +1 die, *or* unlock an evolved effect |
| **Perfected** | Apotheosis; technique functions effectively as one tier higher |

#### 7.7.1 Learning a New Technique (Unknown → Rough)

Requires **access** to the technique in some form. Access comes in grades of quality:

| Source | Quality |
|---|---|
| **Complete sect manual / written by a master** | Full detail. Best-quality learning. |
| **Personal teaching by a practitioner** | Even better — teacher adapts to you. |
| **Fragmentary scroll / notes / incomplete manual** | Partial; missing nuances. |
| **Witnessed in combat, multiple uses observed** | You've seen the moves; you understand the structure. |
| **Witnessed once** | Fragmentary impression; hard to replicate accurately. |
| **Inherited memory / bloodline / technique dream** | Half-formed in your mind; variable. |

**The Learning Roll:**

1. Spend **1 Seclusion Slot** studying the technique (cannot be done in active play — this is focused attempt, not casual practice).
2. Roll **Soul + Realm Tier + modifiers** (d6 pool, 4+ = success).
3. Meet the **Target Successes** for the technique's tier:

| Technique Tier | Target Successes to Learn |
|---|---|
| Spirit | 3 |
| Mysterious | 6 |
| Celestial | 10 |
| Void | 15 |
| Nirvana | 20 |

**Learning Modifiers:**

| Condition | Modifier |
|---|---|
| Complete manual available | +2 dice |
| Teacher actively instructing (present during study) | +4 dice |
| Technique matches your Dao Seed's element/concept | +2 dice |
| Witnessed multiple times in combat | +2 dice |
| Witnessed once in combat | +1 die |
| Inheritance / bloodline match | +2 dice |
| Fragmentary source (incomplete scroll) | −2 dice |
| Your Cultivation Method is incompatible (e.g. Orthodox trying to learn Demonic) | −2 dice |
| Technique is sect-exclusive and you aren't in the sect | −2 dice (the technique itself "resists" outsiders) |

**Outcomes:**

- **Success:** technique is now at **Rough** state. Usable in play.
- **Failure:** slot wasted, no progress. You may try again next seclusion.
- **Catastrophic failure** (0 successes against a target ≥ 10): you've mis-learned something. A "ghost version" of the technique is in your muscle memory — Qi Deviation risk if you try to use it. Must be actively corrected by a proper teacher or scroll study before the real version can be attempted.

**Starting techniques** (from Step 6 of character creation) begin at Rough automatically — you already know them.

**Sect-granted techniques** (from advancing in sect rank) also begin at Rough automatically — you were taught properly.

#### 7.7.2 Mastery Points (MP) — Advancing Mastery

Once a technique is Rough, advancement happens through accumulating **Mastery Points**. MP is technique-specific; the MP you earn for Cloud Piercing Finger doesn't transfer to Iron Body.

**Mastery Thresholds (cumulative):**

| Tier | Total MP Needed |
|---|---|
| Rough → Familiar | 5 |
| Familiar → Proficient | 15 |
| Proficient → Mastered | 35 |
| Mastered → Perfected | 75 |

**Sources of MP:**

| Source | MP Gained |
|---|---|
| **Use the technique in real combat** | +1 MP per use (cap: 3 MP per combat scene, to prevent grinding) |
| **Use the technique during a Moment of Truth** (section 7.8.1) | +2 MP bonus (stacks with combat use) |
| **Use the technique in a Dao-resonant way** (Dao Seed matches effect) | +1 MP bonus |
| **Use against a higher-realm opponent** | +2 MP bonus (you learn more fighting up) |
| **Seclusion Study** (1 slot dedicated) | Roll 1d6 + Soul Attribute MP (minimum 2, max 12) |
| **Master's Teaching** (1 slot + teacher who knows it at Mastered+) | +8 MP |
| **Insight Conversion** (bank Insight from Gambit or other sources) | 1 Insight → 2 MP |
| **Use a Dao Comprehension Pill while studying** | +5 MP bonus to that seclusion's study |
| **Active Practice In-Session** (see 7.7.3) | +1 to +2 MP per practice scene |

**Advancement is automatic** when you hit a threshold — no roll required. Narratively, the GM should frame the advancement moment dramatically (the next time you use the technique after hitting the threshold, something clicks: "You've struck this finger-strike a thousand times, but this one feels different. The qi flows cleaner. Your opponent's defense crumbles like paper.").

**Why these numbers?** A disciple using a technique in 5–6 combat scenes over a campaign arc generates ~15 MP through use alone — enough to reach Proficient. Combining combat use with occasional seclusion study and a Moment of Truth or two, a dedicated technique can reach Mastered over a mid-length campaign. Perfected requires long commitment, teacher access, or significant Insight investment — reserved for signature techniques the character builds their identity around.

#### 7.7.3 Active Practice In-Session

Between combats, during downtime scenes, or as a declared slow-scene activity, a PC can perform **Active Practice** — focused training on a specific technique.

**Requires:** a scene's worth of in-fiction time (a morning of training, an afternoon in the practice yard, an evening alone with your sword), not a full seclusion slot.

**Resolution:**
1. Declare which technique you're practicing.
2. Roll **Soul + Realm Tier** (d6, 4+ successes).
3. Target: **3 successes**.

**Outcomes:**

- **0–2 successes:** nothing gained; the practice was sloppy or you weren't in the right headspace
- **3–5 successes (meet target):** +1 MP
- **6+ successes (exceed target):** +2 MP and you recover some qi (25% of max) as meditation
- **Exceptional (2× target — 6+ with target 3):** +3 MP and a brief flash of Insight — gain 1 banked Insight toward the technique's associated Dao

**Limit:** maximum 2 Active Practices per in-fiction day. Practice beyond that yields diminishing returns (the mind needs rest between focused sessions).

**Design note:** this mechanizes the "I spent the afternoon training" beat that cultivation fiction uses constantly without asking for a full seclusion commitment. It's the mid-session equivalent of downtime crafting.

#### 7.7.4 Evolving Techniques

As before (section 7.7 previous version), a **Perfected** technique can sometimes *evolve* into a higher-tier version. This is rare, GM-arbitrated, and usually requires matching Dao comprehension.

Evolution requirements:
- Technique is Perfected
- You have at least **Adept** comprehension in a Dao relevant to the technique
- You experience a significant Moment of Truth or Dao-resonant breakthrough while using the technique
- You spend **1 Seclusion Slot** synthesizing the evolution

The evolved technique takes the place of the original on your sheet (or becomes a new entry derived from it). A Spirit-tier Cloud Piercing Finger evolved by a Sword Dao Adept becomes a Mysterious-tier *Cloud Shattering Finger*. The new technique starts at **Rough** in its new tier — you must master it all over again, but from a higher baseline effect.

### 7.7.5 Cultivation Method Progression

Cultivation Methods are foundational — you don't "practice" your way into a new one the way you do techniques. Changing methods is a major event with real stakes.

#### Acquiring a New Method

New methods come from rare and specific sources:

| Source | How It Happens |
|---|---|
| **Sect Promotion** | Advancing from Outer to Inner Disciple (or similar tier jump) grants the sect's better method as a reward |
| **Inheritance** | Found in a tomb, ancestral cave, or gifted by a dying master |
| **Trade / Purchase** | Very rare — methods are usually sect-secret; black-market methods exist at high cost |
| **Self-Composition** | Soul Formation or higher cultivators can synthesize their own methods (see below) |
| **Method Refinement** | Your *current* method can be advanced past its Peak Realm if you've walked it far enough (see below) |

#### Integrating a New Method

Swapping your cultivation method is not like changing clothes. The method shapes how your qi flows; introducing a new framework can cause qi deviation or worse.

**Integration Procedure:**

1. Acquire the new method (plot event — never a simple seclusion slot)
2. Dedicate **2–4 Seclusion Slots** to integration (higher-tier methods require more)
3. Roll **Soul + Realm Tier + modifiers** (d6 pool, 4+ successes)
4. Target: **3 × the new method's Peak Realm Tier** (a method with Peak Realm at Ascendant is Tier 25+; target = 75 for the whole Peak Realm, but usually you're integrating one stage at a time; GM calibrates the target to your current realm)

**Modifiers:**

| Condition | Modifier |
|---|---|
| New method matches your Spiritual Root element(s) | +3 dice |
| Your old method was incomplete or fragmentary | +2 dice (easier to replace a broken thing) |
| You have a teacher guiding the integration | +2 dice |
| You've reached your old method's Peak Realm (natural upgrade point) | +3 dice |
| New method is radically different (Orthodox → Demonic) | −3 dice |
| Integrating during a tribulation or crisis | −5 dice (extremely dangerous) |

**Outcomes:**

- **Full Success** (meet target): method is integrated cleanly. Previous method's bonuses cease, new method's bonuses take effect. You may continue cultivating normally.
- **Partial Success** (0–2 short of target): integrated but rough. You suffer **1 sub-stage of Qi Deviation** (drop one sub-stage; recovers over 1–3 Seclusion Slots in Recovery actions).
- **Failure** (3+ short of target): severe Qi Deviation. Drop a full realm. The integration failed; you can abandon the new method and revert (losing the acquired manual to the failure) or try again after Recovery.
- **Catastrophic Failure** (0 successes against high target): cultivation base shatters. Drop multiple realms; may require rare pills or Grandmaster intervention to heal. Possible permanent damage. The worst case in the entire framework.

This is why cultivation novels treat method-swapping as a fraught, high-stakes event. A Foundation Establishment cultivator receiving a great-ancestor's Celestial-tier method shouldn't integrate it casually — they should prepare with pills, formations, allies, and ideally wait until they're at their current method's Peak Realm.

#### Refining Your Current Method

A cultivator who sticks with their original method can eventually **refine it** — improving it past its Peak Realm cap. This is how Wang Lin makes his incomplete family scroll workable at realms it was never designed for.

**Requirements:**
- You must be at the current Peak Realm of your method (the cap) for at least one full seclusion (you've exhausted what it can teach)
- Soul Formation realm or higher (only at this level do you have Dao comprehension sufficient to *edit* cultivation fundamentals)
- **At least Initiate-level Dao comprehension** in a Dao relevant to your method

**Procedure:**
1. Spend **4 Seclusion Slots** of dedicated synthesis
2. Bank **10 Insight from your Dao** into the method (the Insight is consumed; it's no longer available for Dao advancement)
3. Roll **Soul + Realm + Dao Level** (d6, 4+ = success)
4. Target: **10 successes**

**Success:** your method's Peak Realm advances by one tier (e.g., from "Foundation Establishment peak" to "Core Formation peak"). The method now bears your imprint — it's a fusion of the original and your Dao understanding.

**Failure:** the Insight is consumed, the slots are spent, but the method didn't change. You can try again after gathering more Insight.

**Design note:** refinement is slow and expensive by design. A cultivator who refines their method all the way to the top is doing something equivalent to "writing a legendary cultivation manual" in the novels — it's a defining accomplishment of a lifetime. Most characters won't do this; they'll accept the upper-realm limits of their methods and swap at breakthroughs.

#### Self-Composed Methods (Soul Formation+)

At Soul Formation and above, a cultivator with enough Dao Insight and accumulated experience can **compose their own method from scratch**. This is essentially the same as refining a method to its highest possible form, but starting from blank parchment.

- Requires Soul Formation or above
- Requires **Master-level comprehension** in at least one Dao (usually your primary Dao Seed)
- Takes **8–12 Seclusion Slots** over multiple sessions
- Banks **30+ Insight** from various Daos
- Results in a method uniquely suited to the creator — often not compatible with any other cultivator (inheritance difficulty)

Self-composed methods are canonical — they're how sects found their cultivation lineages. A PC who creates one has the option to *found a sect* around it, or to pass it only to a chosen disciple.

### 7.8 In-Combat Insight

Combat is one of the primary sources of enlightenment in cultivation fiction. Two mechanics handle this.

#### 7.8.1 Moments of Truth (Passive)

Automatic Insight triggers. When any of these occur in combat, the player gains **1 Insight point** (banked at scene's end into a chosen Dao or Technique):

- **Survived Above** — you survive a fight against an opponent at least one full realm above you
- **Brink of Death** — you use a life-saving countermeasure (talisman, forbidden art, etc.)
- **Witness a Master** — you see a master of a Dao you pursue use that Dao in action
- **First Blood** — first time you use a newly learned technique in real combat
- **Dao-Resonant Kill** — kill an opponent in a way that resonates with your Dao (Slaughter Dao kills anyone; Sword Dao kills by blade; Karma Dao kills someone who wronged you; etc.)

Multiple triggers in one fight stack. A single fight could yield 2–3 Insight automatically if the conditions are dramatic enough.

#### 7.8.2 The Insight Gambit (Active, Risky)

Once per combat scene, before rolling an action, a player may declare an **Insight Gambit**:

1. Declare the target of the insight — a specific Dao, or a specific Technique you're using.
2. **Split your dice pool:** choose how many dice go to the action and how many go to insight.
3. Roll the action pool first — resolve combat normally.
4. Roll the insight pool. Each success = 1 Insight point *if* the GM judges the moment meaningful.

**Meaningful moments include:**
- Fighting an opponent of higher realm
- Using a technique in a new or creative way
- At or below your last line of defense (no countermeasures remaining)
- Emotional weight — a rival, vow, loved one in danger, a long-sought enemy
- Witnessing or countering a superior Dao

**If the moment is not meaningful**, the insight dice score nothing — you burned attack dice for no reason. This is the gamble.

**Design intent:** the Gambit mechanizes the classic cultivation-novel moment of *"half my mind fought, the other half sought the Dao."* It should feel risky and exciting. Players who chase insight in every fight will get killed; players who pick their moments will grow.

#### 7.8.3 Realm Differential for Insight

Both Moments of Truth and the Insight Gambit scale with the relative realm of the opponent. **This prevents enlightenment farming against weaker foes.**

| Opponent's Realm (vs yours) | Insight Modifier |
|---|---|
| 2+ realms below | None gained |
| 1 realm below | None from defeating, but First Blood / Witness / self-driven triggers still apply |
| Same realm (±3 sub-stages) | Standard (×1) |
| 1 realm above | ×2 |
| 2 realms above | ×3 |
| 3+ realms above | ×4 (if you survive) |

**Design note:** a Core Formation cultivator who spars with a willing Nascent Soul ancestor gains vastly more Insight than one who slaughters Qi Condensation bandits. In-world, sparring with higher-realm mentors is the single most efficient cultivation accelerator — provided the mentor doesn't kill you or grow tired of you.

**Sparring vs. Real Combat:** sparring (explicit mutual consent, non-lethal) grants full Insight scaling, but certain Moments of Truth don't trigger — no Brink of Death (you're not actually dying), no Dao-Resonant Kill. Witness a Master, First Blood, and the Insight Gambit still work fully. A dedicated sparring session can be declared as a Seclusion Action (Seek a Master), and a good master will allow it regularly.

### 7.9 Attribute Advancement

Attribute scores (Body, Qi, Soul) do **not** rise automatically when you breakthrough a realm. Your cultivation *base* advances with realm; your cultivation *capacity within a base* grows through deliberate focus. A lazy Core Formation cultivator has the same Body 2 they started with as a Qi Condensation youth. A dedicated body cultivator of the same realm might have Body 5.

This is why the novels show such dramatic differences between cultivators of the same realm: they've trained different things for different lengths of time.

#### How Attribute Scores Rise

Each attribute has its own independent **Attribute Points (AP)** pool, separate from Mastery Points or Dao Insight. When AP reaches a threshold, the score advances.

| Score Advancement | AP Required |
|---|---|
| 1 → 2 | 10 AP |
| 2 → 3 | 25 AP |
| 3 → 4 | 60 AP |
| 4 → 5 | 150 AP |

**Total to go from 1 to 5: 245 AP** — a genuinely long journey, reserved for cultivators who specialize. Reaching Attribute 5 in any stat is a defining accomplishment.

#### Sources of AP

**Body AP:**

| Source | AP Gained |
|---|---|
| Survive a combat where you took significant damage (>50% wounds) | +1 |
| Survive a killing blow via Body resistance alone (no countermeasure used) | +3 |
| Win a physical combat against a higher-realm opponent | +3 |
| Seclusion: Body Refinement training (1 slot) | +1d6 AP (min 2) |
| Consume a Body-refining pill or bathe in a Body spring | +5 to +15 depending on quality |
| Body Refinement Cultivation Method in use | +1 bonus to all Body AP earned |

**Qi AP:**

| Source | AP Gained |
|---|---|
| Deplete your qi pool fully in a meaningful use (not grinding) | +1 per combat |
| Survive a tribulation wave through qi channeling | +3 |
| Achieve a Moment of Truth using a Qi-based technique (section 7.8.1) | +2 |
| Seclusion: Qi Refinement meditation (1 slot) | +1d6 AP (min 2) |
| Meditate near a spiritual vein during a session gap | +3 bonus to seclusion study |
| Consume a Qi-refining pill | +3 to +10 depending on quality |

**Soul AP:**

| Source | AP Gained |
|---|---|
| Detect a hidden threat via Spiritual Sense (successful sense roll) | +1 |
| Survive a Soul attack or heart demon | +3 |
| Perform a significant crafting success (Superior quality or better) | +2 |
| Comprehend a Dao Insight threshold | +2 |
| Seclusion: Soul meditation / scripture study (1 slot) | +1d6 AP (min 2) |
| Consume a Soul-nourishing pill | +3 to +10 |

#### Per-Session Caps

To prevent grinding, each attribute can gain at most **5 AP per session** from in-play sources (combat, moments, discoveries). Seclusion AP is uncapped but costs slots you could have spent elsewhere. Consumables (pills, springs) bypass the cap entirely — they're rare resources, not spammable.

#### The Specialization Trade-off

Advancing attributes is expensive in seclusion slots. A cultivator grinding Body from 2 → 3 (25 AP) needs roughly 5 dedicated seclusion slots of Body Refinement *plus* in-play AP from physical combat. Those slots could have gone to cultivation milestones, crafting, Dao comprehension, or technique mastery.

**This is the core tradeoff:** you cannot max everything. A cultivator who is Foundation Establishment with Body 4 / Qi 4 / Soul 1 spent their seclusion years hitting the gym and the practice yard instead of the library. They will crush most peers in combat and be useless in a crafting hall.

#### Cultivation Method Interactions

Certain methods accelerate specific AP:
- **Body Refinement Methods** — grant +1 bonus to all Body AP earned; cap AP from Qi sources at 75% efficiency
- **Orthodox Sect Methods** — no bonus, no penalty; balanced
- **Demonic Methods** — +1 bonus to any AP from combat; but each seclusion using the method generates 1 Demonic Heart
- **Soul-focused Methods** (rare) — +1 bonus to Soul AP; penalty to Body

This means your **Method choice partially determines your ceiling**. A pure Body Refinement practitioner can reach Body 5 much faster than a standard cultivator can — but will struggle to push Qi beyond 3.

#### Attribute Decay

Attributes **do not decay** under normal circumstances. A Body 4 cultivator remains Body 4 even if they stop training. Exception: **Qi Deviation from a failed breakthrough** can inflict attribute loss — catastrophic qi deviation may reduce one attribute by 1 permanently, a form of lasting damage that requires rare pills or grandmaster intervention to heal.

#### Realm Advancement vs. Attribute Advancement

These are independent. A cultivator can:
- Advance realm without touching attributes (rare — means they relied entirely on resources and tribulation luck)
- Advance attributes without changing realm (common — Foundation Establishment cultivators often spend decades grinding attributes before attempting Core Formation)
- Advance both in parallel (typical for PCs during active play)

When planning a character's trajectory, think about which attribute you want to push toward 5 by campaign's end. That's your identity — Wang Lin is a high-Soul cultivator with balanced Body; a sword prodigy might max Body and Qi; a crafter maxes Soul above all.

### 7.10 Quick Reference: The Progression Loop

```
SESSION PLAY
    ↓
Combat & Narrative → Insight earned (Moments of Truth, Gambit, events)
    ↓
SESSION END → GM declares time passed
    ↓
Players declare Seclusion Actions (spend slots)
    ↓
Seclusion resolves → Milestones hit, Insight comprehended, resources gained
    ↓
At Peak of realm → Major Breakthrough Scene
    ↓
Core Formation+ → Heavenly Tribulation
    ↓
NEXT SESSION
```

## 8. Combat & Dice Mechanics

### 8.1 Design Philosophy

Cultivation combat is **short, decisive, and resource-driven.** Novels don't depict 20-round attrition fights — they depict a few charged exchanges where technique, preparation, and a single superior strike decide the outcome. The mechanics should reflect this:

- Most fights between peers resolve in **3–5 rounds**
- Cross-realm fights often end in **1–2 rounds**
- Resources (qi, countermeasures, prepared formations) matter more than raw HP
- A single superior technique or Dao insight can flip an unwinnable fight
- Preparation before the fight > decisions during the fight

### 8.2 The Core Roll

**Dice pool = Relevant Attribute + Realm Tier (for the relevant base) + Modifiers.**

Roll d6s, count each die showing **4+** as a success. Compare successes to a target number or an opposing roll.

**Modifiers include:**
- Technique bonus (+X dice if a technique is used)
- Mastery tier of the technique (higher mastery = more dice or effects)
- Equipment (spirit weapons, artifacts)
- Situational (surprise, wounded, favorable terrain)
- Wound penalties (see 8.7)

### 8.3 Realms & Tier Reference

The realm structure follows *Renegade Immortal* (仙逆) canon, capped at **Nirvana Shatterer** (peak of the Second Step) for this framework. Each realm has four mechanical sub-stages — **Early / Mid / Late / Peak**. In the novels, early realms like Qi Condensation canonically have up to 15 narrative levels — these can be flavor-mapped into our four sub-stages (e.g. novel levels 1–3 = Early, 4–7 = Mid, 8–11 = Late, 12–15 = Peak).

The realms divide into two Steps:

- **First Step — "Cultivating"** (Tiers 1–28). Mortal refinement. Body, Qi, and Soul develop in parallel. Most mortal-world play happens here.
- **Second Step — "Cultivating Dao"** (Tiers 29–40). Celestial realms. Transcending the mortal frame. The bridge between raw energy and cosmic law.

*(Third Step and beyond — the Arcane Tribulations, Essence realms, and the Fourth Step "Heaven Trampling" — are beyond this framework's scope. Campaign cap: Nirvana Shatterer.)*

#### 8.3.1 Tier Table

| Realm | Step | E / M / L / P Tier |
|---|---|---|
| **Mortal** | — | 0 |
| **Qi Condensation** | First | 1 / 2 / 3 / 4 |
| **Foundation Establishment** | First | 5 / 6 / 7 / 8 |
| **Core Formation** | First | 9 / 10 / 11 / 12 |
| **Nascent Soul** | First | 13 / 14 / 15 / 16 |
| **Soul Formation** | First | 17 / 18 / 19 / 20 |
| **Soul Transformation** | First | 21 / 22 / 23 / 24 |
| **Ascendant** | First (Peak) | 25 / 26 / 27 / 28 |
| **Nirvana Scryer** | Second | 29 / 30 / 31 / 32 |
| **Nirvana Cleanser** | Second | 33 / 34 / 35 / 36 |
| **Nirvana Shatterer** | Second (Peak) | 37 / 38 / 39 / 40 |

For Realm Barrier purposes, "one full realm above" means **4 sub-stages higher.** Within the same realm, sub-stage differences matter but don't trigger Barrier effects.

#### 8.3.2 Realm Descriptions & Mechanical Gates

**Mortal (Tier 0).** No cultivation. Lifespan ~80 years. Susceptible to cultivator Presence. Cannot wield spirit weapons meaningfully.

**Qi Condensation (T1–4).** The practitioner senses and circulates spiritual energy through their meridians. Lifespan ~120 years. Can use Spirit-tier techniques. Can wield simple mortal-forged weapons effectively. *Starting realm for most PCs after their first cultivation method is learned.*

**Foundation Establishment (T5–8).** Spiritual energy liquefies within the body; the cultivator's foundation is set. Lifespan ~200 years. Can fly short distances on spirit tools (sword, talisman, cloud). Can wield basic spirit artifacts. **Key gate:** a cracked or weak foundation here often prevents advancement to Core Formation. *Breakthrough requires a Foundation Establishment Pill or equivalent resource.*

**Core Formation (T9–12).** The liquefied qi compresses into a **Golden Core** in the dantian. Lifespan ~500 years. **MAJOR GATE: this is the first realm where the cultivator can wield proper magical treasures** (see Treasures section, forthcoming). Also the first realm where **Heavenly Tribulation** triggers at breakthrough. Cultivators here can begin serious crafting (alchemy, formations, artifact refining).

**Nascent Soul (T13–16).** A spiritual duplicate — the Nascent Soul — forms within the dantian. Lifespan ~1000 years. **Second life:** if the physical body dies, the Nascent Soul can flee and seek a new body. This directly unlocks the **Soul Escape countermeasure** (section 4). Nascent Soul cultivators can project Presence strongly enough to paralyze Foundation Establishment characters. Tribulation at breakthrough is significantly more dangerous.

**Soul Formation (T17–20).** **HARD GATE: the cultivator must have comprehended a personal Domain** — a Dao of at least Initiate level — to attempt this breakthrough. Without it, the breakthrough automatically fails catastrophically. Lifespan ~3,000 years. The soul begins to integrate with Dao; insights become more potent. Soul Formation cultivators are typically sect elders and regional powers.

**Soul Transformation (T21–24).** Body and soul begin merging into a divine form. Canonically, cultivators at this threshold often live *a mortal life* for enlightenment — experiencing mundane joys and sorrows they've transcended (Wang Lin spends decades as a wood carver to break through). Lifespan ~10,000 years. **Mechanical note:** the Soul Transformation breakthrough may require a narrative "mortal arc" — a seclusion-long stretch of campaign played as a mundane life — before it can succeed.

**Ascendant (T25–28).** The peak of the First Step. Origin soul *becomes* Domain; there is no longer distinction between the two. Lifespan ~30,000 years. The **Celestial Flame Trial** (a tribulation variant) occurs at this breakthrough — the cultivator is burned away to immortal essence or destroyed entirely. Ascendant cultivators can ignore most material concerns and often leave mortal planets for celestial realms.

**Nirvana Scryer (T29–32).** Entry to the Second Step. The cultivator begins to perceive the illusory nature of form and the real structure of Dao behind it. Celestial realm existence. Most mortals cannot comprehend or properly perceive a Nirvana Scryer.

**Nirvana Cleanser (T33–36).** Cleansing the remnants of mortal self, burning away impurities of thought and attachment. Dao manipulation becomes natural.

**Nirvana Shatterer (T37–40).** Shattering limits imposed by the Heavens themselves. The peak of the Second Step and the campaign cap of this framework. A Nirvana Shatterer is one of the most powerful beings any First-Step mortal world has ever known. *Beyond this lies the Third Step and its Arcane Tribulations — outside the scope of this framework, but a narrative horizon to hint at.*

#### 8.3.3 Heavenly Tribulation Triggers

Tribulation triggers at the breakthrough **into** each of the following realms:

| Breakthrough Into | Tribulation Severity | Special Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Core Formation | 3 waves | First tribulation — baseline |
| Nascent Soul | 5 waves | Heart demons appear in wave 3+ |
| Soul Formation | 6 waves | Dao trials present; Domain-tested |
| Soul Transformation | 7 waves | Requires prior "mortal arc" narratively |
| Ascendant | **Celestial Flame Trial** (special) | Body-soul fusion trial; burns away impurities |
| Nirvana Scryer | 7 waves | Perception-based trials (illusion vs. reality) |
| Nirvana Cleanser | 8 waves | Karmic fire particularly intense |
| Nirvana Shatterer | 9 waves | Approaching the Ancient Thunder Tribulation of the Third Step |

Karma modifies these: +10 White reduces by 1 wave; −10 Black adds a wave and amplifies heart demons.

### 8.4 Action Economy & Initiative

**Each round, every combatant gets:**
- **1 Action** — an attack, a technique, a major move, a breakthrough attempt, etc.
- **1 Reaction** — used on another's turn (defense, counter, intervention). Refreshes at the start of your next turn.
- **Free movement** — narrative positioning, short repositioning. Longer movement may require an Action.

**Initiative (per round):** roll 1d6 + highest of Body or Qi realm tier. Speed-related techniques add dice or bonuses. Ties resolved by higher Body tier, then higher Qi tier.

For extended combats, some GMs may prefer rolling initiative once and keeping the order; others re-roll each round for dynamism. Either works.

### 8.5 Attack & Defense Resolution

**Standard attack:**

1. **Attacker rolls** attack pool (Attribute + Realm Tier + technique + modifiers) → gets A successes
2. **Defender rolls** defense pool (Body or Qi attribute + Realm Tier + technique + modifiers) → gets D successes
3. **Net successes** = A − D (minimum 0)
4. **Realm Barrier** applies if the defender is a realm above the attacker (see 8.6)
5. **Remaining net successes** = wounds dealt, multiplied by technique tier (see 8.7)

**Defensive choices** (declared in response to an attack, costs your Reaction):
- **Dodge** — default, uses Body attribute + Realm
- **Parry/Block** — with a weapon or shield, uses Body + weapon bonus
- **Qi Shield** — spend qi to activate a defensive technique, uses Qi attribute + technique dice
- **Counter** — spend your next Action now to make an attack in response (your defense is halved this round; opponent's response to your counter cannot be countered again)
- **Brace** — no roll, just take the hit. No Reaction spent. Used when out of options.

**If you don't spend a Reaction**, you roll 0 defense dice. Attacks from surprise bypass defense entirely.

### 8.6 The Realm Barrier in Practice

When the defender is at a higher realm than the attacker:

> **Realm Barrier: the defender automatically cancels a number of attacker's net successes equal to (Defender's Realm Tier − Attacker's Realm Tier), to a maximum of 4 per full realm above.**

This applies *after* the defense roll, reducing remaining damage successes.

**Example:** A Qi Condensation Peak cultivator (Tier 4) attacks a Foundation Establishment Early cultivator (Tier 5). Gap = 1. The FE defender auto-cancels 1 of the attacker's net successes.

**Example:** A Qi Condensation Peak cultivator (Tier 4) attacks a Core Formation Early cultivator (Tier 9). Gap = 5. But Realm Barrier caps at 4 per *full realm above*, and CF is exactly 1 full realm above FE, 2 full realms above QC. So: 2 realms × 4 = 8 successes canceled. For a QC cultivator generating ~3–4 successes, this is an unwinnable attack without technique or Dao bypass.

**Bypassing the Realm Barrier:**
- **Higher-tier techniques** reduce the Barrier by their tier difference. A Mysterious-tier technique vs. a Spirit-tier defender's realm ignores 1 realm of Barrier. A Celestial-tier technique ignores 2 realms.
- **Dao Comprehension** reduces the Barrier by your comprehension level (Novice -1, Initiate -2, Adept -3, Master -4, Grandmaster full bypass)
- **Heavenly Resentment** (for low-root characters) can be spent 1-for-1 to cancel 1 Barrier success once per scene
- **Spirit treasures** and artifacts may grant bypass
- **The Insight Gambit**, if successful, can retroactively reduce Barrier by 1 success (once per fight)

This is the core design of the system: **weaker cultivators can beat stronger ones, but only if they have the right tool for the job.**

### 8.7 Wounds & Damage

**Wound Capacity = Realm Tier + Body Attribute.**
- A Mortal with Body 2 has 2 wound boxes.
- A Qi Condensation Peak with Body 3 has 7 wound boxes.
- A Core Formation Peak with Body 5 has 17 wound boxes.

**Damage per technique tier (per remaining net success):**

| Technique Tier | Wounds per Success |
|---|---|
| Spirit | 1 |
| Mysterious | 2 |
| Celestial | 3 |
| Void | 5 |
| Nirvana | 8 |

**Wound Penalties:**

| Wound Boxes Filled | Penalty |
|---|---|
| 0 – 50% | No penalty |
| 51 – 75% | −1 die to all rolls |
| 76 – 99% | −2 dice to all rolls |
| 100% (all boxes filled) | **Dying** — incapacitated; next damage = fatal without a countermeasure |

**Death** occurs when a character at Dying status takes any further damage *and* has no countermeasure available.

**Healing:** Recover slots in seclusion heal 1 wound per slot (more with medicinal pills). In-combat healing is rare and expensive — dedicated medicine-Dao cultivators or consumable pills only.

### 8.8 Qi Pool & Technique Costs

**Qi Pool = Realm Tier × 10 + Qi Attribute × 5.**

A Qi Condensation Peak (Tier 4) with Qi 2 has 50 qi. A Core Formation Peak (Tier 12) with Qi 4 has 140 qi.

**Technique Costs by Tier:**

| Technique Tier | Qi Cost |
|---|---|
| Spirit | 1 |
| Mysterious | 5 |
| Celestial | 20 |
| Void | 75 |
| Nirvana | 300 |

Mastery tiers reduce these costs: Proficient −20%, Mastered −40%, Perfected −60%.

**Qi Recovery:**
- Short rest (10+ minutes meditation): 25% of max
- Long rest (8 hours): 100%
- Seclusion is always full qi

**Running out of qi** means you can only perform physical strikes (Body-based) with no technique support. Pure body cultivators are less affected by this but are still limited.

### 8.9 Technique Effects (Examples)

Techniques are the flavor of combat. Each has:
- **Name**
- **Tier** (Mortal → Divine)
- **Type** (Attack / Defense / Movement / Utility)
- **Cost** (qi)
- **Effect** (dice modifier, special rule, damage type)

**Example — Cloud Piercing Finger** (Spirit, Attack, 1 qi)
- +2 dice to a single ranged attack
- Range: 10 meters
- Mastery: Proficient (range +30m), Mastered (can fire 2 per action at −1 die each), Perfected (evolves to Mysterious-tier *Cloud Shattering Finger*)

**Example — Iron Body Technique** (Mysterious, Defense/Passive, 5 qi to activate for a scene)
- While active, +2 dice to all physical defense rolls
- Reduce damage from Spirit-tier attacks by 1 per success
- Mastery unlocks scaling vs. Mysterious-tier and higher

**Example — Heart-Piercing Sword** (Celestial, Attack, 20 qi)
- +5 dice to a sword attack
- If the attack hits, deals +1 wound per net success on top of tier damage
- Bypasses 1 realm of Realm Barrier
- Mastery at Perfected: becomes a Void-tier technique

**Example — Soul Pierce** (Mysterious, Attack, 5 qi)
- Uses Soul attribute + Realm instead of Body/Qi
- Damages the Soul directly — ignores physical armor/wounds, fills a separate Soul Wound track
- Highly effective vs. Nascent Soul+ enemies (who rely on their Nascent Soul for soul-escape countermeasures)

### 8.10 Countermeasures in Combat

When a character would take damage that fills the Dying box *and* an additional wound (the killing blow):

1. GM announces: *"This wound will kill you."*
2. Player may declare a countermeasure if they have one prepared:
   - **Life-saving Talisman** → activates its effect (teleport, substitute, etc.)
   - **Forbidden Art** → burn lifespan / cultivation base / blood essence as per the art
   - **Nascent Soul Escape** (Nascent Soul+ only) → body dies, soul flees
   - **Sect Protection Mark** → one-use intervention
   - **Pre-placed Formation trigger** → only if you can reach the formation
3. Countermeasures resolve, damage is averted (or the character's body dies but soul survives, etc.)
4. If no countermeasure is available: **the character dies.** Roll up a new one.

Countermeasures are **declared only at the moment of death**, but they must have been acquired, prepared, and be on your person *before* the combat. The GM should track which countermeasures each player has available — no retroactive "I had one after all."

### 8.11 Worked Example: A Duel

**The setup:** Wang Lin (Qi Condensation Late, Tier 3; Body 2, Qi 1, Soul 2) faces rival disciple Zhao Fei (Qi Condensation Peak, Tier 4; Body 2, Qi 2, Soul 1). Both are sect disciples in a sanctioned duel.

**Wound boxes:**
- Wang Lin: Tier 3 + Body 2 = 5 boxes
- Zhao Fei: Tier 4 + Body 2 = 6 boxes

**Qi pools:**
- Wang Lin: 3×10 + 1×5 = 35 qi
- Zhao Fei: 4×10 + 2×5 = 50 qi

**Initiative:** Both roll 1d6 + highest realm tier.
- Wang Lin: 4 + 3 = 7
- Zhao Fei: 5 + 4 = 9 — Zhao Fei goes first.

**Round 1:**

Zhao Fei activates **Flame Palm** (Spirit-tier attack, 1 qi). Rolls Body 2 + Tier 4 + Technique +2 = **8 dice**. Gets 4 successes.

Wang Lin defends with **Concealment Step** (Spirit-tier evasion, 1 qi). Rolls Body 2 + Tier 3 + Technique +2 = **7 dice**. Gets 3 successes.

Net successes against Wang Lin: 4 − 3 = **1**. Realm Barrier: none (same realm). Damage: 1 × Spirit tier (1 wound per success) = **1 wound to Wang Lin.** (1/5 boxes filled — no penalty yet.)

Wang Lin's turn. He declares an **Insight Gambit**, reaching for Sword Dao understanding with 2 dice split off. His main attack: **Cloud Piercing Finger** (Spirit, 1 qi). He throws Body 2 + Tier 3 + Technique +2 − 2 (gambit) = **5 dice**. Gets 2 successes.

Zhao Fei defends without a technique (saving qi) — Body 2 + Tier 4 = **6 dice**. Gets 3 successes.

Net: 2 − 3 = 0. **Wang Lin's attack is fully defended.** He does no damage.

GM judges the Gambit *meaningful* — a rival duel fits — and Wang Lin rolls his 2 insight dice. He gets 1 success. **+1 Insight toward Sword Dao.** (His Gambit worked, even though the attack failed.)

**Round 2:**

Zhao Fei is getting confident. He uses **Heart-Piercing Palm** (Mysterious-tier attack, 5 qi). Body 2 + Tier 4 + Technique +4 = **10 dice**. Gets 5 successes.

Wang Lin is low on options. He uses his **Heavenly Resentment** (1 point from his pool) to cancel 1 success outright, then rolls defense with Concealment Step again: **7 dice**, gets 3 successes.

Net: 5 − 1 (Resentment) − 3 (defense) = **1 success remaining.** Damage: 1 × Mysterious tier (2 wounds) = **2 wounds to Wang Lin.** (3/5 boxes filled — now at 60%, so −1 die to all rolls next round.)

**Round 3:**

Wang Lin knows he's losing. He commits. **Insight Gambit again** — but this time he goes hard, splitting 3 dice off. He uses Cloud Piercing Finger: Body 2 + Tier 3 + Technique +2 − 1 (wound) − 3 (gambit) = **3 dice**. Gets 2 successes.

Zhao Fei defends casually — 6 dice, gets 3 successes. Wang Lin's attack fails.

**But** Zhao Fei doesn't know Wang Lin has been saving a trick. GM asks if Wang Lin wants to play anything — Wang Lin declares he'd been studying his incomplete family scroll, and his 3 Gambit dice actually score a Dao-resonant moment (he chose Solitude Dao — a weird Dao for a young disciple). GM rules this is a powerful moment: Wang Lin has been *alone* in this duel, no sect backing, no master to help. He rolls 3 dice and gets 2 successes. **+2 Insight toward Solitude Dao.** Not enough to change this fight, but banked for later.

Zhao Fei's turn: Heart-Piercing Palm again, 5 qi spent. 10 dice, 5 successes.

Wang Lin, at −1 die, defends with 6 dice instead of 7. Gets 2 successes.

Net: 5 − 2 = 3 successes. Damage: 3 × Mysterious tier = **6 wounds.** Wang Lin had 2 wounds left (3/5) — 6 damage fills him past Dying.

**GM: "This would kill you."**

Wang Lin's player declares a **Life-Saving Talisman** he bought at the market last session. It teleports him 50 meters. The talisman shatters. He loses the duel by default (he fled the ring) but he survives, bleeding, beaten, with 3 Insight in Sword Dao, 2 in Solitude Dao, and a burning reason to come back stronger.

**This is the system working as intended.** He lost. But he survived because he prepared, he gained Insight through risk, and he has a story to tell.

### 8.12 Abstraction at High Realms

Dice pools can get large at Core Formation+. To keep play fast:

- **Pools over 20:** roll half the dice and count each success as 2.
- **Pools over 40:** GM may use a flat "average successes" estimate (50% of dice rounded down) rather than rolling, with the player rolling only if they want to gamble variance.

Techniques with many dice modifiers can be pre-computed per character.

## 9. Karma & Face

### 9.1 Overview: Two Social Metaphysics

Cultivation worlds have two separate systems for tracking "who you are" to others:

- **Face (面子, miànzi)** is *social*. It's how other humans perceive you — your reputation, your weight, your dignity in a given context. It's negotiable, contextual, and can be lost in a single humiliating moment.
- **Karma (因果, yīnguǒ — "cause and effect")** is *cosmic*. The Heavens record every significant deed. Karma is impersonal, inescapable, and eventually demands resolution. You can hide from people; you cannot hide from Karma.

Both systems pull on each other (high Face means more witnesses to your deeds, which accelerates Karma), but they resolve through different mechanics.

---

### 9.2 Face: Your Social Weight

#### 9.2.1 What Face Is

Face is the weight your presence carries in a social context — reputation, standing, the deference you command. The more Face you have, the more people trust your word, defer to your judgment, grant you access, and hesitate to oppose you.

Face is **not charisma or personal magnetism** — it's *standing*. A powerful, ugly, unpleasant sect elder has enormous Face. A kind, brilliant, loved village doctor has very little. Face is earned through visible achievement and position, not through personality.

Face is also **contextual**. You have separate Face ratings in each meaningful context: your sect, your home city, a specific faction, a geographic region, or the cultivator world at large. A Face 8 in your sect may be Face 1 in a foreign empire where no one has heard of you.

#### 9.2.2 Face Ratings

| Rating | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **0** | Unknown / Nobody |
| **1–3** | Recognized locally; a minor figure in one circle |
| **4–6** | Notable within a region or a mid-tier organization |
| **7–9** | Major figure; name recognized across regions |
| **10** | Legendary; setting-level presence — entire continents know your name |

Most starting PCs have Face 0 everywhere. Young sect disciples might have Face 1 in their sect. A Face 10 character is usually an NPC (a Nascent Soul patriarch, a legendary rogue cultivator, an empire's cultivation ancestor).

#### 9.2.3 Face Types (Reputation Alignment)

Every pool of Face has a **type** — the flavor of the reputation. Your Face may be mixed: Face 4 Righteous in your sect, Face 6 Fearsome among local bandits.

| Type | Flavor | Opens | Closes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Righteous** | Virtue, orthodoxy, heroic deeds | Orthodox sects, imperial courts, mortal towns | Demonic markets, outlaw groups |
| **Fearsome** | Raw power, dangerous competence | Respect everywhere; obedience from subordinates | Genuine friendship, casual trust |
| **Demonic** | Ruthlessness, dark cultivation, cruelty | Demonic paths, outlaw networks, forbidden traders | Orthodox society, kind people |
| **Hidden** | Known in specific circles only | Underground, shadow factions, intelligence networks | Open society (nobody knows you) |
| **Mysterious** | Rumored but unverified; legend-like | Fascination, curiosity, offers of alliance | Those who demand proof or clarity |

Type affects **how** people read your weight, not **how much** it weighs. A Demonic Face 7 in an orthodox sect will be met with hostility — but everyone will still treat you with deadly seriousness.

#### 9.2.4 Gaining Face

Face is gained through **visible, witnessed achievement**. Private deeds don't count.

| Action | Face Gained |
|---|---|
| Publicly defeat an opponent of equal realm | +1 in witnesses' context |
| Publicly defeat an opponent a full realm above | +2 to +4 |
| Publicly produce a rare pill, artifact, or formation | +1 to +3 depending on rarity |
| Promotion within a sect, faction, or rank | +1 to +3 |
| Complete a visible heroic/infamous deed | +1 to +3 (Righteous or Demonic depending on deed) |
| Become known for a Dao (earn "Sword Immortal" or similar epithet) | +1 to +2 permanent |

GM discretion on exact values. Face gains are scaled by who witnesses — a duel in front of sect leadership is worth more than one behind a tavern.

#### 9.2.5 Losing Face

Face is lost through **witnessed failure or humiliation.** Face-loss is what drives most cultivation-novel conflict.

| Action | Face Lost |
|---|---|
| Publicly defeated by a peer | −1 to −2 |
| Publicly defeated by a lower-realm opponent | −3 to −5 (**massive humiliation**) |
| Insult goes unanswered in public | −1 |
| Broken oath becomes known | −2 to −5 (plus Karma, see 9.3.4) |
| Exposed as less than claimed (bluff called) | −2 to −4 |
| Fleeing a challenge | −1 to −3 |

**The "Face Slap" trope:** when a young master type publicly humiliates someone, they *transfer* Face — the humiliator gains some of what the humiliated loses. This is why arrogant young masters are always picking fights: they're farming Face.

#### 9.2.6 Using Face (Passive Effects)

When you interact with someone who knows your Face in that context:

- **Social rolls:** add your Face rating (in that context) as bonus dice to persuasion, intimidation, requests for favor, and similar checks.
- **Deference:** NPCs of lower Face generally yield, grant audience, and speak respectfully to higher-Face characters by default.
- **Access:** doors open. Face 3+ in a sect gets you past junior disciples; Face 6+ gets you meetings with elders; Face 9+ gets you an audience with the sect master.
- **Credit / Loans:** shops, alchemists, and crafters extend credit to high-Face patrons.

#### 9.2.7 Spending Face (Active Uses)

Face can be deliberately spent for specific effects. Spent Face returns over time through ongoing achievements (GM discretion — usually within a session or two if the character continues to act notably).

| Spend | Cost | Effect |
|---|---|---|
| **Call a favor** | 1 Face | A known contact or subordinate does something within their means for you |
| **Force an audience** | 2 Face | An NPC of higher Face cannot refuse to see you |
| **Decline a challenge** | 1 Face | Refuse a duel without the usual Face-loss penalty ("I do not deign to fight you") |
| **Vouch for someone** | 2–3 Face | Transfer a portion of your Face temporarily to a companion; you lose equivalent if they disgrace themselves |
| **Reverse a minor slight** | 1 Face | Spend Face to make a small Face-loss event publicly "not count" — you brush it off |

#### 9.2.8 Presence (Cultivator Aura)

A related but distinct mechanic. High-realm cultivators project an **aura** that lower-realm beings physically struggle to withstand. This is not Face — it's the metaphysical pressure of a concentrated cultivation base.

When a cultivator **releases their presence** against a lower-realm target:
- Target must pass a **Soul roll** (Soul attribute + Realm Tier) against the aggressor's Realm Tier as target successes
- Failure: target cannot take hostile action against the aggressor this scene, and loses 1–2 dice on all rolls in their presence
- Success: target acts normally, but the aggressor knows they're resistant

Presence is why a Nascent Soul patriarch can walk through a mortal village and no one dares look at them. It's also why young masters with high-realm grandfathers get away with everything in public — the grandfather's presence is sometimes enough to chill the room without a word.

---

### 9.3 Karma: The Heavens' Ledger

Karma is the cosmic accounting of your significant deeds. Where Face is social and can be manipulated, **Karma is impersonal and inescapable** — it always resolves, sooner or later. The Heavens forget nothing.

#### 9.3.1 How Karma Works (Core Rules)

Each character has a **Karma Ledger** — a list of specific karmic attachments, plus two accumulating pools (White and Black Heavenly Karma).

Karma is generated automatically by play. When a player takes a karma-creating action, the GM writes it in the character's ledger. **Karma is not spent like Face** — it *resolves* through events, usually at dramatic or structurally significant moments (breakthroughs, tribulations, chance encounters).

There are five karma types:

1. **Life Debts** — named; you owe or are owed
2. **Blood Debts** — named; someone you killed left ties
3. **Heart Oaths** — sworn bindings on the soul
4. **Heavenly Karma** — accumulating White/Black pools
5. **Fated Karma** — setting-level destined encounters

The Ties created at character creation (love / owe / hate) are the starting entries in this ledger.

#### 9.3.2 Life Debts

A Life Debt is created when someone saves your life — or when you save theirs. It is **named** (specific to that person) and persists until resolved.

**If you owe a Life Debt:**
- When the debtor calls on you for aid, you must respond (travel to them, help as you can). Refusing generates 1 Black Karma and may cause Qi Deviation at your next breakthrough.
- Resolution: save them back, serve them for an agreed period, or die in their defense.

**If you are owed a Life Debt:**
- You may call on the debtor once for significant aid. They will come if they can.
- A called-in Life Debt is a powerful narrative tool — it's often how weaker cultivators get higher-realm allies to intervene.

**Dying while owing a Life Debt:** the debt is inherited by whoever fulfills the debtor's role in your life — your disciple, your descendant, your sworn brother. This is a setting-defining feature: debts can reach across generations.

#### 9.3.3 Blood Debts

A Blood Debt is created when you kill someone with ties — family, sect, master-disciple relations. The Heavens record the killing; the ties of the slain will seek resolution.

**Mechanical effects:**
- Each named Blood Debt on your ledger adds a **Pursuit Clock** — the GM tracks the likelihood of vengeance catching up.
- At each session break (seclusion), the GM rolls or decides whether a pursuer's investigation advances. High-profile kills advance faster.
- When the pursuer arrives, it's a narrative event — often a high-realm NPC, or a concerted effort by many lower-realm ones.

**Resolution options:**
- **Kill the pursuer** — generates a *new* Blood Debt (their ties will come next)
- **Flee far enough** — changing continents, taking on a new identity, hiding for decades
- **Make restitution** — pay blood price, submit to punishment (often means a debt of your own)
- **Erase the trail** — kill everyone who could seek you out (Demonic path; massive Black Karma)

**Design note:** this is the *Renegade Immortal* engine. Wang Lin spends much of the series hunted by sects he wronged, constantly running from Blood Debts. Players who go demonic-route will accumulate these fast and must plan around them.

#### 9.3.4 Heart Oaths

A **Heart Oath** is a cultivation-binding promise sworn on your own cultivation base. Not every promise is a Heart Oath — most are just words. Heart Oaths are deliberately invoked, usually with specific ritual phrasing ("I swear on my heart," "I bind this to my Dao," etc.).

**When sworn:**
- The oath is recorded by the Heavens
- The terms must be specific and fulfillable
- The swearer chooses the consequence of breaking (severity matches the weight of the oath)

**If fulfilled against hardship:** the swearer gains 2 Insight toward their Dao Seed (or chosen Dao). Significant oaths may grant more. This is why sect inductions, master-disciple bonds, and sworn brotherhood oaths are common — they're a cultivation accelerator.

**If broken:**
- Immediate **Qi Deviation** (drop 1 sub-stage; may drop a full realm if the oath was severe)
- +3 to +5 Black Karma
- Face loss in any context where the broken oath becomes known

**If sworn:** a cultivator may hold only a handful of active Heart Oaths — typically (Realm Tier / 2) + 1. Swearing more dilutes all of them. High-realm cultivators who hold many Heart Oaths are often bound to their sects, masters, and vows in complex webs.

#### 9.3.5 Heavenly Karma (White & Black)

Accumulating pools tracked separately. These represent your aggregate moral weight in the cosmic ledger.

**White Karma** is earned by:
- Saving many lives (a village from a beast tide = +1, a city from invasion = +3, etc.)
- Healing or teaching without charge
- Creating or preserving significant things (rare pills for free distribution, restoring ancient works, founding a school)
- Keeping Heart Oaths through severe hardship

**Black Karma** is earned by:
- Killing many innocents
- Breaking oaths (Heart or public)
- Demonic cultivation methods (generates Black Karma per use)
- Betraying sect, master, or sworn kin
- Destroying without restitution

**Effects on Tribulation (see section 7.5):**

| Net Karma Balance | Effect on Tribulation |
|---|---|
| +10 or more White | Tribulation waves reduced by 1; bonus residue on success |
| +3 to +9 White | Minor favor — slightly gentler trials |
| −2 to +2 | Standard tribulation |
| −3 to −9 Black | Extra wave; harsher trials; heart demons draw on your sins |
| −10 or worse | Multiple extra waves; tribulation actively hunts you (catches you mid-seclusion); Karmic pursuers may arrive |

**This is the structural cost of the demonic path.** Demonic cultivators advance faster but their tribulations become nightmarish. Many demonic cultivators spend significant time trying to *cleanse* Black Karma before attempting a breakthrough — by accumulating White Karma (rare) or by using specialized pills/rituals (expensive, often require other karma trades).

**Karma is visible to Karma Dao cultivators.** They can sense your accumulated balance, sometimes even see specific debts. Hiding Karma requires specialized techniques, concealing artifacts, or Karma-cleansing rituals.

#### 9.3.6 Fated Karma

The setting-level plot karma — destined encounters, prophecies, bloodline curses, ancient vendettas. These are usually set by the GM (or emerge from character backgrounds) and function as narrative hooks.

Examples:
- Your master's murderer still lives
- An ancient demon marked your bloodline; you'll face their descendants
- A prophecy names you as challenger to a specific existing power
- You accidentally entered a forbidden tomb and the guardian remembers your face

Fated Karma resolves at GM discretion but should feel *inevitable* — not "if" but "when."

#### 9.3.7 Karmic Resolution Moments

Karma activates narratively at specific trigger points:

1. **Major breakthroughs** — accumulated Heavenly Karma modifies tribulation
2. **Seclusion passes** — Blood Debt pursuers advance; Fated events approach
3. **Chance encounters** — the Heavens arrange meetings (the person whose master you killed happens to arrive at the same city)
4. **Death scenes** — unpaid Life Debts and unavenged Blood Debts shape what happens next
5. **Dao comprehension thresholds** — high-level Karma Dao cultivators may intervene in your karma at key moments

**GM tool:** at session prep, the GM reviews each PC's karma ledger and looks for resolution opportunities. Karma shouldn't all fire at once — it should steadily introduce pressure.

#### 9.3.8 Manipulating Karma

Karma is cosmic but not untouchable. Specific tools exist:

- **Karma Dao comprehension** — lets you see, alter, and trade karma. High-tier Karma Masters can *transfer* karma between parties, cleanse debts for a price, or weaponize karmic backlash against enemies.
- **Karma-cleansing pills & rituals** — rare, expensive, often require specific ingredients (innocent blood for Black, Dao-tears for White, specific herbs). Usually only sect-level resources.
- **Merit Deeds** — deliberately performing White Karma actions to offset Black. Slow and requires genuine benefit (the Heavens see through performative virtue).
- **Karmic Contracts** — binding agreements (often between sects or powerful individuals) that transfer specific debts. Used for hostage arrangements, alliance bonds, and sect-level resolutions.
- **Hiding identity** — delays karmic pursuit. Doesn't erase karma but keeps it from finding you. Many infamous figures have hidden identities (common in *Renegade Immortal*).

---

### 9.4 How Karma & Face Interact

- **High Face accelerates Karma.** When a high-Face character acts publicly, many witnesses make the deed karmically significant. A low-Face assassin who kills in shadow accumulates less Karma than a famous one who executes in public.
- **Karma can affect Face.** When Fated Karma or Blood Debts resurface publicly, Face loss can be catastrophic. The sect elder who hid his demonic past losing everything when karma catches up is a canonical scene.
- **Face-loss does not generate Karma.** Humiliation is social, not cosmic.
- **Karma-cleansing does not restore Face.** Even if the Heavens forgive, people remember.

### 9.5 Example in Play

Wang Lin's ledger after a few sessions:

**Face:**
- Misty Cloud Sect: Face 2 (Righteous) — minor recognition as a diligent disciple
- Home Village: Face 4 (Righteous) — local hero returning in finery
- Cultivator World at large: Face 0

**Karma Ledger:**
- **Life Debt (owed to):** Uncle Wang (paid his tuition — owed since childhood)
- **Blood Debt (from):** Killed a Zhao clan disciple in a duel — the disciple's brother is searching
- **Heart Oath:** Swore to avenge his mother's death at Late Qi Condensation; unresolved
- **White Karma:** +2 (saved some villagers from a spirit beast)
- **Black Karma:** +1 (used a demonic technique from the family scroll once, pragmatically)
- **Fated Karma:** The Ancient Demon whose tomb his family scroll came from — it remembers.

When Wang Lin attempts his Foundation Establishment breakthrough, the GM rolls the Zhao clan pursuer clock: it advances. The brother arrives in the city two days before Wang Lin's breakthrough. Combined with the fated pressure of his unresolved Heart Oath and the slight Black Karma, the tribulation will be slightly harsher — and Wang Lin now has to handle a pursuer *during* his breakthrough window.

**This is the system working as intended:** karma is not a number on a character sheet, it's the engine of plot. The player's choices echo back through the Heavens and through the world simultaneously.

## 10. Crafting Subgames

### 10.1 Overview

Three crafts share mechanical DNA but produce very different things:

- **Alchemy** — produces *pills* and *medicines* (consumables). The most common craft and the one most tied to cultivation progression.
- **Formations** — produces *inscribed patterns* on surfaces or objects that create persistent environmental effects. Slow, spatial, and often strategic.
- **Artifact Refining** — produces *spirit tools and treasures* (permanent equipment). The rarest and most prestigious craft; deeply tied to the Treasures system (section 11, forthcoming).

All three share the same core roll structure, grade system, and failure philosophy. What differs is their outputs, their time costs, and their place in the cultivator's life.

**Talismans** are a hybrid craft — paper inscribed with simplified formation patterns — treated as a subcategory of Formations (see 10.5).

### 10.2 Common Crafting Framework

#### 10.2.1 The Craft Roll

**Craft pool = Soul Attribute + Realm Tier + Craft Dao dice + Tool/Workspace bonus + Material bonus.**

Roll d6s, count 4+ as successes. Compare successes to the target for the intended grade:

| Grade | Target Successes | Typical Usage |
|---|---|---|
| **Spirit** | 3 | Apprentice work; healing salves, ward papers, plain weapons |
| **Mysterious** | 6 | Foundation Pills, basic spirit weapons, standard formations |
| **Celestial** | 10 | Core Formation Pills, refined spirit treasures, advanced formations |
| **Void** | 15 | Nascent Soul aids, ancient-tier treasures, grand formations |
| **Nirvana** | 20 | Legendary pills, sect-defining treasures, nation-scale formations |

#### 10.2.2 Quality Sub-Ranks

Every successful craft has a quality based on how far you beat the target:

| Margin Over Target | Quality | Effect |
|---|---|---|
| 0 (exactly meets) | **Standard** | Baseline effect as described |
| +1 to +2 | **Refined** | +1 effective bonus (extra dice, extended duration, or similar) |
| +3 to +4 | **Superior** | +2 bonus or unlocks a secondary effect |
| +5 or more | **Perfect** | Functions effectively as a tier higher |

Below target:

| Margin Below Target | Result |
|---|---|
| Missed by 1 | **Flawed** — usable but unreliable: roll 1d6, on a 1–2 the item's effect is negated or backfires |
| Missed by 2–3 | **Failed** — materials wasted, nothing produced |
| Missed by 4+ | **Catastrophic** — materials destroyed plus a consequence (explosion, qi deviation, lingering curse, attention from the Heavens) |

#### 10.2.3 Craft Rank (Profession Tier)

A cultivator's standing *as a crafter* is separate from their cultivation realm. You can be a Core Formation cultivator and a Grandmaster Alchemist — or a Nascent Soul cultivator who's barely a Journeyman at formations.

| Craft Rank | Can Safely Attempt | Can Risk Attempting | Bonus Dice to Roll |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Novice** | Spirit | Mysterious | +0 |
| **Apprentice** | Spirit (reliable) | Mysterious | +1 |
| **Journeyman** | Mysterious | Celestial | +2 |
| **Master** | Celestial | Void | +3 |
| **Grandmaster** | Void | Nirvana | +5 |
| **Dao Master** | Nirvana | — | +7 |

"Can risk attempting" means you can try, but the target grade is harder (effectively +3 target successes) and failure is more likely to be catastrophic.

**Advancing in rank** requires two things:
1. A number of successful crafts at your current max grade (typically 3 Standard, 1 Superior, or 1 Perfect)
2. Craft Dao Insight (Novice → Apprentice at 3 Insight, Apprentice → Journeyman at 8, Journeyman → Master at 20, Master → Grandmaster at 50, Grandmaster → Dao Master at 120)

Most cultivators never rise past Journeyman. Masters are regional figures. Grandmasters are sect-defining. Dao Masters are setting-level.

#### 10.2.4 The Dao Requirement

Higher-grade crafting requires Dao comprehension in the relevant craft Dao:

| Grade | Craft Dao Required |
|---|---|
| Spirit | None (pure skill) |
| Mysterious | Novice |
| Celestial | Initiate |
| Void | Adept |
| Nirvana | Master |

Choosing **Medicine, Poison, Formation, Artifact, or Alchemy** as your Dao Seed at character creation means you gain Insight in that craft at 2× rate — the dedicated-crafter build.

#### 10.2.5 Materials

Every craft consumes materials graded to match the intended output:

- **Spirit-grade materials** — herbs, common ore, paper — cheap and widely available
- **Mysterious-grade materials** — spirit herbs (100+ years old), spirit iron, spirit ink — available from sect stores, specialist merchants
- **Celestial-grade materials** — ancient spirit herbs (1000+ years), spirit beast cores, refined essence ingots — expeditions, auctions, sect rewards
- **Void-grade materials** — legendary ingredients (10,000+ year spirit herbs, hearts of high-realm beasts, essence of natural disasters) — quest items or stolen treasures
- **Nirvana-grade materials** — fragments of fallen stars, blood of ancient gods, roots of the World Tree — myth-level acquisition

**Material quality bonus:** superior materials grant +1 to +3 craft dice, scaling with rarity. A standard Foundation Pill uses standard Mysterious-grade herbs; a superior Foundation Pill uses one Celestial-grade herb as a catalyst. *This is why cultivators hunt for materials — it's not just having them, it's improving what you can make with them.*

#### 10.2.6 Workspace & Tools

Serious crafting requires a dedicated space and proper tools:

| Workspace Quality | Effect |
|---|---|
| **Improvised** (campfire alchemy, floor formations) | −2 dice to craft roll; cannot attempt Celestial+ grade |
| **Basic** (home workshop, standard pill furnace) | No modifier; max Celestial grade |
| **Sect Workshop** | +1 die; max Void grade with proper oversight |
| **Ancestral / Legendary Workshop** | +3 dice; Nirvana grade possible; often attached to spiritual veins |

Special tools (a famed pill furnace, a formation master's compass, a refining anvil marked by the Heavens) add further dice and may be treasures in their own right.

#### 10.2.7 Time Cost

Crafting consumes Seclusion Slots:

| Grade | Seclusion Slots per Attempt |
|---|---|
| Spirit | 1 (or produce multiple per slot for simple items) |
| Mysterious | 2 |
| Celestial | 4 |
| Void | 8 (spans multiple sessions' seclusion) |
| Nirvana | 16+ (multi-year undertaking) |

This is the crafter's core tradeoff: while you're crafting, you're not cultivating. A pure crafter advances in realm more slowly, but the party's preparation depth is enormously boosted.

### 10.3 Alchemy (Pill Refining)

The most impactful craft for cultivation progression. Pills are consumables that enable breakthroughs, heal wounds, extend lifespan, cleanse karma, and serve as one of the primary economies of the cultivation world.

**Dao path:** Medicine Dao (orthodox, healing) or Poison Dao (dark, debilitating, often demonic). Some alchemists walk both.

**Primary tool:** Pill Furnace. Grades of furnaces exist — a mortal's iron furnace vs. a Nascent Soul patriarch's ancient spirit flame cauldron make a huge difference.

**Workspace:** Alchemy Chamber. Best ones are located on spiritual veins with access to elemental flames.

#### 10.3.1 Pill Categories

| Category | Examples | Typical Grade |
|---|---|---|
| **Cultivation Pills** | Qi Gathering Pill, Foundation Pill, Core Formation Pill, Nascent Soul Pill | Spirit → Void |
| **Breakthrough Pills** | Tribulation Pill (survive tribulation), Dao Comprehension Pill | Mysterious → Nirvana |
| **Healing Pills** | Flesh Mending, Meridian Restoration, Qi Deviation Cure | Spirit → Celestial |
| **Combat Pills** | Berserker Pill (+dice, lose control), Iron Skin Pill (temporary defense) | Mysterious → Celestial |
| **Utility Pills** | Concealment Pill (mask aura), Poison Cure, Sleep Pill | Spirit → Celestial |
| **Karma Pills** | Black Karma Cleansing Pill, Oath Severance Pill | Celestial → Void |
| **Poisons** | Incapacitation, qi-disruption, slow-kill, soul-poison | Spirit → Nirvana |

Each pill has:
- **Grade** (Mortal → Divine)
- **Effect** (what it does mechanically)
- **Potency cap** (what realm can benefit — a Foundation Pill does nothing for a Core Formation cultivator)
- **Side effects** (Berserker Pills cause loss of control; Tribulation Pills often leave lasting weakness)

#### 10.3.2 Example Pills

**Foundation Establishment Pill** (Mysterious, target 6)
- Grants +2 dice to the Foundation Establishment breakthrough roll
- Superior (+3 to +4 over target): +4 dice instead
- Perfect (+5 over target): +6 dice and reduces Qi Deviation severity on failure

**Qi Deviation Cure Pill** (Mysterious, target 6)
- Removes 1 Qi Deviation penalty after a failed breakthrough
- Superior: removes 2 penalties
- Perfect: fully restores lost sub-stage

**Tribulation Pill** (Celestial, target 10)
- Grants +3 dice to tribulation wave defense rolls for the duration of the tribulation
- Superior: +5 dice
- Perfect: +5 dice *and* reduces one wave's damage outright

**Black Karma Cleansing Pill** (Void, target 15)
- Cleanses −3 Black Karma from the user's ledger
- Requires a rare ingredient (innocent blood or a specific rare herb — GM choice) and often carries its own karmic cost
- Superior: cleanses −5; Perfect: cleanses −8

#### 10.3.3 Alchemy Failure Modes

- **Flawed pill:** may be inert, may cause mild Qi Deviation if consumed, or produce unpredictable side effects
- **Failed pill:** materials wasted, pill is ash
- **Catastrophic pill failure:** furnace explosion (damage equal to grade: Mysterious = 3 wounds, Celestial = 5 wounds, etc.), may injure bystanders; rare pills may also attract karmic attention

---

### 10.4 Formations (Arrays)

Inscribed patterns of qi circuitry that produce persistent environmental effects. Formations are *placed* — they exist in a location (a cave, a mountain, a battlefield) and affect everything within their boundaries until disabled or broken.

**Dao path:** Formation Dao.

**Primary tool:** Formation Compass and Formation Flags — the flags are physical anchors planted around the formation's perimeter; the compass maps qi flow and resonance.

**Workspace:** Formations are created *in situ* — the chosen location is the workspace. Spiritual veins, specific star positions, and ancient ruins make better formation sites.

#### 10.4.1 Formation Categories

| Category | Example Effects |
|---|---|
| **Defensive** | Barrier that absorbs X successes of damage before breaking; repels aura; shields against lightning |
| **Offensive / Trap** | Triggered illusion, qi-drain, damage, sealing |
| **Concealment** | Hides aura, appearance, or entire locations from detection |
| **Spirit-Gathering** | Concentrates ambient qi in an area — *bonus to cultivation speed during seclusion* |
| **Illusion** | Misdirection, hallucination, area-denial through confusion |
| **Teleportation** | Fixed-point portals between two established formations |
| **Tribulation** | Diverts, weakens, or modifies Heavenly Tribulation (crucial for preparation) |
| **Sealing** | Imprisons souls, spirits, or lower-realm cultivators |
| **Life-preserving** | Stasis, protection from death within zone |

#### 10.4.2 Formation Parameters

Every formation has:
- **Grade** (Spirit → Nirvana) — determines potency and the realm it can affect
- **Size** — how large an area it covers (small room → city-wide)
- **Power source** — needs qi fuel: spirit stones (most common), a spiritual vein, or the creator's own cultivation
- **Duration** — until spirit stones depleted, until broken, or permanent if keyed to a vein
- **Triggers** — when it activates (always-on, intruder-sensed, manually activated)

#### 10.4.3 Example Formations

**Concealment Ward** (Mysterious, target 6)
- Hides the warded area from casual spiritual sense scans up to Core Formation
- Radius: small (cave-sized) for Standard, house-sized for Refined, estate for Superior
- Fuel: 1 low-grade spirit stone per day

**Spirit-Gathering Array** (Mysterious, target 6)
- Concentrates ambient qi; seclusion within grants +1 die to cultivation breakthrough attempts
- Superior: +2 dice
- Perfect: +3 dice *and* reduces Qi Deviation severity on failure
- Fuel: spiritual vein required for permanent, or 10 low-grade spirit stones per week

**Tribulation Diversion Array** (Celestial, target 10)
- Reduces tribulation by 1 wave for anyone breaking through within the formation
- Superior: reduces by 2 waves; Perfect: reduces by 3 waves and redirects half the damage elsewhere
- Single-use — burns out after one tribulation
- Fuel: one Celestial-grade spirit stone or equivalent

**Nine-Dragon Sealing Formation** (Void, target 15)
- Imprisons a cultivator up to Nascent Soul realm; they cannot escape by their own power
- Requires nine Formation Flags placed around a perimeter, plus a keyed focal anchor
- Power decays if fuel runs out; powered Nascent Soul prisoners break out in months, Core Formation in years

#### 10.4.4 Breaking Formations

Formations can be broken by:
- **Overwhelming force** — an attack strong enough to exceed the formation's defensive rating
- **Disrupting a Formation Flag** — removing one flag weakens the formation; removing enough breaks it
- **Counter-formation** — another Formation Master can unweave yours if their rank equals or exceeds yours
- **Fuel depletion** — if spirit stones run out, the formation dies

This creates tactical play — sect assaults often begin with scouts finding and destroying Formation Flags.

#### 10.4.5 Formation Failure Modes

- **Flawed formation:** works at reduced power, may flicker out, may produce side effects (illusion formations may affect the creator too)
- **Failed formation:** doesn't activate; materials consumed
- **Catastrophic formation failure:** qi backfires through the creator (Qi Deviation risk); may trap the creator in their own seal; may summon unintended entities

---

### 10.5 Talismans (Inscribed Papers)

A subcategory of Formations — simplified, single-use formation patterns inscribed on paper, silk, or other portable surfaces. The workhorse countermeasure of the cultivation world.

**Craft Roll:** Uses Formation Dao but at reduced targets (Spirit 2, Mysterious 4, Celestial 7, Void 10). They're less powerful than placed formations but portable.

**Effects:** essentially single-use formation effects — teleportation talismans, shielding talismans, offensive talismans, concealment talismans. Most life-saving talismans referenced in section 4 are talismans in this sense.

**Time:** talismans can be mass-produced — one slot produces (Craft Rank level) talismans of up to the crafter's safe grade.

**Key design point:** a sect that cycles its Formation Masters through talisman-production duties keeps its disciples alive. Rogue cultivators who lose access to talisman supply become dramatically more vulnerable.

---

### 10.6 Artifact Refining

The slowest, rarest, and most prestigious craft. Refines spirit iron and essence-bearing materials into spirit tools, spirit weapons, and treasures. Products are *permanent* — a refined artifact may outlast its creator by millennia.

**Dao path:** Artifact Dao.

**Primary tool:** Refining Furnace — vastly more demanding than a Pill Furnace; requires elemental flames, often from spiritual veins or trapped spirit beasts.

**Workspace:** Refining Hall. The best are attached to volcanic vents, lightning-strike mountains, or ancient war relics.

#### 10.6.1 Artifact Categories

| Category | Examples |
|---|---|
| **Weapons** | Swords, spears, bows, whips, fans (all these can be spirit weapons) |
| **Defensive** | Armor, cloaks, bracelets, rings with shielding effects |
| **Transport** | Flying swords (classic cultivator transport), flying clouds, spirit boats |
| **Utility** | Spatial rings (storage), beast-taming whips, scrying mirrors |
| **Soul Items** | Soul-refining tools, Nascent Soul escape pendants, life-binding rings |

Every artifact has:
- **Grade** (Spirit → Nirvana)
- **Realm requirement** — the minimum realm to wield it effectively. *Core Formation is the gate for any artifact above Spirit-grade* (canonical Xian Ni rule).
- **Binding status** — unbound, blood-bound, soul-bound (see 10.6.3)
- **Abilities** — base effects, plus unlocked abilities with higher mastery

Specific treasure mechanics are detailed in the forthcoming Treasures section (11). This section covers how they're *made*.

#### 10.6.2 The Refining Process

Refining a spirit artifact is a multi-stage process:

1. **Material preparation** — raw spirit iron is purified in the furnace over days of concentrated flame. Impurities burn off.
2. **Essence infusion** — elemental essences (fire, metal, lightning) are forced into the material to grant it properties.
3. **Pattern inscription** — the refiner inscribes formation-like patterns within the artifact's structure to grant it abilities. Often incorporates part of Formation Dao.
4. **Binding preparation** — for blood-bound items, the refiner reserves a binding point that future wielders activate with their blood.
5. **Final forging** — shape, hardness, balance. This is where the artifact becomes a *thing*.
6. **Soul nourishment** (for Celestial+ artifacts) — the refiner may imbue a spark of their own soul or the captured spirit of a creature. Higher-tier artifacts are quasi-sentient; the greatest are fully sentient.

Each stage is a Seclusion Slot. A Celestial-grade artifact takes 4 slots total. A Void-grade artifact takes 8 slots spread over multiple sessions.

#### 10.6.3 Artifact Binding

Artifacts must be **bound** to a wielder to unlock their full power:

- **Unbound:** the artifact works at ~30% effectiveness; anyone can pick it up and use it at this level
- **Blood-Bound:** the wielder drips blood onto the artifact during meditation (one seclusion slot); the artifact is tied to them and reaches ~70% effectiveness. Can be forcibly seized and rebound by a significantly higher-realm cultivator.
- **Soul-Bound:** the wielder embeds a fragment of their soul into the artifact; ~100% effectiveness, cannot be used by others while the wielder lives. **Dying severs the bond** (important for loot distribution after kills).

Binding is also its own time cost — don't forget to bind.

#### 10.6.4 Example Artifacts

**Frost Crane Flying Sword** (Mysterious, minimum wielder: Foundation Establishment)
- +3 dice to sword attacks
- Grants basic flight (moderate speed, medium distance)
- Refined: +4 dice and can release a small ice burst 1/scene
- Perfect: effectively Celestial-grade; +5 dice, extended flight, sentient enough to return when called

**Azure Cloud Robe** (Celestial, minimum wielder: Core Formation)
- +3 dice to defense rolls
- Absorbs 1 qi-based attack per scene fully
- Refined: +4 dice, self-repairs minor damage overnight

**Nascent Soul Escape Pendant** (Void, minimum wielder: any)
- Stores a prepared escape formation; when the wearer's Nascent Soul flees the body, the pendant channels it to a pre-set safe location up to regional distance
- Consumed on use

*(Full treasure taxonomy and wielder interactions in the Treasures section.)*

#### 10.6.5 Artifact Failure Modes

- **Flawed artifact:** works at reduced effectiveness; may have unwanted behaviors (a sword that refuses to cut certain materials, an armor that grows cold)
- **Failed artifact:** the material is ruined — usually recyclable for scrap but the form is lost
- **Catastrophic artifact failure:** furnace explosion (heavy damage, often fatal at Celestial+ grade); may summon elemental backlash; soul-nourishment may corrupt the refiner's own soul

### 10.7 Crafters in Play: Design Notes

#### 10.7.1 The Crafter Tradeoff

A dedicated crafter PC advances in realm noticeably slower than a pure combatant — they're spending Seclusion Slots on craft attempts rather than Cultivate actions. In exchange, they become *invaluable* to the party:
- They supply pills that accelerate everyone's breakthroughs
- They plant formations that change tactical maps
- They forge treasures that their allies wield
- They craft the life-saving talismans that keep everyone alive

A party of three combatants and one alchemist will out-scale a party of four combatants by late Foundation Establishment. This is as intended.

#### 10.7.2 Commissioning & Economy

Crafters can be commissioned — NPCs will pay crafter PCs handsomely for rare pills, formations, or artifacts. This is a major income stream.

Conversely, non-crafter parties must *buy* from NPC crafters, which costs spirit stones, favors, or requires sect access. Auctions, sect stores, and wandering alchemists are standard encounter types.

**Rough price guide (in low-grade spirit stones):**

| Item Grade | Price Range |
|---|---|
| Spirit | 1–20 |
| Mysterious | 20–500 |
| Celestial | 500–20,000 |
| Void | 20,000–1,000,000 |
| Nirvana | Priceless / myth-item |

Quality sub-ranks multiply cost roughly: Refined ×2, Superior ×4, Perfect ×10+.

#### 10.7.3 Theft, Inheritance, and Loot

Artifact refining in particular creates loot economies:
- Defeated enemies' soul-bound artifacts become unbound at their death (see 10.6.3); looters can rebind them
- Flawed / failed crafting outputs can sometimes be scavenged for materials
- Ancient tombs and ruins often contain unbound or abandoned artifacts — a major dungeon draw
- Craft knowledge itself is valuable: a grandmaster's formula or inscription manual may be worth more than the pills it produces

#### 10.7.4 Quick Reference Table

| Craft | Output | Dao | Workspace | Key Tool | Time Scale |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Alchemy** | Pills (consumables) | Medicine / Poison | Alchemy Chamber | Pill Furnace | Hours–Weeks |
| **Formations** | Placed arrays (persistent) | Formation | In-situ at site | Compass + Flags | Days–Months |
| **Talismans** | Inscribed papers (single-use) | Formation | Writing desk | Spirit brush + ink | Hours (mass-producible) |
| **Artifacts** | Spirit tools (permanent) | Artifact | Refining Hall | Refining Furnace | Weeks–Years |

## 11. Treasures

### 11.1 Overview

A **treasure** (宝 / bǎo) is any artifact, item, or naturally formed object that carries cultivation significance. Most treasures come from Artifact Refining (section 10.6), but the world is also full of ancient relics, inherited heirlooms, naturally formed wonders, and living treasures with their own will.

Treasures are how weaker cultivators punch above their realm. A Foundation Establishment disciple with a Celestial-grade sword their grandmother hid in the attic can outfight Core Formation cultivators, *if* they can bear the backlash and can actually wield it.

### 11.2 The Xian Ni Tier System

Every treasure sits on this canonical ladder:

| Tier | Canonical Name | Flavor | Typical Wielder Realm |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Spirit Treasure** (灵宝) | The workhorse tier. Commonly crafted, widely circulated. | Qi Condensation — Foundation Establishment |
| 2 | **Mysterious Treasure** (玄宝) | Refined, notable. The ambitious disciple's goal. | Foundation Establishment — Core Formation |
| 3 | **Celestial Treasure** (仙宝) | The bridge to the Second Step. Rare and prized. Most First-Step cultivators never own one. | Nascent Soul — Soul Formation |
| 4 | **Void Treasure** (虚宝) | Ancient relics from lost eras. Even Nirvana Shatterers hunt these. | Soul Transformation — Ascendant |
| 5 | **Nirvana Treasure** (涅宝) | Campaign-cap treasures. Legendary. Often unique. | Nirvana realms |

**Between-tier classification:** *Pseudo-X* treasures exist — a "Pseudo-Celestial Treasure" is a Mysterious-grade item that punches at the low Celestial tier due to exceptional quality, a rare material, or a lucky refinement. Canonically, Wang Lin himself crafts a Pseudo-Celestial Treasure (the Hairpin with Thousand Illusion Ruthless Domain). Mechanically, a **Perfect-quality treasure at any grade functions as Pseudo-next-tier** (see 11.3).

### 11.3 Quality Sub-Ranks

Every treasure also has a quality within its tier:

| Quality | Effect | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Flawed** | Works at ~60% — dice bonuses reduced, unreliable | Usually unstable; may misfire under stress |
| **Standard** | Baseline effect as described | Most treasures you'll encounter |
| **Refined** | +1 effective bonus (dice, range, duration, or a minor secondary ability) | Good find |
| **Superior** | +2 bonus, or unlocks a named ability | Sect-treasure level |
| **Perfect** | Functions as **Pseudo-next-tier** — the quality eclipses the grade | A Perfect Celestial sword fights at low Void |

When describing a treasure, both its **tier** and **quality** matter: a Refined Celestial weapon and a Perfect Mysterious weapon are close in raw power, but the Refined Celestial scales better, bypasses more Realm Barriers, and has higher ceilings for evolution.

### 11.4 Treasure Categories

Treasures are grouped by function. Most categories can exist at any tier; a weapon could be Spirit-tier or Nirvana-tier. Category determines *what it does*; tier determines *how well*.

| Category | Function | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| **Weapons** | Offensive — swords, spears, bows, whips, fans, brushes, seals | Flying Sword, War Spear, Celestial Brush |
| **Defensive** | Protection — armor, robes, shields, rings, bracelets | Azure Cloud Robe, Three Bells Shield, Devil Armor |
| **Transport** | Flight and travel — flying swords, flying boats, cloud-mounts | Flying Sword (dual-use with Weapons), Cloud Skiff |
| **Utility** | Storage, scrying, concealment, binding | Spatial Ring, Scrying Mirror, Beast-Taming Whip |
| **Soul Items** | Affect the soul directly — offensive, defensive, or soul-housing | Soul Flag, Nascent Soul Escape Pendant, Soul Lasher |
| **Formation Items** | Portable or special-purpose formation anchors | Formation Flag Set, Trapping Orb |
| **Puppets & Guards** | Living or semi-living constructs (see 11.9.4) | Celestial Guards, Corpse Puppets |
| **Consumable-Treasures** | Permanent items that consume on use (edge case) | Escape Pendants, Tribulation-Enduring Seals |

### 11.5 Wielder Requirements & Realm Gates

The defining constraint of the treasure system — ported directly from *Xian Ni* canon:

| Treasure Tier | Minimum Realm to Wield | Effective Use Requires |
|---|---|---|
| **Spirit** | Qi Condensation | No gate |
| **Mysterious** | Foundation Establishment | Basic binding |
| **Celestial** | **Core Formation** | Golden Core to channel |
| **Void** | Nascent Soul | Nascent Soul to resonate |
| **Nirvana** | Soul Formation+ | Comprehended Domain to control |

**What "can't wield" means mechanically:** a cultivator below the minimum can *hold* the treasure, *attempt* to use it, but:

- **One realm below:** treasure functions at ~30% effectiveness (reduce all dice bonuses by 2/3, minimum 1). Each attempted use costs double qi. Risk of qi deviation on any botched roll.
- **Two realms below:** treasure is functionally inert. At best, it serves as expensive decoration. At worst, it backfires — **the cultivator's body may not survive the first attempt at channeling.** This is canonical — it's why young cultivators who loot treasure tombs often die.
- **Three realms below:** touching the treasure can injure or kill. High-tier treasures have ambient pressure that crushes weaker souls.

**Narrative implication:** A low-realm PC who inherits or steals a high-tier treasure has a bomb, not a weapon. Either hide it until they catch up, or find someone to use it for them. This creates excellent plot hooks — the ancestral blade you can't yet wield, the tomb-looted pendant that burns in your bag.

### 11.6 Binding in Depth

From section 10.6.3, expanded:

**Unbound (wild):** the treasure is inert or at ~30% effectiveness. Anyone can pick it up at this level. Most loot from corpses is unbound at acquisition (because the previous wielder's soul-bond broke at death). High-tier unbound treasures may be *aware* and *choosing* not to cooperate with unworthy holders.

**Blood-Bound:** the wielder drips essence blood onto the treasure over a binding meditation (1 Seclusion Slot). Effectiveness rises to ~70%. The treasure responds to them specifically. **Can be forcibly unbound** by a cultivator at least 2 realms higher than the wielder, through ritual or torture. Cheap to bind, cheap to lose. Most disciples blood-bind their working gear.

**Soul-Bound:** the wielder embeds a fragment of their soul into the treasure (1 Seclusion Slot, plus a brief, painful self-harm to the origin soul). Effectiveness: **100%, plus evolution potential** (see 11.7). Cannot be used by others while the wielder lives. **Severed permanently at the wielder's death** — the treasure becomes unbound loot.

**Core-Bound** (advanced):** the wielder merges their Golden Core with the treasure through a months-long process. Effectiveness: 120% (can exceed the treasure's base grade due to resonance). **Requires Core Formation.** The treasure becomes a literal extension of the wielder. If either is destroyed, both suffer: the treasure shatters if the core does, and the core deviates if the treasure is broken. Wang Lin does this with his Core-Treasure Sword.

**Binding interactions:**

| Scenario | Outcome |
|---|---|
| Kill a Blood-Bound wielder | Treasure becomes Unbound; anyone can claim and rebind |
| Kill a Soul-Bound wielder | Treasure becomes Unbound; previous soul fragment returns or disperses |
| Kill a Core-Bound wielder | Treasure is **often destroyed** alongside them; if intact, it's permanently damaged (downgrade one quality) |
| Attempt to steal a bond (cultivator captured alive) | Long ritual over weeks; risk of qi deviation on both sides; may require a Soul Dao master |
| Inherit a treasure as a gift from a dying master | Bond transfers cleanly; no damage to treasure |

### 11.7 Treasure Sentience

Higher-tier treasures develop awareness. This isn't flavor — it affects play.

| Tier | Sentience Level | Behavior |
|---|---|---|
| **Spirit / Mysterious** | Inert | No will. Pure tools. |
| **Celestial** | Nascent awareness | Has preferences (likes skilled users, resists crude handling); may refuse to activate fully under a wielder it finds unworthy. |
| **Void** | Quasi-sentient | Can be "persuaded." Often has a vestigial name, favored Dao, and a "personality." Will sometimes refuse to attack specific targets (old friends, kin, sworn enemies of its own). |
| **Nirvana** | Fully sentient | A named entity with memory, preferences, and agenda. May demand specific wielders. Legendary swords choose their bearers. A Nirvana treasure in someone's hand is also a relationship. |

**Sentient treasures can:**
- Refuse to activate (reducing their effectiveness)
- Demand acts of the wielder (quests, tributes, restrictions)
- Communicate with a bonded wielder (telepathically or through dreams)
- "Die" if their sentience is shattered (reducing them to Unbound husks)
- Pass from wielder to wielder over generations, accumulating history

**Mechanical handling:** the GM plays sentient treasures as NPCs. A Void-tier sword with a grudge against Demonic cultivators will refuse to strike some targets; a Nirvana Bell that loved its previous master may not fully respond to their killer even after rebinding.

**This is where much of the dramatic weight of treasures lives in cultivation fiction** — the loaned ancestral sword that only works while you honor the family, the demonic blade that whispers for blood, the bell that sings when its old enemy is near.

### 11.8 Dao-Linked Treasures

Many treasures resonate with a specific Dao. The resonance affects both what they do and who can wield them properly.

**Resonance benefits** (when wielder walks the matching Dao):
- +1 effective tier when wielded (Mysterious functions at Celestial level)
- Treasure unlocks "hidden" abilities the Dao reveals
- Evolution potential (see 11.7) improves — treasure may grow with the wielder

**Dao-mismatch penalties** (wielder walks an *opposing* Dao):
- −1 effective tier
- Treasure may resist activation (sentience check at Celestial+)
- At Void+ tier, opposing-Dao treasures may actively harm the wielder

**Common Dao-Linked types:**
- **Sword Dao Treasures** — most flying swords; harmonize with Sword Dao
- **Karma Treasures** — the Karma Whip (Wang Lin's), Karma Bells, Fate-binding Rings
- **Slaughter Treasures** — Demonic swords, blood-soaked blades, Butcher's Clevers; gain power with kills, demand them
- **Soul Treasures** — Soul Flags, Soul Bells, Soul-Devouring spears
- **Space Treasures** — Spatial Rings, Teleportation Stamps, Gate Keys
- **Time Treasures** — extremely rare; slow/speed/loop effects; often Void+ tier only
- **Restriction Treasures** — formation-based, sealing, binding

A Slaughter Dao treasure in the hands of an orthodox healer is useless — and possibly hostile. This is a major consideration when distributing loot among a party.

### 11.9 Special Treasure Types

#### 11.9.1 Naturally Formed Treasures (自然之宝)

Not refined by any cultivator. Formed by nature over millennia, by coincidence of qi flows, elemental concentrations, or absorbed cosmic influence. Canonically, a pair of metal-element flints that Wang Lin finds is a naturally formed magical treasure.

- **Always high quality for their tier** (Refined minimum; many are Superior)
- **Often irreplaceable** — once consumed or destroyed, cannot be remade
- **Usually unbound** at discovery; can be bound normally
- **Common in:** spiritual veins, ancient battlefields, meteor craters, beast lairs, unexplored ruins
- **GM use:** natural treasures are what makes exploration rewarding. Unlike crafted loot, they represent *discovery* — the world had something waiting

#### 11.9.2 Inherited Treasures

Passed down through sects, clans, or master-disciple lines. Come with conditions.

- **Usually Soul-Bound to the lineage**, not an individual — the treasure responds to any blood descendant or formally accepted disciple
- **Carry karmic residue** — unresolved oaths of the previous wielder may obligate the inheritor (this connects to the Karma system, section 9)
- **Often have unlock conditions** — the full power only manifests when the inheritor meets a criterion (reach Core Formation, avenge the previous wielder, fulfill a vow)
- **May be reclaimed** by the original lineage if the inheritor acts against its values

**Design note:** inherited treasures are great GM tools because they come with built-in plot. "Your grandfather's sword, but it won't draw unless you honor his enemies in the Western Continent" is a whole arc in one item.

#### 11.9.3 Living Treasures (Spirit Beasts and Bound Creatures)

Spirit beasts that have been bound or tamed function as treasures mechanically. They have their own realm, attributes, and Dao — but serve a wielder through a contract or imprinted bond.

- **Summoned in combat** at qi cost (scales with beast's realm)
- **Can be fed and grown** — raising a spirit beast is a long-term investment
- **Emotional — can betray** if mistreated
- **Scale-breaking potential:** a Foundation Establishment wielder with a Core Formation bound beast is effectively Core Formation in combat power

Beast-taming is technically a craft of its own, but for TTRPG purposes we treat bound beasts as a treasure category with additional mechanics that the GM and player negotiate.

#### 11.9.4 Celestial Guards (Puppet-Treasures)

Canonical *Xian Ni* type: refined from cultivators (willing — consent is a hard requirement) whose body is hardened, soul amplified, organs withered, and divine sense wiped. The result is a sentient-but-unfeeling combat construct.

Celestial Guards use their own sub-ranking:

| Rank | Approximate Combat Power |
|---|---|
| **Copper** | Foundation Establishment Peak |
| **Silver** | Core Formation Peak |
| **Gold** | Nascent Soul / Soul Formation (can challenge Nirvana Scryers) |
| **Platinum** (GM extension) | Soul Transformation+ |

Celestial Guards:
- Obey their master absolutely (control mechanisms embedded at creation)
- Cannot cultivate further but do not age
- Are Void-tier treasures in value; losing one is a major blow
- Creating one requires a willing sacrifice, which is rare; many Celestial Guards are made under duress or spiritual compulsion, which is canonically demonic

This opens a dark-path subsystem if your table wants it — Demonic cultivators who raise "disciples" only to refine them into Guards.

#### 11.9.5 Spatial Rings (the Ubiquitous Utility)

Canonically ubiquitous — practically every cultivator of Foundation Establishment or above has one. A Spatial Ring contains a pocket dimension used for storage.

| Ring Tier | Storage Volume | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Spirit-grade** | Small room (~30 m³) | Standard starter-tier |
| **Mysterious-grade** | Large room (~200 m³) | Sect-issue for promising disciples |
| **Celestial-grade** | Small building (~2000 m³) | Prized possession |
| **Void-grade** | Manor / estate (~10,000 m³) | Sect treasure |
| **Nirvana-grade** | Small world | Setting-level; may contain inhabitants |

**Restrictions:**
- Living creatures normally cannot be stored (exceptions at Void+ tier)
- Soul-bound rings cannot be looted from the living unless the bond is broken first
- Rings can be stolen from the dead (unbound on death as usual)
- Pocket dimensions can be *entered* if large enough — this is a common narrative device

Spatial Rings are the inventory management solution for cultivators. At higher tiers they become mobile caves and quest locations unto themselves.

### 11.10 Worked Example: A Party's Treasure Distribution

After killing a rogue Core Formation cultivator, the party recovers:

1. **Blood-bound Core-Treasure Sword (Celestial, Superior quality, Sword Dao resonance)** — unbound on death. The party's Foundation Establishment Peak swordsman wants it, but he can't wield a Celestial treasure (two realms below Core Formation's requirement). Options:
   - *Hold it.* He keeps it in his spatial ring for when he breaks through.
   - *Bind it now anyway.* Gets ~30% effectiveness, risks qi deviation every fight. In novels this often kills the wielder.
   - *Sell it.* A Celestial sword is worth 5000+ spirit stones. Funds several breakthroughs.
   - *Lend it.* Give to a Core Formation ally to use, with agreement to return when the swordsman breaks through.

2. **Soul-bound Spatial Ring (Mysterious, Standard)** — bond severed at death, now unbound. Contains: spirit herbs, 200 spirit stones, some personal items, a letter detailing a rendezvous with another cultivator in two weeks (plot hook!). The party's Qi Condensation scout can bind and use this freely. Anyone can.

3. **A naturally formed Metal Flint Pair (Mysterious, Refined, naturally formed)** — found in his pocket. Useful for fire/metal Dao work. The party's Formation Master wants them for upcoming inscription work.

4. **An unbound, clearly resentful Gold-rank Celestial Guard** — the rogue had one. Without its creator's control mechanism, it's inactive but dangerous. Disposing of it is a problem; some sects would pay for the materials, but transporting it is hazardous. The GM notes that the original master's sect might come looking for it.

This is the economy in action: each treasure creates choices and consequences.

## 12. Sects & World Structure

### 12.1 The Layered World

The *Xian Ni* setting is organized as a **nested hierarchy of scales.** A mortal village sits inside a mortal country, which sits inside a cultivator's sphere of influence, which is part of a cultivation planet, which belongs to a star system, which is part of a continent-level realm, which is part of the Vast Expanse. Each level is its own "world" in terms of scope, politics, and what a PC can affect at their current realm.

| Scale | What It Is | Typical PC Realm for This Scope |
|---|---|---|
| **Mortal village / town** | 100–10,000 mortals, one or two cultivators at most | Qi Condensation |
| **Mortal kingdom / country** | Up to millions of mortals, several cultivators | Qi Condensation — Foundation Establishment |
| **Cultivation country** | A state whose peak ruler is a cultivator; formally ranked | Foundation Establishment — Core Formation |
| **Cultivation region** | Multiple countries and sects in a recognized geographic area | Core Formation — Nascent Soul |
| **Cultivation planet** | A whole world; formally ranked; has its own sects and sovereignties | Nascent Soul — Ascendant |
| **Star system** | Dozens to thousands of planets, governed by a specific model (see 12.5) | Ascendant — Nirvana Scryer |
| **Continent (Immortal Astral Continent & equivalents)** | Setting-defining landmasses floating in cosmic space | Nirvana Shatterer (campaign cap) |
| **Vast Expanse** | The entire cosmos. Mostly beyond campaign scope. | Beyond Nirvana Shatterer |

**Design principle:** a party typically "graduates" to a new scale each time they advance a full realm. The local village drama that was terrifying at Qi Condensation is a backwater footnote by Core Formation; the sect politics that dominated Core Formation play are minor when the party reaches Nascent Soul and starts dealing with cultivation-country affairs.

### 12.2 Sect Rank System (1–9)

Sects are formally ranked 1–9 based on the power of their peak cultivators. This is canonical *Xian Ni* (Cloud Sea Star System model) and serves as the default ranking wherever sects exist.

| Rank | Description | Peak Realm | Disciple Count | Scope |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **1** | Family cultivation group — barely a sect | Qi Condensation Peak | 5–20 | Single village |
| **2** | Small cultivation school | Foundation Establishment | 20–80 | Town + surrounding |
| **3** | Town-level sect | Core Formation | 80–300 | A city and hinterland |
| **4** | Minor sect | Nascent Soul | 300–800 | A province |
| **5** | Mid-tier sect (often a branch of a rank 8) | Soul Formation | 800–2,000 | A region |
| **6** | Major regional sect | Soul Transformation | 2,000–5,000 | Large region / small planet |
| **7** | Greater sect | Ascendant | 5,000–15,000 | Planet or minor star-cluster |
| **8** | Elite sect — **may claim a cultivation planet as home territory** | Nirvana Scryer / Cleanser | 15,000+ | Star-system-level; has branch sects |
| **9** | Setting-defining sect — **resides on its own Rank 8+ cultivation planet** | Nirvana Shatterer | 15,000+ (plus branches) | Star-system dominant; 3–5 exist per setting |

**Rank 9 sects are rare** — canonically, the Cloud Sea Star System has only four (God Sect, Ghost Sect, Demon Sect, Heaven Breaking Sect). A campaign setting typically has 3–5 Rank 9 sects total, and their leaders are the "old monsters" shaping the world.

**The Branch Competition** (canonical *Xian Ni* mechanic): Rank 8 sects sponsor Rank 5 branch sects. Every 1,000 years the branches compete; last-place-two-cycles-in-a-row branches are disbanded, survivors reorganized. This creates a built-in tournament structure the GM can deploy — a generational arc can climax with the Branch Competition.

**Rank advancement:** a sect advances rank when its peak cultivator breaks through to the realm required for the next tier, *and* when its membership / resources / territory can support that rank. A Rank 6 sect whose single Soul Formation elder dies drops to Rank 5 automatically.

### 12.3 Country Rank System (1–5)

Countries are *mortal political structures* with a cultivator as the ultimate protector/ruler. Ranked by the peak cultivator's realm.

| Rank | Peak Cultivator Realm | Mortal Population | Scope |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Mortal** | None (no cultivator ruler) | Up to a few million | Cultivators may pass through, but the state is politically mortal |
| **1** | Foundation Establishment | ~1–10 million | A cultivator-ruled duchy or minor kingdom |
| **2** | Core Formation | 10–100 million | A substantial kingdom |
| **3** | Nascent Soul | 100 million – 1 billion | A continental empire |
| **4** | Soul Formation | Billions | A planetary power |
| **5+** | Soul Transformation or above | Planetary / multi-planetary | A cultivation country in its own right; often overlaps with planet ranking |

**Countries and sects interact but aren't the same.** A sect may operate within or across countries. Countries provide tribute (mortal resources, spirit veins), sects provide protection and occasional intervention. Some settings have countries dominant, some have sects dominant — it's a choice.

**Tribute relationships:** high-rank countries often owe tribute (pills, spirit stones, disciples) to higher powers — sects, star-system alliances, or higher-rank countries. This is a canonical plot engine for inter-country conflict.

### 12.4 Cultivation Planets & Star Systems

Above the country scale sit two more layers of rank.

#### 12.4.1 Cultivation Planets

Ranked 1–9, based on the planet's strongest permanent residents and the density of cultivation resources.

| Rank | Description |
|---|---|
| **1–3** | Backwater planets, scattered sects, weak spiritual energy |
| **4–5** | Standard cultivation planet: several Rank 5–6 sects, decent spirit veins |
| **6** | Major planet: hosts a Rank 7 sect, substantial politics |
| **7** | **Peak surface-dwelling world:** the common peak for mortal-inhabited planets. Has Ascendant cultivators. |
| **8** | Rare — only a handful exist per star system. Hosts Nirvana realm cultivators. |
| **9** | **Unique — one per star system.** Mysterious, often hidden or guarded. Hosts the star system's most powerful hidden ancestors. |

Planet rank matters for travel, resource availability, and the realm threshold of conflict. A Foundation Establishment PC on a Rank 3 planet is a significant figure; the same PC on a Rank 7 planet is a street-level nobody.

#### 12.4.2 Star Systems

A star system is a cosmologically bounded region containing many planets, governed by a specific **governance model** (see 12.5). Star systems are the maximum scope most campaigns operate within.

**Canonical examples from *Xian Ni*** (useful as flavor or direct borrow):

- **Cloud Sea Star System** — Sect-dominant. The largest. Notable for origin-crystal resources. Four Rank 9 sects (God / Ghost / Demon / Heaven-Breaking).
- **Alliance Star System** (formerly Brilliant Void) — Cultivation Alliance governance. Two sects, four temples, eight realms. One Rank 9 country in the whole system. Strict rules. A Rank 7 planet is the peak of surface existence.
- **Allheaven Star System** — Clan/bloodline-dominant. Families rather than sects. Focused on bloodline purity and inheritance.
- **(Fourth unnamed system)** — Thunder-realm adjacent; less documented.

Travel between star systems is reserved for cultivators at Soul Formation and above; between continents for Ascendant and above.

#### 12.4.3 Sect-Planet Ownership

At high enough ranks, the distinction between "sect" and "cultivation planet" collapses — a sect *becomes* the planet it rules. This is canonical *Xian Ni*:

| Sect Rank | Relationship to Cultivation Planets |
|---|---|
| **Rank 1–6** | Operate within territory granted or tolerated by greater powers. Own grounds, not planets. |
| **Rank 7** | Typically the dominant power *on* a planet (often a Rank 5–6 planet) but does not formally *own* it — shares the surface with countries and lesser sects. |
| **Rank 8** | **May claim a cultivation planet as home territory** (typically Rank 6–7). The sect's name and the planet's name often begin to blur — e.g. a Rank 8 sect named "Evil Earth" rules the planet called Evil Earth. Branches on other planets. |
| **Rank 9** | **Resides on its own Rank 8+ cultivation planet as sovereign territory.** Canonical: all four Rank 9 sects of Cloud Sea reside on their own cultivation planets, which is explicitly "a rarity across the entire Cloud Sea." |

**What planet-ownership means mechanically:**

- **Absolute authority** within the planet's atmosphere — sect law *is* the law
- **Control of spirit veins** — the planet's cultivation resources flow to the sect's disciples first
- **Formation sovereignty** — a sect-owned planet typically has planetary-scale defensive formations (Void+ grade) that ancestral Elders power
- **Immigration control** — outsiders may be barred, taxed, or monitored
- **Tribute from lesser worlds** — nearby lower-rank planets often owe tribute to the sect's planet

**Campaign implication — a long-arc goal:** "Our sect is Rank 6. One day, we claim a planet of our own." This is a 50-session-plus arc that naturally maps to the party advancing from mid-campaign realms (Core Formation / Nascent Soul) to late-campaign realms (Ascendant / Nirvana). Claiming a planet requires:

1. Reaching Rank 8 as a sect (peak cultivator must be Nirvana Scryer+)
2. Either **finding an unclaimed planet** (expedition arc) or **seizing one** (war arc, often against an existing Rank 7–8 sect)
3. Establishing planetary defensive formations (crafting arc, months–years of Seclusion Slots)
4. Surviving the inevitable challenges from other Rank 8+ sects testing the new neighbor

**Canonical Xian Ni examples:** the Rank 9 God Sect's home planet; the Alliance Star System's four Rank 8 cultivation planets (Country of Rising Cloud, Country of Reed Flute, Temple of Heaven, Evil Earth Sect); the single Rank 9 cultivation planet per star system (always mysterious, often hidden).

### 12.5 Three Governance Models

Every region of the world organizes cultivation power differently. The GM picks one (or mixes them) for their setting.

#### 12.5.1 Sect-Dominant (Cloud Sea model)

Sects are the primary power. Mortal countries exist but are secondary — they pay tribute to the sects whose territory they fall within. Sect politics dominate the cultivation world.

- **Pros:** simple, iconic, well-supported by the Xian Ni framework
- **Setting feel:** Sect A vs. Sect B, young-master rivalries, disciple competitions
- **Campaign fit:** Sect-disciple campaigns, sect-war campaigns
- **Canonical example:** Cloud Sea Star System

#### 12.5.2 Alliance-Governed (Alliance Star System model)

A central Cultivation Alliance rules. Alliance is divided into constituent bodies — typically **two sects, four temples, and eight realms** (or similar). Rules are strict. Rank 4+ countries are Alliance members with tribute obligations. Slaughter is canonically part of daily life here — survivors are cunning.

- **Pros:** enables political intrigue, rules-lawyering, internal factions
- **Setting feel:** strict hierarchy, competitions, sanctioned combat, daily lethality
- **Campaign fit:** rising-through-ranks campaigns, political cultivators, Alliance-service arcs
- **Canonical example:** Alliance Star System

#### 12.5.3 Clan-Dominant (Allheaven model)

Power lives in **bloodline cultivation families.** Clans pass techniques, treasures, and even cultivation methods through heredity. Individual talent matters less than lineage. Politics is family rivalry; alliances are marriages; betrayals are branch schisms.

- **Pros:** rich for inheritance plots, family drama, bloodline mechanics (ties into Heavenly Bodies)
- **Setting feel:** *Dune*-like great houses, marriage politics, bloodline awakenings
- **Campaign fit:** heir-apparent campaigns, family-betrayal arcs, bloodline-awakening stories
- **Canonical example:** Allheaven Star System, Immortal Astral Continent's Ancient Clan

#### 12.5.4 Mixing Models

A setting can blend models by region. The eastern continent of a star system might be sect-dominant while the western continent is clan-dominant. A city-state on the frontier of the Alliance might operate under Alliance law officially but be controlled by a sect behind the scenes. Conflicts between models are a rich source of campaign plot.

### 12.6 Sect Internal Hierarchy

Within any sect (regardless of rank), the internal structure follows a common pattern. The names vary — Xian Ni's Heng Yue Sect uses colored robes to mark inner disciple rank, for example — but the functional tiers are consistent.

| Tier | Who They Are | Typical Realm | Count in a Typical Rank 6 Sect |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Servants / Staff** | Mortals or failed cultivators who keep the sect running | Mortal / QC | Hundreds to thousands |
| **Honorary Disciples** | Entry-level — may have been bribed/connected in, not full members | QC Early–Mid | Hundreds |
| **Outer Disciples** | Proper disciples, low tier — do chores, receive basic training and pills | QC Late–Peak to FE | ~500 |
| **Inner Disciples** | Ranked by strength (in Heng Yue Sect, by robe color: red → white → black → purple); receive significant resources | FE Late to CF | ~100 |
| **Core Disciples / True Disciples** | The sect's hopes; personally tutored by Elders or Ancestors | CF to Nascent Soul | 10–30 |
| **Elders** | Run the sect; teach, lead expeditions, handle external affairs | Nascent Soul to Soul Formation | 10–30 |
| **Grand Elders / Peak Elders** | The strongest active elders, usually running specific divisions (Alchemy Hall, Outer Affairs, etc.) | Soul Formation to Soul Transformation | 3–10 |
| **Sect Master** | Public leader; typically Soul Transformation+ | Soul Transformation / Ascendant | 1 |
| **Ancestor(s)** | Reclusive high-realm figures, rarely active, invoked only in crises | Ascendant to Nirvana Shatterer | 0–3 |

**Note on "Elder":** in smaller sects (Rank 3–4), cultivators at surprisingly low realms (Peak Qi Condensation, Early Foundation Establishment) may hold the title "Elder" simply because they're the oldest active cultivators. Wang Lin's first master, Sun Dazhu, is an Elder at Qi Condensation 12. In a Rank 7 sect, a Nascent Soul cultivator might be a middling Elder. Title ≠ realm.

**Sect Resources Granted Per Tier** (from *Xian Ni* flavor):

| Tier | Typical Resources |
|---|---|
| Honorary | Basic cultivation method, mortal-grade lodging, minor pill allowance |
| Outer | Sect-standard techniques, common spirit stones stipend, access to outer library |
| Inner | Mysterious-grade techniques, monthly pill supply, access to training formations |
| Core / True | Celestial-grade techniques, direct Elder tutelage, private cultivation cave |
| Elder | Access to sect's Void-grade treasures; personal disciples |
| Grand Elder / Sect Master / Ancestor | Sect-defining resources; can commission Nirvana-grade crafting; access to forbidden archives |

**Face modifier:** sect tier grants Face within the sect. Outer Disciple = Face 1. Inner = 2. Core = 4. Elder = 6. Sect Master = 8. This is the sect-Face value referenced in section 9.2.

### 12.7 Realm Distribution (Populating a World)

Rough population estimates for a "default" setting. These are guidelines, not absolutes — some settings deliberately have denser or sparser cultivator populations.

| Realm | Rough Proportion / Example Count |
|---|---|
| **Mortal** | ~99.99% of all sentient beings |
| **Qi Condensation** | 1 in 10,000 of those who seriously attempt cultivation. A large mortal kingdom may have a few hundred. |
| **Foundation Establishment** | 1 in 20 QC survives to FE. A province might have 50. |
| **Core Formation** | 1 in 20 FE reaches CF. A rank 5 sect's middle disciples. A region might have 20–40. |
| **Nascent Soul** | 1 in 30 CF reaches NS. A rank 6 sect has a few; a large region has 20–30. |
| **Soul Formation** | 1 in 50 NS reaches SoF. A planet might have 20–50. |
| **Soul Transformation** | 1 in 100 SoF. A planet might have 3–10. |
| **Ascendant** | 1 in 200 ST. A Rank 7 planet has 1–3. |
| **Nirvana Scryer** | A star system has 20–80 |
| **Nirvana Cleanser** | A star system has 10–30 |
| **Nirvana Shatterer** | A star system has 3–20 (the setting's demigods) |

**Why this matters:** this guides GM decisions about NPC populations and campaign stakes. If a Rank 7 planet has 1–3 Ascendant cultivators, then "killing an Ascendant" is a *planetary event* with enormous political ripples. A Nirvana Shatterer dying reshapes the star system.

**The attrition matters too.** Most cultivators die before Core Formation — tribulations, qi deviations, rivals, and bad luck take them. This is why preparation and countermeasures (section 4) are the backbone of the system: the world is a meat grinder and everyone who survives is someone who planned.

### 12.8 The Celestial Realms & Cave Worlds

Above the mortal-accessible world lie higher realms. Three types matter for a campaign capped at Nirvana Shatterer:

#### 12.8.1 Celestial Realms

Fragments of the ancient Celestial Domain, linked to mortal planets through **Celestial Doors** (major permanent portals). Canonical examples: Thunder, Rain, Wind, Lightning Celestial Realms.

- **Access:** typically requires Soul Formation+ to survive the Celestial Spiritual Energy (which burns weaker cultivators)
- **Contents:** celestial corpses, origin-energy hotspots, ancient techniques, rare Void and Nirvana materials
- **Political status:** the Thunder Celestial Temple (canonical) operates title competitions, granting "Celestial Titles" — massive Face gains to winners
- **Campaign use:** late-campaign arc destinations. A party entering a Celestial Realm is operating at the edge of the framework's scope.

#### 12.8.2 The Immortal Astral Continent

The setting-level horizon. Home to the Ancient Clan (rigid monarchy) and Celestial Clan (loosely federated). At its center, a vast sea that periodically opens to the **Immemorial God Realm** (a cosmic event usually happening every few centuries).

- **Realm requirement:** Nirvana Shatterer minimum to even travel there meaningfully
- **Feel:** setting-ending arc territory. A campaign that reaches here is approaching its climax.
- **GM note:** you probably won't use this as an active play area, but naming it gives the world a horizon — characters *know* it exists, know legends from it, and may receive messengers from it.

#### 12.8.3 Cave Worlds

A powerful cultivator's **pocket dimension** — a world-within-a-world they created. Canonically, the Seven-Colored Celestial Sovereign's Cave World contains entire civilizations who don't know they live inside a pocket dimension.

- **Scale:** small (a cave-sized training space) to vast (containing kingdoms and wars)
- **Access:** created by a Soul Formation+ cultivator (typically); entered through spatial keys or the creator's permission
- **Inhabitants:** if a Cave World is populated, its inhabitants are usually sealed in — they may rebel or kill the master but canonically cannot leave
- **Campaign use:** fantastic dungeon/arc locations. A whole mid-campaign arc can take place inside a Cave World the party discovered. The "real world" is frozen in time outside while the party spends subjective years inside.

### 12.9 Campaign Frames

Six standard campaign frames for this framework. Each comes with defaults for scale, starting realm, and the kinds of problems the party faces.

#### 12.9.1 Sect Disciple (the default)

The party are disciples of the same sect, probably Rank 3–5. Starting realm: Qi Condensation / Foundation Establishment.

- **Problems:** sect politics, rival disciples, assigned missions, sect rivalries, inner tournaments
- **Power trajectory:** disciples → elders over the campaign; sect advances in rank alongside them
- **Structure:** regular sessions at the sect punctuated by missions and tournaments
- **Xian Ni fit:** perfect — this mirrors Wang Lin's early arc

#### 12.9.2 Rogue Cultivators

The party has no sect affiliation. They are independent, often fugitives or wanderers. Starting realm: varies, usually Foundation Establishment (they've survived this far on their own).

- **Problems:** no safety net, Blood Debts accumulating, pursuit, no steady resource supply
- **Power trajectory:** high-risk high-reward; rogues can reach extraordinary heights or die forgotten
- **Structure:** traveling campaign; each region has new factions and dangers
- **Xian Ni fit:** Wang Lin's mid-late arc after he leaves his first sect

#### 12.9.3 Clan Heirs

The party are heirs or prominent members of cultivation clans (Allheaven-style setting). Starting realm: Foundation Establishment / Core Formation.

- **Problems:** family politics, marriage alliances, bloodline awakenings, inheritance disputes
- **Power trajectory:** aligned with clan fortunes; a clan's rise elevates all PCs
- **Structure:** seasonal — gatherings, competitions, inheritance rites
- **Xian Ni fit:** partially — the Ancient Clan / Celestial Clan dynamics inform this

#### 12.9.4 Mortal World Survivors

A low-power frame. The party are mortals or fresh Qi Condensation cultivators in a setting where the world just recently opened to cultivation — or where they're local heroes far from any sect.

- **Problems:** a single Foundation Establishment cultivator threatening their homeland; no access to techniques or resources; building from nothing
- **Power trajectory:** slow — the party might spend the entire campaign reaching Core Formation
- **Structure:** grounded, local-stakes; every resource found is precious
- **Xian Ni fit:** Wang Lin's very earliest chapters

#### 12.9.5 Sect War

An active conflict between sects. PCs may be disciples on one side, mercenaries, or survivors caught in the middle. Starting realm: Foundation Establishment+.

- **Problems:** mass combat, assassinations, siege formations, refugee management, flipping loyalties
- **Power trajectory:** fast — war accelerates cultivation through constant Moments of Truth
- **Structure:** campaign of connected military actions
- **Xian Ni fit:** the 18,000-years-ago war against the Heaven Breaking Sect is a canonical example

#### 12.9.6 Exploration / Frontier

The party ventures into unmapped territory — an ancient tomb opened, a Cave World discovered, a Celestial Realm breach, a freshly-settled frontier planet.

- **Problems:** unknown geography, rare-but-deadly encounters, treasure temptations, sealed entities
- **Power trajectory:** unpredictable — a single lucky find can vault a PC multiple realms in an arc
- **Structure:** expedition arcs with return-to-civilization interludes
- **Xian Ni fit:** Wang Lin's Foreign Battleground and various Celestial Realm arcs

### 12.10 GM Starter Toolkit: Building Your Setting

A quick-build procedure for a GM who wants to spin up a setting in one session of prep.

**Step 1: Pick a scale.** What's the widest scope of the campaign?
- *A region* (for sect-disciple frames at Qi Condensation → Core Formation)
- *A planet* (for Core Formation → Nascent Soul)
- *A star system* (for Nascent Soul → Nirvana)

**Step 2: Pick a governance model.**
- Sect-dominant (default, easiest)
- Alliance-governed
- Clan-dominant
- Mixed (more work, richer setting)

**Step 3: Place the peak powers.**
- For a region: 1–2 Rank 5–6 sects, a Rank 3 country
- For a planet: 3–5 Rank 5–7 sects, one Rank 8 sect or equivalent, several cultivation countries
- For a star system: 3–5 Rank 9 sects (or equivalents), multiple cultivation planets at various ranks

**Step 4: Sketch three factions the party will care about.** At minimum:
- The party's home or origin (sect / clan / country)
- One direct rival (local)
- One distant threat or attraction (regional / planetary)

**Step 5: Name three old monsters the world is afraid of.** Even if the party never meets them. These are the ancestors whose names make people check over their shoulders. Examples in the mold of canonical Rank 9 sect ancestors: "Thunder Ancestor," "the Blood Reaver," "the Silent Empress." Give each a realm (typically Nirvana something) and one defining trait.

**Step 6: Seed two complications.** Things that will break the status quo during the campaign:
- A prophecy about to come true
- An old debt about to be called in
- A sect about to break into war
- A tomb about to open
- A tribulation about to descend

**Step 7: Drop the PCs in at step 1's scale.** Start small; the world expands as the party rises in realm.

## 13. GM Tools

### 13.1 The Core Loop (One-Sentence Rules)

Everything else is modifiers:

> **Dice Pool** = Attribute Score + Realm Tier + technique/Dao/equipment/practice modifiers → roll d6s → count **4+** as successes → compare to a target or an opposed pool.

**When in doubt, the GM's default target numbers:**

| Difficulty | Target Successes |
|---|---|
| Trivial | 1 |
| Standard | 3 |
| Hard | 5 |
| Very hard | 7 |
| Extreme | 10+ |

If there's an opposing roller, use opposed-roll (their successes cancel yours) instead of a flat target.

**The two most-used modifiers:**
- **Technique dice** (+2 to +5 typical, based on tier)
- **Practice** (+1 at Trained, +2 at Well-Practiced, +3 at Masterful)

### 13.2 GM's Session-Zero Checklist

Before the first session, the GM has these conversations with the table and makes these decisions:

**With the players:**
- [ ] Campaign frame (Sect Disciple / Rogue / Clan Heir / Mortal / Sect War / Exploration)
- [ ] Starting realm (typically Mortal → Qi Condensation during session 1)
- [ ] Confirm random roots, lethality, content limits
- [ ] Group concept: same sect? rival factions? sworn siblings? strangers?
- [ ] Agree on "session zero" character creation or have it done solo

**GM makes for themselves:**
- [ ] Pick governance model (Sect / Alliance / Clan / Mixed)
- [ ] Sketch the starting scale (region / planet / star system)
- [ ] Name 3–5 factions the party will encounter
- [ ] Name 3 "old monsters" whose shadow hangs over the world
- [ ] Seed 2 complications that will break the status quo during play
- [ ] Decide house-rule answers to: does the Insight Gambit work against sparring partners? Does Resentment refill in Incognito Mode? Etc.

### 13.3 Session Prep Workflow

Roughly 30–60 minutes per session.

**Step 1: Review Seclusion Actions (10 min)**
- Each player declared actions at end of last session or via chat
- Resolve each: did the crafting succeed? Did the cultivation reach a milestone? Did anyone fail a breakthrough?
- Update PC sheets with results before session start

**Step 2: Advance Karma Clocks (5 min)**
- Review each PC's Karma Ledger
- Roll or decide: does any Blood Debt pursuer advance toward the PC this gap?
- Does any Fated Karma event come closer?
- Note 1–2 karmic events that *could* occur this session (don't have to — just have them ready)

**Step 3: Plot This Session's Shape (15 min)**
- What's the party's declared goal this session?
- What's the reactive world doing? (Faction NPC moves, pursuer progress, tribulation of an NPC)
- One scene of quiet, one scene of tension, one scene of action — as a default
- Prepare the *space* for improvisation, not a script

**Step 4: Stat Up What You Need (10–15 min)**
- Any named NPC the party might fight or interact deeply with
- Use the templates in 13.5
- Don't overprep — a typical session uses 1–2 new statblocks

**Step 5: Reset Your Own Tracking Sheet (5 min)**
- Countermeasures each PC has (so you can call them out before killing blows)
- Each PC's current Insight/MP/Resentment totals (so you know what resources they could deploy)
- Current pursuer clocks, fated timers, sect tensions

### 13.4 The GM's Running Sheet

Maintain one document across sessions tracking:

**Per-PC:**
- Current realm + sub-stage + attribute scores
- Countermeasures available (the critical one — you must know these before declaring killing blows)
- Active Karma debts + pursuer clock progress
- Active Heart Oaths (so you can complicate them)
- Face values per context

**World-level:**
- Faction tension states (who's feuding with whom, currently)
- Pursuer clocks (NPCs hunting PCs, with next-action triggers)
- Fated events with approaching triggers
- Recent Face-shifts (who just gained/lost standing)
- Branch Competition or sect-war timers (if applicable)

**This session's prep:**
- Planned scenes and their hooks
- NPC statblocks needed
- Locations with formations / spiritual veins noted
- Treasures available as loot in this session (and their binding states)

### 13.5 NPC Stat Templates

Rather than statting every NPC fully, use these quick templates. **Fill in attributes only when they'll matter this session.**

#### 13.5.1 Minimal NPC (background character)

```
Name · Realm (Tier) · One defining trait
```

Example: *Elder Hui · Foundation Establishment Mid (Tier 6) · bitter, gatekeeper of the Pill Division*

That's enough for 80% of NPCs. Only add more when they'll roll dice.

#### 13.5.2 Functional NPC (might roll dice)

```
Name · Realm (Tier)
Attributes: Body X / Qi X / Soul X
Key derived: Wound Cap · Qi Pool · Sense Range
Techniques: 2–4 named, with tier and mastery
Dao: 1–2 at known levels
Face Type & Value: in relevant contexts
Notable item or treasure: one line
```

Example:
```
Zhao Fei · Qi Condensation Peak (Tier 4)
Attributes: Body 2 / Qi 3 / Soul 1
Wound Cap 6 · Qi Pool 55 · Sense Range 50m
Techniques: Flame Palm (Spirit, Proficient), Heart-Piercing Palm
  (Mysterious, Familiar), Flame Shield (Spirit, Familiar)
Dao: Fire (Novice, 6 Insight)
Face: 3 Righteous (Heng Yue Sect), 1 (local towns)
Notable: Iron Flame Sword (Mysterious, Blood-Bound)
```

Takes ~2 minutes to write. Good for an important rival, a sect elder, a dueling opponent.

#### 13.5.3 Major NPC (recurring, setting-level)

Add to the Functional template:
- **Personality core**: one-line motivation + one defining flaw
- **Karma Ledger summary**: 1–3 active Karmic threads
- **Ties to PCs**: who they know among the party and how
- **Countermeasures prepared**: the items/abilities they'll deploy before dying
- **Signature moment**: a "big scene" move they save for narrative peak

This is 5–10 minutes of prep per NPC. Reserve it for characters who will matter across multiple sessions.

### 13.6 Encounter Design

#### 13.6.1 Calibrating Difficulty

Use the PCs' **Realm Tier** as the unit.

**For each full realm gap:**
- Party up against: laughable → standard → tough → unwinnable-without-tools
- 1 realm *below* party: speed bump; defeat automatic unless outnumbered heavily
- Same realm as party: even fight; tactical choices matter
- 1 realm *above* party: tough; need technique/Dao advantage or prep
- 2 realms above: unwinnable directly; must flee, negotiate, or tool-cheese

**Party-size rule of thumb:** a Party of 4 same-realm PCs is roughly equivalent to a single opponent one realm above them. A Party of 4 can typically handle ~2–3 same-realm foes without it being routine.

#### 13.6.2 Encounter Types

Not every encounter is a fight. Variety keeps the system alive.

| Type | What It Tests |
|---|---|
| **Duel** | Tactical technique use, Dao mastery, resource management |
| **Brawl** | Mass combat, crowd control, formation use |
| **Ambush** | Preparation, Spiritual Sense, countermeasures |
| **Chase** | Body + Qi, terrain navigation, movement techniques |
| **Infiltration** | Stealth, Deception, Face manipulation |
| **Social confrontation** | Face, Etiquette, Karma leverage, oath-swearing |
| **Tribulation** | Every resource at once — the system's big set-piece |
| **Puzzle location** | Lore, Formation-reading, collaborative deduction |
| **Resource contest** | Auction, negotiation, sect politics |

Aim for a variety mix across sessions. A campaign of pure combat burns out the system's combat-rarity assumption.

#### 13.6.3 Loot & Reward Pacing

**Per session, roughly:**
- 1 meaningful material find (herbs, ingredients, spirit stones) — enough to matter, not to break progression
- 1 treasure per party per 3–5 sessions (must be distributed; loot arguments are real)
- 1 technique scroll or manual per 4–6 sessions (reward for exploration or challenge)

**Avoid:** flooding the party with spirit stones (devalues the economy), giving Celestial-tier treasures to Foundation Establishment PCs (they can't use them yet, which frustrates), gifting life-saving talismans freely (the scarcity of these is what makes lethality interesting).

**Prefer:** treasures the party must grow into, inheritances with conditions, pills that enable specific breakthroughs at planned moments.

### 13.7 Tribulation Design Template

Tribulations are the system's big set-piece scenes. Build them intentionally.

#### Step 1: Pick Base Structure

| Breakthrough Into | Base Waves |
|---|---|
| Core Formation | 3 |
| Nascent Soul | 5 |
| Soul Formation | 6 |
| Soul Transformation | 7 |
| Ascendant | Celestial Flame Trial (see 8.3.2) |
| Nirvana Scryer | 7 |
| Nirvana Cleanser | 8 |
| Nirvana Shatterer | 9 |

#### Step 2: Apply Karma Modifier

- Net White Karma +10: −1 wave
- Net Black Karma −10: +1 wave, heart demons stronger
- Net Black Karma −20: +2 waves, heart demons draw on specific past sins by name

#### Step 3: Vary Wave Types

Mix across the tribulation. Each wave is a distinct threat — not 7 rounds of "lightning again."

| Wave Type | Response Required | Scales With |
|---|---|---|
| **Heavenly Lightning** | Body or Qi defense | Realm Tier |
| **Karmic Fire** | Burns based on Black Karma | Karma debt |
| **Heart Demon** | Soul defense roll; faces inner demons drawn from Ties/regrets | Soul + Realm |
| **Dao Trial** | Specific to Dao being pursued; tests comprehension | Dao level |
| **Truth Mirror** | Shows the cultivator a vision they must accept/reject | Character choice, not roll |
| **Elemental Purge** | Damage + effect keyed to Spiritual Root's weakest element | Root grade |
| **Memory Erasure Attempt** | Tribulation tries to wipe a significant Tie | Roll to preserve |

**Design principle:** tribulation should feel personal. A generic lightning-only tribulation is boring. A tribulation that calls out the PC's broken Heart Oath in the heart-demon wave, burns them with Karmic Fire for their demonic cultivation, and tests their specific Dao comprehension — *that's* a scene worth a session climax.

#### Step 4: Wave Target Numbers

Scale with realm:

| Breakthrough Into | Typical Wave Target (successes to pass) |
|---|---|
| Core Formation | 5 |
| Nascent Soul | 7 |
| Soul Formation | 9 |
| Soul Transformation | 11 |
| Ascendant | 15 (plus special trial mechanics) |
| Nirvana realms | 12 → 15 → 18 (per realm) |

Failing a wave causes damage proportional to the wave's tier; failing the final wave is typically fatal without a countermeasure.

#### Step 5: Allow Countermeasures & Intervention

Before tribulation begins, the PC can:
- Use a Tribulation Diversion Array (Celestial, section 10.4.3) to reduce waves
- Consume a Tribulation Pill (Celestial, section 10.3.2) for dice bonuses
- Have a higher-realm ally prepared to intervene (they draw the Heavens' attention too)
- Position themselves strategically (terrain modifies specific wave types)

**The fair GM rule:** if the PC prepared well, they live. If they didn't, they die. Tribulation is the purest expression of the system's preparation-over-power ethos.

### 13.8 Handling Player Invention

Cultivation fiction is full of clever cheese — Wang Lin in particular survives because he improvises. A TTRPG needs to reward this, not shut it down.

#### The Three-Question Ruling

When a player proposes something clever, ask:

1. **Is it mechanically plausible?** — Does the system as written support it? If yes, it's fine.
2. **If not quite, what existing mechanic is it closest to?** — Use that as the template; adapt target numbers or resources.
3. **What's it cost?** — Qi, Insight, a resource, a risk? No free actions; everything trades.

#### Common Improvisations and Rulings

| Player Says | Ruling |
|---|---|
| "I use my Sword Dao Insight to cut the formation barrier" | Opposed roll: Dao level vs. formation target; costs 1 banked Insight to try |
| "I offer my Blood Debt to an NPC who hates the enemy, to turn them into an ally" | Karmic negotiation; opposed social roll; only works if NPC's Karma-alignment permits |
| "I imbue my technique with lightning from the storm overhead" | One-time modifier; +2 dice if terrain supports; risk of attracting tribulation if overused |
| "I force the enemy's soul-bound artifact to reject them" | Difficult Soul roll + Dao Artifact (Initiate+); if it works, treasure becomes Blood-Bound (weaker) during the scene |
| "I use my Resentment pool to tear through the Realm Barrier" | Already a rule (section 4); 1 Resentment cancels 1 Barrier success |
| "I threaten the NPC using their pursuer Karma" | Intimidation roll + Karmic Lore bonus; leverage = narrative advantage |

When unsure: **say yes, set a cost, move on.** The worst outcome is a slow-down because you're adjudicating.

#### What NOT to Reward

- **Retroactive preparation.** "I actually have a talisman for this" is the one thing the countermeasure system explicitly forbids. Stay firm.
- **Infinite resource chains.** If a clever play would grant unlimited qi or Insight, cap it and move on.
- **Table metagaming as "cleverness."** A player invoking Rule 13.8.2 by name to get a free outcome isn't clever play; it's rules-lawyering.

### 13.9 Running Seclusion Between Sessions

Seclusion can eat a lot of prep time if you're not careful. Here's how to run it efficiently.

#### 13.9.1 Collect Actions Before Prep

- End each session by asking: "How many seclusion slots, and what are you doing?"
- Or collect via chat/email in the day(s) after the session
- Have a standard form: **Slot 1: X. Slot 2: Y. Slot 3: Z.**

#### 13.9.2 Resolve in Bulk

During your prep time, resolve everyone's seclusion together:

- **Milestone advancement** — automatic if conditions met; note on sheet
- **Cultivation** — check for sub-stage advancement (usually 1–2 slots per sub-stage)
- **Crafting** — roll the craft pool, determine quality
- **Insight gain** — meditation gives 1 Insight per slot (or 2 for Seed Dao)
- **Breakthrough attempts** — resolve the full Breakthrough Scene (might warrant a short async scene with the player rather than bulk resolution)

#### 13.9.3 Big Events Get Scenes

Don't resolve a Heavenly Tribulation offscreen. If a PC is breaking through to Core Formation and triggering tribulation, that's a session opener or a climax — not a prep-time result.

Similarly, if a Blood Debt pursuer arrives during seclusion, play the encounter at the next session's start.

#### 13.9.4 Result Handoff

Prep the session's opening by describing the seclusion results narratively:

> "Five months have passed. Wang Lin emerges from his cave pale and thinner, but his qi moves cleaner — he's hit Qi Condensation Mid. Bai Yun has three new Mysterious-grade talismans tucked in her sleeve. Li Hao's attempted Foundation breakthrough failed — he's bedridden with minor qi deviation and will need a week of recovery."

This is both narratively satisfying and mechanically compact.

### 13.10 Running a Tribulation Scene

A checklist for when tribulation finally hits.

**Before the first wave:**
- Announce the breakthrough is starting
- Let the PC and party declare preparations (talismans, formations, ally intervention)
- Resolve preparations (note their effects)
- Set the initial wave count (base + karma modifier)

**Per wave:**
1. Describe the wave's nature ("The heavens split — black lightning threaded with karmic fire descends")
2. State what the response requires (Body/Qi/Soul defense, specific resistance)
3. Player rolls; apply modifiers from preparations
4. Outcome:
   - **Meet target:** wave resisted; take minimal or no damage; grant "residue" benefit
   - **Miss by a little:** wave partly resisted; take damage equal to miss gap × tier factor
   - **Fail badly:** wave crushes; major damage, potentially trigger countermeasure
5. Describe the aftermath; set up the next wave's flavor

**After the last wave:**
- Describe the breakthrough completing (if survived)
- Apply realm advancement mechanically on the sheet
- Apply tribulation residue (bonus dice, longevity, soul cultivation, etc.)
- Note any Face gains from witnesses or losses from being witnessed failing
- Roleplay the post-tribulation clarity — PCs often have immediate Dao insights here

### 13.11 Pacing Realm Advancement

A common GM mistake is letting PCs breakthrough too fast or holding them back too long. Use this pacing as a guideline:

| Sessions Per Realm (at active campaign pace) | Notes |
|---|---|
| Mortal → Qi Condensation | Session 1 |
| Qi Condensation Early → Peak | 4–8 sessions (one per sub-stage) |
| Foundation Establishment | 6–10 sessions across the realm |
| Core Formation | 10–15 sessions (includes first tribulation) |
| Nascent Soul | 15–25 sessions |
| Soul Formation and above | 20+ sessions each |

This means a campaign from Mortal to Nascent Soul is roughly 40–60 sessions — a substantial multi-year commitment. A campaign capping at Foundation Establishment or Core Formation is more typical for tables that want to complete an arc.

**When to accelerate:** a player whose character is stuck in the same realm for 10+ sessions despite trying, probably needs a resource gift or a story-level opportunity (found the missing part of their incomplete method, a senior master gives them a breakthrough pill, etc.)

**When to slow down:** a player speed-running realms with no Insight, no Karma accumulation, no relationships — they're min-maxing and missing the genre. Introduce consequences (tribulation harsher due to shallow cultivation, sect elders suspicious of fast advancement, etc.).

### 13.12 GM's Shortlist of House-Rule Decisions

Decisions to make explicitly at session zero or session one:

- **Insight Gambit in sparring**: yes, but no Brink-of-Death triggers
- **Resentment refill rate**: once per session maximum by default
- **Multi-Dao comprehension**: allowed, but Seed's 2× rate only on one
- **Karma accumulation for party actions**: is it shared, or individual? (Recommend: individual; players who didn't cause the kill don't get the Karma)
- **Tribulation spectators**: does witnessing another's tribulation count as a Moment of Truth? (Recommend: yes, once per realm level)
- **Player character death**: do you roll up a disciple of the same sect, a fated reincarnation, or start fresh? Affects how attached players get
- **Can PCs start sect branches?** (Relevant for long campaigns; recommend: yes, at Nascent Soul+, if the table wants it)

Lock these in early; shift only with table consensus.

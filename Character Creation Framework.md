# Character Creation Framework
### Player's Guide — Cultivation TTRPG (Renegade Immortal / 仙逆 Setting)

**Purpose.** This is the player-facing companion to [[cultivation_ttrpg_framework]]. It walks you, step by step, from a blank page to a session-ready character. Every table you need lives here. Every decision is explicit. Every archetype has a recommended build path.

**Read order.** Skim the [[#1. The Eleven Steps at a Glance|roadmap]] first. Then either follow the [[#3. Step-by-Step Walkthrough|step-by-step walkthrough]] cold, or pick an [[#4. Archetype Build Paths|archetype build path]] and let it guide your choices. New players should pick a build path. Veterans can freelance.

**Design ethos reminder.** Talent is a cruel lottery. Preparation beats power. The Heavens are hostile. There is no "wrong" character — but there are characters who get killed in session three because they didn't think about countermeasures. Make your character with that in mind.

## 1. The Eleven Steps at a Glance

The full process. Steps marked **★** are decision-heavy. Steps marked **⚠** involve dice you cannot reroll.

| # | Step | What You Decide | Time |
|---|---|---|---|
| 0 | **Session Zero** | Frame, lethality consent, group concept | Group |
| 1 | **Origin & Background** ★ | Who you were before cultivation | 5 min |
| 2 | **Attributes** ★ | Body / Qi / Soul (5 points, max 3) | 5 min |
| 3 | **Spiritual Roots** ⚠ | Roll 2d10 publicly. The dice decide. | 1 min |
| 4 | **Dao Heart** ★ | Your inner conviction; defines aligned Daos & Heart Demon resistance | 5 min |
| 5 | **Cultivation Method** ★ | The framework that governs your qi | 5 min |
| 6 | **Starting Techniques** ★ | 2 Spirit-tier techniques | 10 min |
| 7 | **Kit, Wealth, Countermeasures** | Gear, spirit stones, life-saving items | 5 min |
| 8 | **Affiliation** ★ | Sect / clan / rogue / mortal | 5 min |
| 9 | **Lores & Practices** | 3 Lores + 3 Practices | 5 min |
| 10 | **Karma, Ties, Heart Oaths** ★ | Three named people; optional binding oath | 10 min |
| 11 | **Name, Age, Appearance** | Identity and presentation | 5 min |

**Total time:** ~60 minutes for a deliberate build, faster with an archetype template.

**Key constraint:** Steps 1, 4, 5, 6, 8, and 10 chain together. Your Origin nudges Affiliation and Karma. Your Dao Heart shapes which techniques resonate (via its Aligned Daos). Your Cultivation Method gates which techniques you can learn cleanly. **Build coherently — a Body Cultivator with a Demonic Method, a Sword Heart, and Stealth Practices is a mess.**

## 2. Pre-Creation — What to Bring to Session Zero

Before you touch a character sheet, the table needs to align. This is **Step 0**, and skipping it produces session-one disasters.

### 2.1 Questions for the Group

| Question | Why It Matters |
|---|---|
| **Campaign Frame?** (Sect Disciple / Rogue / Clan Heir / Mortal World / Sect War / Exploration) | Determines Step 8 options |
| **Starting Realm?** (Mortal → QC, or higher) | Affects derived stats and starting kit |
| **Random Roots confirmed?** | If anyone wants to opt out, do it now — not after a Mortal Root roll |
| **Lethality on?** (Default: yes) | Confirm everyone wants the brutal version |
| **Group concept?** | Same sect / rivals / sworn siblings / rogue band / family |
| **Content lines?** | Demonic path, slavery-binding, sacrifice, cruelty — what's in/out? |

### 2.2 Questions for Yourself

Before picking anything mechanical, decide your character's **emotional core.** The system rewards *defiance* — characters who want something the world won't easily give.

- **What does my character want that they cannot currently have?** (Power, vengeance, knowledge, freedom, a person back, transcendence)
- **What are they willing to do for it?** (Cross what lines? Sacrifice what?)
- **What is their relationship to Heaven's order?** (Submit to it, exploit it, defy it, ignore it)
- **What's their breaking point?** (When do they go demonic? When do they walk away?)

You don't need answers to all of these — but at least one of them should be sharp before Step 1.

### 2.3 Decision Pre-Roll: Are You Open to Mortal Root?

The 2d10 roll in Step 3 is binding. Before you roll:

- **Are you OK playing a Mortal Root / Wang Lin character?** (Slow grind, Resentment-fueled, possibly capped at Foundation Establishment without intervention)
- **Are you OK playing a Heavenly Body?** (World-changing rare gift; everyone wants you dead or pregnant)
- **If you really want a specific archetype**, talk to the GM about *narrative* alternatives (a Heavenly Root character whose root is *cracked*, a Body Cultivator whose roots don't matter, etc.) before Step 3 — not after.

Once you roll, you live with the result.

## 3. Step-by-Step Walkthrough

### Step 1 — Origin & Background ★

Every cultivator was someone before they were a cultivator. Pick one.

#### 3.1.1 The Origin Table

| Origin | Attribute Bonus | Lores | Practices | Wealth | Starting Face | Family Ties | Hook |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Mortal Village Youth** | +1 Body | Mortal, Herb | Survival, Athletics | Impoverished | Face 1 home village | Living parents (Life Debt) | Filial obligation |
| **Fallen Noble** | +1 Soul | Sect Politics, Mortal | Etiquette, Insight | Modest (debts) | Face 2 noble / 0 elsewhere | Scattered kin, ruined name | House to restore |
| **Merchant's Child** | +1 to any | Mortal, Artifact Appraisal | Negotiation, Deception | Modest | Face 1 across 2–3 trade cities | Living trading family | A specific debt |
| **Mountain Hunter / Herbalist** | +1 Body | Herb, Beast | Tracking, Survival | Modest (self-reliant) | Face 0 (rural) | Isolated family or sole survivor | A wilderness only you know |
| **Servant of a Sect** | +1 Soul | Sect Politics, Cultivation | Stealth, Etiquette | Impoverished | Face 1 in home sect | A former master | Unfinished sect business |
| **Orphan / Wanderer** | +1 to any | Mortal | Stealth, Survival | Destitute | Face 0 | **No family. No net.** | Your life starts now |
| **Battlefield Survivor** | +1 Body | Cultivation | Intimidation, Tracking | Modest (loot) | Face 1 among veterans | Scattered comrades | **Bloodstained karma** |
| **Young Master / Clan Heir** | +1 Soul | Sect Politics, Cultivation | Etiquette, Insight | Wealthy | Face 3 clan / 1 allied | Extensive clan; rivals | Inherited duties + enemies |

#### 3.1.2 GM-Approved Variants

- **Temple Initiate** — +1 Soul, religious context, spiritual calling
- **Criminal / Outlaw** — +1 to any, Face **−1** (infamous), 1 active Blood Debt
- **Disgraced Disciple** — +1 to any, Face 0 in former sect (negative reputation), 1 unresolved Heart Oath

Reflavor any base origin — talk to your GM.

#### 3.1.3 What Your Origin Locks In

- **One starting attribute bonus** (applied at Step 2)
- **2 Lores** (knowledge — see Step 9)
- **2 Practices** at Trained / +1 die (skills — see Step 9)
- **Starting Wealth tier** (see Step 7)
- **Initial Face** in your origin context
- **One concrete Tie** (often a Life Debt to family — see Step 10)

> **Pitfall:** Origin grants are *not* optional. You do not get to swap "Etiquette" out for "Tracking" because it doesn't fit your concept. If the package doesn't fit, pick a different origin.

### Step 2 — Attributes ★

You have **3 attributes**: Body, Qi, Soul. Distribute **5 points** across them, **max 3** in any single attribute. Then add your Origin bonus (+1 to one attribute, applied after the 5-point distribution — this is how you can reach starting score 4 with a Mortal Village Youth pumping Body).

#### 3.2.1 What Each Attribute Does

| Attribute | Governs | If Low (1) |
|---|---|---|
| **Body** (体) | Wound capacity, physical strikes, durability, body-cultivation techniques | You break easily. Physical crises kill you. |
| **Qi** (气) | Qi pool size, technique fuel, flight, qi-projected attacks | Most techniques cost 1+ qi. You'll run dry fast. |
| **Soul** (魂) | Spiritual sense range, soul defense, crafting (alchemy/formations/artifacts), Insight intake | You can't see threats coming. You can't craft. |

#### 3.2.2 The Score Scale

| Score | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **1** | Weak / neglected — a liability. Entire subsystems are gated off. |
| **2** | Typical disciple level |
| **3** | Strong focus (starting maximum) |
| **4** | Exceptional — only via Origin bonus at start, or play advancement |
| **5** | Peak mortal cap. The hard ceiling. |

#### 3.2.3 Suggested Distributions

Pick before applying Origin bonus.

| Archetype | Body | Qi | Soul | Plays Like |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Body Cultivator** | 3 | 1 | 1 | A walking fortress; devastating physical strikes, hard to kill |
| **Classic Cultivator** | 1 | 3 | 1 | Novel default — techniques, flight, elemental attacks |
| **Scholar / Crafter** | 1 | 1 | 3 | Alchemist/formation master; weak in a fight, invaluable in seclusion |
| **Sword Cultivator** | 2 | 2 | 1 | Blade and qi; the duelist |
| **Balanced Disciple** | 2 | 2 | 1 | Well-rounded; no weakness, no standout |
| **Soul Cultivator** | 1 | 2 | 2 | Subtle; soul attacks, formations, perception play |

> **Pitfall:** A **1** in any attribute is a real handicap, not a flavor choice. A Body 1 character takes 2 wounds and starts limping. A Soul 1 character can't see other cultivators coming until they're in stabbing range. Choose with eyes open.

#### 3.2.4 Worked Distribution

You're a Mortal Village Youth (+1 Body) building a Body Cultivator:
- Allocate 5 points: Body 3 / Qi 1 / Soul 1
- Apply Origin: Body **4** / Qi 1 / Soul 1
- **You start at the cap for Body** at character creation. This is the only way to do it.

You're a Fallen Noble (+1 Soul) building a Scholar:
- Allocate 5 points: Body 1 / Qi 1 / Soul 3
- Apply Origin: Body 1 / Qi 1 / Soul **4**
- Same trick — Origin bonus unlocks the 4.

#### 3.2.5 Starting Realm

You begin at **Mortal (Tier 0)**. You are *not* a cultivator yet. You become one by learning your Cultivation Method (Step 5) and progressing through session-one play, where you typically reach **Qi Condensation Early (Tier 1)**.

### Step 3 — Spiritual Roots ⚠

**Roll 2d10. Publicly. At the table. No rerolls. The dice decide.**

In-fiction, this is a public ceremony — a sect testing platform, a merchant's appraisal stone, a hermit's pulse-reading. Witnesses see your result. Shame and pride are part of the drama.

#### 3.3.1 The Roots Table

| 2d10 | Root Grade | Speed | Elements |
|---|---|---|---|
| **2** | **Heavenly Spiritual Root** | ×3 | **Single pure element** (you pick from Five Elements) |
| **3–4** | **Heavenly Dao Root** | ×2.5 | **Dual harmonious elements** (you pick a harmonious pair) |
| **5–7** | **Superior Root** | ×2 | **Triple element** (you pick three) |
| **8–12** | **Common Root** | ×1 | **Four or five elements mixed** (you pick) |
| **13–15** | **Mortal Root (Waste Root)** | ×0.5 | All five impure. Cannot pass Foundation Establishment without intervention. |
| **16–17** | **Broken Root** | — | Damaged. Cannot cultivate normally. |
| **18–19** | **Mutant Root** | Variable | Roll 1d6 on Mutant table. The mutation picked you. |
| **20** | **Heavenly Body** | N/A | Roll on Heavenly Body table — see [[cultivation_ttrpg_framework#5. The Heavenly Body Table\|main framework §5]] |

#### 3.3.2 The Five Elements

| Element | 五行 | Feel |
|---|---|---|
| **Metal** (金) | Sharp / blade / refinement | Weapons, armor, purification |
| **Wood** (木) | Growth / life | Healing, poison, vitality |
| **Water** (水) | Flow / ice-adjacent | Adaptation, soul-calming |
| **Fire** (火) | Flame / destruction | Damage, forging, explosion |
| **Earth** (土) | Stone / stability | Defense, formation-adjacent |

**Generative cycle (harmonious):** Wood → Fire → Earth → Metal → Water → Wood. Adjacent on this loop = harmonious.

**Destructive cycle (clashing):** Wood pierces Earth · Earth absorbs Water · Water quenches Fire · Fire melts Metal · Metal cuts Wood.

**Element Rules at Creation:**

- **Heavenly Dao Root (dual):** must pick a **harmonious** pair from the generative cycle. No Wood+Metal. No Water+Fire.
- **Superior Root (triple):** any three. Three harmonious in sequence is strongest.
- **Common Root (four or five):** internal conflict is built-in — that's why speed is ×1.
- **Mortal Root:** all five present but impure. No bonus, no penalty from element matching.

#### 3.3.3 Mutant Root Subtable (1d6 on rolls of 18–19)

| d6 | Mutant Root | Behavior |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Ice Root** | Water variant; ×1 speed; ice +2 dice, fire −2 dice |
| 2 | **Thunder Root** | Lightning (Metal+Fire fusion); ×1.25 speed; tribulations +1 wave |
| 3 | **Wood-Metal Hybrid** | Paradox; ×0.75 speed; produces "Blood Silver" qi (armor-piercing) |
| 4 | **Yin Spirit Root** | Darkness/ghost; ×1 Soul-path, ×0.5 Body-path; orthodox sects distrust |
| 5 | **Yang Spirit Root** | Light/life; ×1 Life/Medicine; cannot conceal aura |
| 6 | **Unnamed / Unique** | GM invents — campaign-specific, one of a kind |

#### 3.3.4 Element Bonus Mechanic

Once you have your elements, they affect every technique with an elemental keying:

- Technique element matches one of yours → **+1 die**
- Technique element opposes one of yours (destructive cycle) → **−1 die**
- Mortal Root: no bonus, no penalty (you cancel out)
- Mutant Root: ×1 in affinity element, ×0.5 in opposing

#### 3.3.5 Heavenly Resentment Pool (Wang Lin Mechanic)

Characters with **Mortal Root or Broken Root** start with a **Heavenly Resentment pool of 3.** Mutant Root characters start with **1.**

**Voluntary downgrade:** at character creation, you may declare a worse root than you rolled, **+2 Resentment per grade downgraded** (one-time, permanent).
- Common Root rolling player → Mortal Root by choice → starts with **5** Resentment.
- This is the explicit "I want to play Wang Lin" button.

**Spending:**

| Cost | Effect |
|---|---|
| 1 | Reroll a die after seeing the result |
| 1 | Cancel 1 success of a Realm Barrier (1/scene) |
| 2 | +2 dice to a breakthrough roll |
| 2 | Survive a killing blow once (drains pool, cannot refill for 3 sessions) |
| 3 | Unlock a hidden inheritance, dormant bloodline, or plot-level discovery |

**Refilling:**
- +1 when humiliated/defeated by a higher-realm cultivator and you vow to surpass them
- +1 when losing a major Tie (death, betrayal)
- +1 at each session where you suffer for your weakness and refuse to quit

Caps at (starting + 3). Cannot be grinded.

> **The Wang Lin trade-off:** ×0.5 cultivation speed and a Foundation Establishment hard cap — *unless* you push past via Insight, Resentment, or plot. In exchange: a mechanically distinct narrative engine and a resource other characters don't have.

### Step 4 — Dao Heart (道心) ★

The framework distinguishes three related but distinct concepts. Untangle them before you choose.

| Concept | What it is | When set |
|---|---|---|
| **Dao Heart** (道心) | Your inner conviction — the philosophical anchor of your cultivation. *Who you are.* | **Character creation** — Step 4 |
| **Dao** (道) | A path of comprehension you study — Sword, Karma, Slaughter, Space, etc. *What you study.* | Through play (see [[#8.3 Where to Go for More\|main framework §7.6]]) |
| **Domain** (领域) | The manifestation formed at Soul Formation by fusing your most-comprehended Dao with your Nascent Soul. *What you become.* | Soul Formation breakthrough (mid-late campaign) |

This step covers only your **Dao Heart.** Dao comprehension accumulates through play. Domains form much later at Soul Formation. Don't conflate them.

#### 3.4.1 What a Dao Heart Is

Your Dao Heart is the deepest truth your character believes about existence. It is **not a power** — it's a stance. When pressure hits, when temptation arrives, when loved ones die, when the Heavens themselves test you, *your Dao Heart is what holds.*

Mechanically, your Heart does three things:

1. **Sets your Heart Demon Resistance baseline.** When tribulation throws Heart Demons at you, you roll **Soul Defense + Dao Heart modifier** vs the demon's target successes. A weak Dao Heart fails; a strong one carries you through.
2. **Determines your Aligned Daos.** Each Dao Heart has natural Dao gravities. Insight in those Daos accumulates at **2× rate** (this replaces the old "Dao Seed" mechanic — your Heart determines the gravity, not a single chosen Dao).
3. **Defines the moments that test you.** The GM uses your Dao Heart to design personal trials, heart demon manifestations, and Karma resolutions. A Ruthless Heart's tribulation looks nothing like a Benevolent one's.

#### 3.4.2 The Seven Canonical Dao Hearts

| Dao Heart | Conviction | Aligned Daos (2× Insight) | Tribulation Stance |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Ruthless** (无情) | "Emotion is weakness. Cut what hinders." | Slaughter, Demonic, Sword, Poison | +2 dice vs Heart Demons; **−2 dice on Karma-cleansing rolls** |
| **Sword** (剑心) | "I am the blade. The blade is truth." | Sword, Metal, Wind, Lightning | +2 dice vs Heart Demons in single combat; standard otherwise |
| **Seeking** (求道) | "I must know. Truth is the only treasure." | Karma, Cause & Effect, Space, Time, all Craft Daos | +1 die vs Heart Demons; **+1 die to all Insight rolls** |
| **Free Path** (逍遥) | "I refuse to be bound." | Wind, Space, Wood (growth), all Craft Daos | +2 dice vs Heart Demons related to obligation; vulnerable to demons of meaning |
| **Benevolence** (仁心) | "I cultivate for others, not myself." | Life, Medicine, Wood, Cause & Effect | +1 die vs Heart Demons; **−2 dice on Slaughter/Demonic Insight** |
| **Slaughter** (杀道) | "Strength alone justifies. The strong rise on corpses." | Slaughter, Demonic, Fire, Metal | +3 dice vs Heart Demons; **double Black Karma per kill, halved Karma cleansing** |
| **Defiance** (逆道) | "I defy Heaven itself. My path is my own." *(Wang Lin's Heart)* | Karma, Resentment, Solitude, any "personal" Dao | +1 die vs Heart Demons; **Resentment pool refills 1 extra/session**; Heavenly Tribulation +1 wave |

#### 3.4.3 Dao Heart in Action

**Heart Demon Resistance.** When tribulation, mind attacks, or breakthrough trials throw heart demons at you, roll **Soul Defense + Dao Heart modifier** vs the demon's target successes. Failing means the heart demon takes hold for the duration of the scene — compulsion, paralysis, dice loss from doubt, etc.

**Dao Alignment.** Insight you earn in any Dao listed under your Heart's "Aligned Daos" comes at **2× rate.** This is your long-term comprehension engine. A Sword Heart cultivator pursuing Sword Dao gains Insight twice as fast as one pursuing Earth Dao. *Pursue your aligned Daos for fast progress; pursue off-alignment Daos when story demands.*

**Karma Interactions.**
- **Ruthless** can resist remorse but generates +1 Black Karma per significant betrayal
- **Slaughter** doubles Black Karma per kill (canonical — the cost of the path)
- **Defiance** generates +1 Black Karma per major Heavenly transgression (which a defiant cultivator does often) but can spend Resentment to reduce karmic backlash

**Personal Trials.** The GM uses your Heart to design your tribulation:
- **Ruthless** → heart demons of *attachment* — visions of the loved ones they discarded
- **Sword** → demons of *doubt* — moments where their blade failed
- **Benevolence** → demons of *futility* — those they tried to save and couldn't
- **Defiance** → the Heavenly Will *itself* manifesting to demand submission

#### 3.4.4 Choosing Your Dao Heart

Pick one at character creation. Your Heart should be **deeply tied to your Origin and starting Ties.** Match the conviction to the lived backstory.

| If Your Backstory Says… | Recommended Hearts |
|---|---|
| "Power through pain and loss; the world denied me what I deserved" | **Defiance**, **Ruthless**, **Seeking** |
| "I will cut down anything in my way; emotion is weakness" | **Ruthless**, **Sword**, **Slaughter** |
| "The blade is the path. Everything else distracts." | **Sword** |
| "I will outlast everything; nothing breaks me" | **Defiance**, **Seeking** |
| "I cultivate to save people, not for myself" | **Benevolence** |
| "I must know. Truth is more precious than power." | **Seeking** |
| "I refuse to be bound — by sect, family, or fate" | **Free Path**, **Defiance** |
| "Strength alone justifies; corpses pave my path" | **Slaughter**, **Ruthless** |

> **Wang Lin reference.** Wang Lin's canonical Heart is **Defiance** — the "I refuse Heaven's denial" stance that gives the novel its title. His Heart is set at creation and persists across thousands of chapters. What changes is the *Daos* he pursues (Karma, Slaughter, Life-Death, etc.) and eventually the Domains he forms — not his Heart.

#### 3.4.5 Hybrid Hearts (Advanced — GM Permission Only)

Some cultivators canonically combine two Hearts — a Sword-Ruthless duelist, a Seeking-Defiance scholar-rebel. These are **harder to maintain** (the dual conviction creates internal tension) but offer broader Dao alignment.

A hybrid Heart:
- Combines aligned Daos of both, but only the **shared** Daos benefit from 2× Insight (the *union*, not the sum)
- Heart Demon resistance is the **average** of both contributing Hearts
- Tribulation is harsher: +1 wave, with one wave specifically testing the *tension between* the two Hearts

Hybrid Hearts are **GM-permission only.** Best reserved for experienced players who understand the framework.

#### 3.4.6 Can a Dao Heart Change?

In theory yes, in practice rarely. A **Heart Transition** is canonical and possible — but campaign-defining trauma, not casual reframe. To change your Heart:

- A profound, irreversible event (death of a Tie, betrayal by your sect, witnessing the failure of your old Heart)
- A full Seclusion Slot dedicated to the transition
- A Heart Demon trial during the transition — failing means temporary demons, not Heart change
- One sub-stage of cultivation lost (the foundation reshuffles)

Most cultivators die with the Heart they started with. Wang Lin's Defiance never wavers — it deepens, but doesn't break. Pick a Heart you can live with for the campaign.

#### 3.4.7 Starting Insight

You begin with **0 Insight in any Dao.** You gain Insight through play — combat Moments of Truth, the Insight Gambit, seclusion meditation, key narrative experiences.

If you take a Starting Heart Oath in Step 10, you begin with **+2 Insight in one of your Heart's Aligned Daos** of your choice. That's your one creation-time Insight investment.

> **Resonance bonus.** Any technique whose effect aligns with one of your Heart's Aligned Daos gains **×1.5 Mastery Points** during play. This is the long-term reward for a coherent build — pick techniques that lean into your Heart.

#### 3.4.8 Reference: The Daos You Can Pursue

You don't pick a Dao at creation — your Heart sets the gravity, and you pursue Daos through play. This is the menu of available Daos for reference.

**Elemental** — common, combat-versatile, well-documented
- Sword · Fire · Water · Earth · Metal · Wood · Wind · Lightning

**Conceptual** — rare, difficult, devastating at Adept+
- Space · Time · Karma · Cause & Effect

**Dark** — fast progression, karmic cost
- Slaughter · Demonic · Ghost · Corpse

**Life / Death**
- Life · Death · Medicine · Poison

**Craft Daos** — slow in combat, devastating in downtime
- Formation · Artifact · Alchemy

**Personal / Unconventional** — Wang Lin–style
- Solitude · Endurance · Resentment · Perseverance

**Compatibility notes for Daos pursued together:**
- **Natural pairings:** Sword+Wind · Fire+Lightning · Metal+Earth · Medicine+Life · Slaughter+Demonic
- **Difficult but powerful:** Space+Time · Karma+Cause & Effect
- **Opposing pairs (one cancels the other):** Life vs Death · Medicine vs Poison · Slaughter vs Life · Order/Cause-Effect vs Demonic

Pursuing opposing Daos simultaneously is canonically possible but brutally hard — usually causes Qi Deviation at breakthroughs.

### Step 5 — Cultivation Method ★

Your **Cultivation Method** is the framework that governs how you refine qi. It is *distinct* from individual techniques — it's the *manual within which* all your cultivation happens.

#### 3.5.1 Method Types

| Method Type | Peak Realm Cap | Bonus | Cost |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Orthodox Sect Method** | Soul Formation (typical) | Safe; +1 die to Spirit-Gathering formations; broadly compatible | Sect-branded; loyalty expectations; not portable |
| **Demonic Method** | Variable; some reach Nirvana | +1 cultivation speed tier; faster Insight | Demonic Heart corruption (1/seclusion); +1 Black Karma per major use; Qi Deviation at corruption ≥10 |
| **Body Refinement Method** | Ascendant (typical) | +1 Body tier advancement; wounds halved | Qi growth halved; qi-techniques permanently weaker |
| **Family Heirloom / Incomplete** | Partial — missing upper realms | Unique techniques; often secret | Must find rest of method by Core Formation or stall |
| **Self-Taught / Improvised** | Limited; depends on talent | Resistance to mind attacks; +1 die on novel technique use | Slow progression; no support network |

#### 3.5.2 Method-Origin Pairings (Recommendations)

| If You Took… | Default Method |
|---|---|
| Sect Disciple Affiliation | Orthodox Sect Method |
| Clan Heir Affiliation | Family Heirloom (often complete; less common to start incomplete) |
| Rogue Cultivator | Self-Taught **or** Demonic **or** Incomplete |
| Mortal Village Youth → Sect | Orthodox |
| Mortal Village Youth → No Sect | Incomplete (Wang Lin path) |
| Battlefield Survivor | Body Refinement (often) |
| Orphan / Wanderer | Self-Taught or Incomplete |

#### 3.5.3 Sample Named Methods

- **Heng Yue Pure Heart Method** — Orthodox; Foundation Establishment peak. Wang Lin's first sect method. Unremarkable, broadly compatible.
- **Underworld Ascension Method** — Demonic; Ascendant peak. Situ Nan's gift to Wang Lin canonically. Fast but corrupting.
- **Iron Flesh Scripture** — Body Refinement; Soul Formation peak. Brutal physical grinding.
- **Nine Yang Golden Body** — Body Refinement, Yang variant; Ascendant peak. Better quality, fire-affinity required.
- **Three-Slash Sword Method** — Orthodox sword specialization; Soul Formation peak. Three named slashes.
- **Wandering Cloud Scripture** — Self-Taught; variable. A patchwork grimoire from disparate sources.

#### 3.5.4 Replacing Your Method Later

You can swap methods mid-campaign through:
- Sect promotion (Outer → Inner often grants a better method)
- Inheritance (tomb, gift, scroll)
- Writing your own (Soul Formation+ only)

Replacement requires a dedicated seclusion to reconcile — disruptive, but canonical.

> **Pitfall:** A Demonic Method on a Righteous-sect-affiliated character is a ticking bomb. A Body Method on a Qi 3 archetype halves your concept. Match method to concept and Affiliation. The GM should warn you before locking it in if there's a glaring mismatch.

### Step 6 — Starting Techniques ★

You get **2 Spirit-tier techniques.** They come from your Method, your Origin, or your starting Affiliation. Both start at **Mastery Rough** — usable in play, not yet refined.

#### 3.6.1 Starter Technique Menu

**Attack** (1 qi each, +2 dice unless noted)
- **Cloud Piercing Finger** — ranged Qi attack
- **Azure Wind Slash** — sword/blade attack
- **Rising Sun Palm** — close-range fire-tinged strike
- **Crimson Pulse Arrow** — bow/projectile
- **Bone-Shaking Shout** — soul-adjacent intimidate/stun (Soul-based)

**Defense**
- **Iron Skin (Lesser)** (1 qi/activation) — +1 die physical defense for a scene
- **Cloud Barrier** (1 qi) — temporary qi shield, absorbs 1 success
- **Sliding Step** (1 qi) — reactive dodge, +2 dice on one defense roll

**Movement**
- **Concealment Step** (1 qi) — stealth/evasion, +2 dice
- **Wind Walk** (1 qi) — short burst speed, double movement one action
- **Shadow Trace** (1 qi) — silent, leaves no tracks for a scene

**Utility**
- **Spirit Sight** (1 qi) — reveals spiritual presences/auras nearby
- **Muffle Sound** (1 qi) — silence around you for a scene
- **Minor Flame** (free) — basic fire ignition, illumination

You may also propose a custom Spirit-tier technique with GM approval. See [[Techniques Freamework]] for full anatomy and PP budgets.

#### 3.6.2 Selection Strategy

**A balanced two-pick:** one technique that *closes* (attack or movement), one that *opens space or saves your life* (defense, stealth, perception).

**Common pitfall:** taking two attacks. You die because you couldn't escape, defend, or see the threat.

**Recommended pairs by archetype:**

| Archetype | Technique 1 | Technique 2 |
|---|---|---|
| Body Cultivator | Iron Skin (Lesser) | Cloud Piercing Finger or Azure Wind Slash |
| Classic Cultivator | Cloud Piercing Finger | Cloud Barrier |
| Sword Cultivator | Azure Wind Slash | Sliding Step |
| Crafter | Spirit Sight | Concealment Step (you're not winning fights) |
| Stealth/Demonic | Concealment Step | Bone-Shaking Shout |
| Healer/Wood | Cloud Barrier | Spirit Sight or Wind Walk |

#### 3.6.3 Resonance and Element Bonuses

- **Dao-resonant** technique → ×1.5 MP gain. A technique is resonant if it aligns with one of your **Dao Heart's Aligned Daos** (Step 4). Pick at least one resonant technique.
- **Element-matching** technique (your Spiritual Root contains the element) → +1 die when cast.
- **Element-opposing** technique → −1 die.

A Fire-rooted **Sword Heart** cultivator picking Azure Wind Slash gets Dao resonance (Sword is aligned with Sword Heart) but not an element match. Picking Rising Sun Palm gets the element bonus. Picking *something Sword-themed* would get Dao resonance via Sword alignment. The system rewards coherent, build-aligned choices.

### Step 7 — Kit, Wealth, Countermeasures

Your starting material loadout. Mostly determined by Origin and Affiliation.

#### 3.7.1 Default Kit (everyone)

- **1 mortal weapon or tool** (sword, staff, dagger, brush, herb knife — fits Origin)
- **3 months travel rations** (longer for hunter origins)
- **Basic clothing** (sect robes if affiliated; plain travel garb if not)

#### 3.7.2 Spirit Stones (Starting Wealth)

Roll 1d6 low-grade. Modify by Origin:

| Wealth Tier | Origin | Roll |
|---|---|---|
| **Destitute** | Orphan, Wanderer | 1d3 low-grade |
| **Impoverished** | Village Youth, Sect Servant | 1d6 low-grade |
| **Modest** | Most origins | 2d6 low-grade |
| **Wealthy** | Young Master, prosperous Merchant | 1d6 mid-grade (= ~10 low-grade each) |

**Spirit stone equivalences:**
- 1 low-grade ≈ one month's mortal-world living expenses
- 1 mid-grade ≈ 10 low-grade
- 1 high-grade ≈ 10 mid-grade
- 1 origin-grade (Void+ treasures) ≈ 10 high-grade

#### 3.7.3 Personal Item

Every character gets **one personal item** — not mechanically powerful, *narratively significant*. A mother's ring. A dead brother's sword. A sect master's calligraphy. The letter you never delivered.

**The GM takes note. This item will come up in play.** It's an anchor for Karma, a key to hidden plots, the thing you lose at your lowest.

#### 3.7.4 Starting Countermeasures (CRITICAL)

This is **the single most important resource at low realm.** Without a countermeasure, a killing blow kills you.

| Affiliation | Starting Countermeasure |
|---|---|
| Sect-Affiliated | **1 Spirit-tier life-saving talisman** (sect-issued). Single-use. Minor teleport, soul-delay, etc. |
| Clan Heir | **1 Mysterious-tier talisman** (family heirloom). More powerful; may have one-time activation phrase |
| Rogue / Orphan | **None.** You start without a safety net. |

> **Critical lesson:** Rogue cultivators die more in session one than any other Affiliation. If you go Rogue, your Step 6 / Step 9 choices need to compensate (Concealment Step, Stealth, Survival).

#### 3.7.5 Spatial Ring

| Affiliation | Spatial Ring |
|---|---|
| Sect (Inner Disciple+) | Spirit-grade Spatial Ring (small room volume) |
| Clan Heir | Mysterious-grade Spatial Ring (large room volume) |
| Outer Disciple / Servant / Merchant Child | GM call; typically no |
| Other origins | None — pockets, pouches, saddlebags |

Without a Spatial Ring, you're limited to what you can physically carry. Treasure transport becomes a logistics problem.

### Step 8 — Affiliation ★

Your **organizational context.** This is where Step 0's Campaign Frame meets your character. Your Affiliation determines starting Face, obligations, ongoing income, and who comes looking for you when you're in trouble.

#### 3.8.1 Sect Disciple (most common)

- **Sect Name:** GM-assigned or table-agreed
- **Sect Rank:** typically 3–6 at campaign start (see [[worlds_sects_framework#Sect Ranks 1–9|Sect Ranks]])
- **Tier within Sect:** **Honorary Disciple** (entry-level, Face 1) or **Outer Disciple** (Face 2). Inner Disciple starts only in advanced-start campaigns.
- **Face Type:** Righteous (orthodox sect) or Demonic (demonic sect)
- **Obligations:** monthly sect tasks, loyalty to superiors, attendance at competitions
- **Resources:** basic Cultivation Method, Spirit-tier techniques, modest pill allowance, starter spatial ring (Inner+)
- **Lore +1:** Cultivation Lore (or another if known)
- **Practice +1:** Spiritual Sense

#### 3.8.2 Clan Heir (Allheaven-style campaigns)

- **Clan Name:** table-agreed
- **Clan Rank:** 3–5 in regional standing
- **Position:** direct-line heir, branch family, or marriage-in
- **Face:** **3 in clan, 1 in allied clans**
- **Obligations:** family duties, marriage considerations, honor obligations
- **Resources:** family Cultivation Method, **one heirloom Mysterious-tier technique**, clan treasury access, family spatial ring
- **Lore +1:** Karmic Lore *or* Artifact Appraisal (your choice)
- **Practice +1:** Negotiation

> **Clan Heir is the most resource-rich starting position.** It also has the most complicated obligations and the most pre-existing enemies.

#### 3.8.3 Rogue Cultivator

- **No formal affiliation.** May have informal ties (mentor, friend, old sect you left).
- **Face:** 0 everywhere by default
- **Obligations:** none — total freedom
- **Resources:** **whatever you scrounged.** No safety net, no pill supply, no rescue.
- **Lore +1:** free choice (whatever you learned by surviving)
- **Practice +1:** free choice

> **Rogue is mechanically the hardest start.** It is also the most narratively flexible. Wang Lin spends much of canon as a rogue. He survives by being exceptionally prepared. *You* must do the same.

#### 3.8.4 Mortal World Starter

- **You are not yet a cultivator.** Lowest-power frame.
- **No sect, no method yet.** Session one usually has you encountering the cultivation world.
- **Face:** mortal-world only (village hero, merchant reputation); Face 0 in cultivator world
- **Obligations:** mortal life — family, village, town
- **Resources:** mortal-world equivalent only; no spirit stones or techniques
- **Lore / Practice +1:** none additional (you'll learn in play)

This is the slowest-starting frame but produces the richest "becoming a cultivator" arc.

### Step 9 — Lores & Practices

Two kinds of specialties beyond combat: **Lores** (what you *know*) and **Practices** (what you *do*).

#### 3.9.1 How They Work

**Lores (binary).** You either have it or you don't.
- With it: auto-know basic facts in domain (no roll); advanced inquiry uses **Soul + Realm Tier + 2** roll
- Without it: requires teacher, book, or extensive research

**Practices (active skills, +1 die when relevant).**
- **Trained** (1 rank) — +1 die
- **Well-Practiced** (2 ranks) — +2 dice
- **Masterful** (3 ranks) — +3 dice (caps here)

All starting Practices begin at **Trained.**

#### 3.9.2 What You Get

- **2 Lores** from Origin
- **2 Practices** from Origin (Trained)
- **+1 Lore** from Affiliation
- **+1 Practice** from Affiliation (Trained)

**Total: 3 Lores + 3 Practices at start.**

#### 3.9.3 The Lore List

| Lore | Covers |
|---|---|
| **Mortal Lore** | Mortal politics, trade, languages, dialects, passing as mortal |
| **Sect Politics** | Sect hierarchies, recent events, rivalries, who's who |
| **Cultivation Lore** | Realms, famous techniques, legendary cultivators, history |
| **Herb & Pill Lore** | Spirit herbs, pill recipes, medicinal effects |
| **Artifact Appraisal** | Treasure grades, binding states, Dao resonance |
| **Beast Lore** | Spirit beasts — realms, habits, weak points, habitats |
| **Ruin & Tomb Lore** | Ancient sites, common restrictions, traps, treasure placement |
| **Karmic Lore** | Reading karmic threads, blood debts, prophetic signs (rare) |

#### 3.9.4 The Practice List

| Practice | Covers | Typical Base |
|---|---|---|
| **Spiritual Sense** | Detecting auras, scanning, reading realm | Soul |
| **Stealth** | Hiding, silent movement, masking aura | Body or Soul |
| **Deception** | Lies, disguise, forgery, misdirection | Soul |
| **Insight** | Reading intentions, detecting lies, judging character | Soul |
| **Intimidation** | Frightening mortals or weaker cultivators | Body or Face |
| **Negotiation** | Persuasion, deals, alliance-building | Soul / Face |
| **Tracking** | Following trails, wilderness navigation, residue pursuit | Body |
| **Survival** | Wilderness life, foraging, weathering, shelter | Body |
| **Etiquette** | Formal protocols, sect/clan decorum, ceremony | Soul |
| **Athletics** | Climbing, leaping, running, non-combat physicality | Body |

#### 3.9.5 What These Don't Cover

- **Combat** → Body/Qi attributes + Techniques + Realm
- **Cultivation progression** → Insight + Daos
- **Crafting** → Craft Ranks + Craft Dao (see [[cultivation_ttrpg_framework#10. Crafting Subgames|main framework §10]])

If a subsystem already handles it, use that subsystem.

### Step 10 — Karma, Ties, and Heart Oaths ★

Your starting **Karma Ledger.** This is the web of obligations, grudges, and cosmic debts your character carries into play. The full Karma system lives in [[cultivation_ttrpg_framework#9. Karma & Face|main framework §9]].

#### 3.10.1 The Three Ties (mandatory)

Write down **three named individuals.** Real names, real relationships.

1. **One person you love** — parent, sibling, lover, master, sworn friend. Their safety moves you.
2. **One person you owe** — a Life Debt. Someone who saved you, paid for you, did you a kindness.
3. **One person you hate** — *specific*, not abstract. A rival, a betrayer, a killer of someone you loved. Seed of a future Blood Debt.

These are the GM's hooks. Tie-people will appear, be threatened, die, return changed.

> **Pitfall:** Vague ties don't work. "I love my family" is not a Tie. "I love my younger sister Wang Mei, who is fourteen and still in our village" is a Tie.

#### 3.10.2 Optional: Starting Heart Oath

You may begin play with **one Heart Oath** — a cultivation-binding promise sworn on your origin soul.

**Examples:**
- "I swear on my cultivation to avenge my mother's death before I reach Core Formation."
- "I swear on my Dao to protect my younger sister's child until she is grown."
- "I swear never to take a human life unnecessarily."

**A Starting Heart Oath grants +2 Insight toward one of your Dao Heart's Aligned Daos** (your choice).

**Breaking it:**
- Immediate Qi Deviation (drop 1 sub-stage; possibly a full realm if the oath was severe)
- +3 to +5 Black Karma
- Face loss in any context where the breaking becomes known

> **The Heart Oath trade-off:** strong start (+2 Insight is significant — that's ~25% of the way to Novice) for ongoing risk. Pick an oath that's *plausibly fulfillable in play* and *meaningful to your character*. "I will never speak again" is not actually a Heart Oath; it's a gimmick.

#### 3.10.3 Starting Karma by Origin

Usually **0 White / 0 Black**, modified by Origin:

| Origin | Modification |
|---|---|
| Mortal Village Youth | Life Debt to parents (named) |
| Fallen Noble | −1 Black (ruined name soft-karma) |
| Battlefield Survivor | 1 named Blood Debt (pursuer clock inactive but can activate) |
| Young Master / Clan Heir | 1 inherited family enemy (Blood Debt from ancestor's action) |
| Criminal / Outlaw (variant) | 1 active Blood Debt + −2 Black |
| All others | 0 / 0 |

#### 3.10.4 Max Active Heart Oaths

You can hold (Realm Tier ÷ 2) + 1 active Heart Oaths. At Mortal/QC Early, that's **1.** Don't try to swear more.

### Step 11 — Name, Age, Appearance

#### 3.11.1 Names

Cultivation naming is layered:

- **Birth name** — family + personal (e.g., Wang Lin: Wang family, Lin personal)
- **Style/courtesy name** — taken at adulthood or sect entry. Often references aspirations or character. Elders and peers use it; juniors use "Senior Brother [Courtesy Name]."
- **Cultivation name / title** — earned later by deeds. "Master Demon" (Wang Lin's), "Sword Sovereign," "Flame Empress." Title unlocks via Face and reputation.

For play, pick a **birth name** now. Add a **courtesy name** if your origin grants it (Young Master typically; Wanderer typically not).

#### 3.11.2 Age

| Starting Realm | Typical Age |
|---|---|
| Mortal | 14–20 |
| Qi Condensation (recently broken through) | 16–25 |
| Foundation Establishment (experienced start) | 30–60 (cultivators age slowly) |
| Core Formation (rare PC start) | 80–200 |

Cultivators age dramatically slower after Foundation Establishment. A Core Formation cultivator in their hundreds appears thirties. At Nascent Soul, appearance becomes semi-chosen.

#### 3.11.3 Appearance

Cultivators and mortals look identical at Qi Condensation. **Foundation Establishment** brings subtle signs — clearer eyes, smoother skin, faint presence. **Core Formation** has unmistakable controllable aura. **Nascent Soul+** radiates Presence that mortals find instinctively unsettling.

Note:
- **Distinguishing marks** — scars, heterochromia, hair color (white hair = canonical tragic backstory or rare bloodline)
- **Robes / garb** — sect colors if affiliated; rank-coded styles (Heng Yue inner disciples wear red/white/black/purple by tier)
- **Carried objects** — what's visible? Sword? Brush? Beads?

## 7. Sanity Checks Before Session One

Before locking your character, walk this list. **If any answer is "no" or "unclear," fix it before play.**

### 7.1 Coherence Checks

1. **Does my Cultivation Method match my Affiliation?**
   *(Demonic Method + orthodox sect = ticking bomb. Body Method + Crafter concept = wasted slots.)*

2. **Does my Dao Heart's Aligned Daos overlap with at least one of my starting Techniques or my Method?**
   *(Sword Heart with two non-sword techniques and a fire-themed method = no resonance. ×1.5 MP gain wasted.)*

3. **Are my Spiritual Root elements compatible with at least one starting Technique?**
   *(Water-rooted character with Rising Sun Palm = −1 die forever. Pick another technique.)*

4. **Did I take at least one defensive or escape technique?**
   *(Two attacks = you die when something bites back. See §3.6.2.)*

5. **Do my Lores and Practices reflect my Origin and Affiliation?**
   *(They should be granted — this is automatic. Just verify nothing got dropped.)*

### 7.2 Survival Checks

6. **Do I have at least one countermeasure?**
   *(If Rogue and the answer is no — fix this before session one. Buy one. Steal one. Owe someone.)*

7. **Is my Wound Capacity at least 4?**
   *(Below 4 means you die in two hits at QC Early. Acceptable for Crafter who avoids combat — dangerous otherwise.)*

8. **Is my Spiritual Sense Range at least 30m?**
   *(Below 30m means you don't see threats coming. Acceptable for Body Cultivator who walks straight at them — dangerous otherwise.)*

### 7.3 Narrative Checks

9. **Are my three Ties named, specific, and present in the world?**
   *(Not "my family" — my mother, my father, my younger sister Wang Mei.)*

10. **Does my hated Tie give the GM a hook?**
    *(They should be reachable, threatenable, or actively in motion — not just "the empire" or "the Heavens.")*

11. **If I took a Heart Oath: is it fulfillable in play?**
    *(Not "I will become the strongest in the universe" — pick a milestone the GM can deliver.)*

12. **Does my character want something the world won't easily give?**
    *(If the answer is "they're content" — your engagement engine is missing. Reconsider.)*

### 7.4 Mechanical Checks

13. **Did I apply my Origin attribute bonus AFTER the 5-point distribution?**
    *(Easy mistake. Affects a starting score 4 character; check the math.)*

14. **Are my derived stats calculated for QC Early (Tier 1), not Mortal (Tier 0)?**
    *(Session one usually advances you to QC Early. Pre-calculate both rows on your sheet.)*

15. **Did I record my Heavenly Resentment pool, if applicable?**
    *(Mortal/Broken Root: 3. Mutant: 1. Voluntary downgrade: +2 per grade. Easy to miss.)*

16. **Did I record my Dao Heart's full mechanical package?**
    *(Aligned Daos for 2× Insight rate, Heart Demon Resistance modifier, and any tribulation/karma effects. The Heart isn't just a flavor label — it's a stat block. See §6.7.)*

17. **If I took a Heart Oath: is the +2 starting Insight allocated to one of my Heart's Aligned Daos?**
    *(Otherwise it doesn't get the 2× rate going forward — you're handing back half the bonus.)*

## 8. After Creation — Session One and Beyond

### 8.1 What Happens at the Table

Your sheet shows you at **Mortal (Tier 0)** with derived stats not yet calculated for QC. Session one typically opens with one of:

- A **spiritual root test** at a sect testing platform (the public 2d10 roll happens *in fiction*)
- Your **first cultivation method lesson** — you sit, you breathe, you feel qi for the first time
- A **disaster or crisis** that pushes you across the threshold into Qi Condensation under pressure
- A **mortal-world catalyst** — a beast attack, a family death, a vision

After session one, your sheet updates to **Qi Condensation Early (Tier 1)** and your derived stats lock in for that realm.

### 8.2 What to Track in Play

Beyond your sheet's static information, track:

- **Mastery Points** per technique (toward Familiar / Proficient / Mastered / Perfected)
- **Insight** per Dao (toward Novice 5 / Initiate 15 / Adept 40 / Master 100 / Grandmaster 300)
- **Heavenly Resentment** current pool (if applicable)
- **Karma Ledger** updates — new Life Debts, Blood Debts, White/Black accumulation, Heart Oath status
- **Face** in each context as it shifts
- **Wounds** filled / Qi current
- **Sub-stage progression** (Early → Mid → Late → Peak within current realm)
- **Seclusion Slots** declared between sessions
